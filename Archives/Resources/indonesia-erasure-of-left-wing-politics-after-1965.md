## Overview

Indonesia is a rare case where one entire half of the political spectrum — the **left** — was physically annihilated and then culturally erased for three decades. Understanding this trauma is essential to making sense of Indonesian politics today and why the Western left–right framework maps poorly onto it.

---

## Pre-1965: A Vibrant Left

Before 1965, Indonesia had one of the largest communist parties in the world (the PKI, *Partai Komunis Indonesia*), with an estimated 3 million members and millions more in affiliated organisations. President Sukarno's **Nasakom** alliance (*Nasionalisme, Agama, Komunisme*) formally incorporated the PKI into the ruling coalition alongside nationalists and religious groups. Left-wing thought was mainstream.[@AcademiaMappingIndonesia]

---

## 1965–1966: The Annihilation

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **30 September Movement (G30S)** | A group of junior army officers with alleged PKI links attempted a purge of the Army high command on 1 October 1965. Six generals were killed. |
| **Military counter-coup** | General Suharto crushed the movement within days and blamed the PKI, launching a nationwide purge. |
| **Mass killings** | An estimated **500,000 people** were killed over the following months[@NationalSecurityArchive1965] — suspected PKI members, left-leaning intellectuals, landless peasants, ethnic Chinese, and religious minorities. A million more were imprisoned without trial. |
| **US role** | Declassified documents show the US Embassy tracked the mass killings and provided lists of PKI figures to the Indonesian military.[@NationalSecurityArchive1965] |

---

## 1966–1998: The New Order's Ideological Purge

Under Suharto's New Order:

- **The word *kiri* ("left") was criminalised** — equated with communism, atheism, and treason. Being accused of *berhaluan kiri* (left-leaning) could destroy a career or result in imprisonment.
- **Pancasila was imposed as the sole state ideology** (*azas tunggal*) — a depoliticised, "sterilised" version stripped of its original left-nationalist content.
- **Left-wing literature was banned** — books by Marx, Lenin, Pramoedya Ananta Toer, and even Sukarno's own writings were forbidden.
- **Former "leftists" were blacklisted** — from government jobs, academia, journalism, and the military.
- **Anti-communist indoctrination** was built into the education system and remains compulsory to this day (the "National Insight" curriculum teaches that communism is incompatible with Pancasila).

---

## 1998–Present: The Lingering Trauma

After Suharto's fall in 1998 (*Reformasi*):

- No major party identifies as "left-wing" in the classical socialist or communist sense.
- The accusation of being *kiri* or *komunis* remains a potent political weapon. In every election cycle, candidates are attacked with allegations of communist links.
- The 1965 narrative is still fiercely contested — the military's version (blaming the PKI) is taught in schools, while alternative accounts are suppressed. The government has repeatedly rejected calls for a truth commission.[@CEUIndoDemocracy2026]
- Left-wing economics (welfare, protectionism, nationalism) are widely popular, but they are never labelled as "left" — they are framed as "Pancasila economics" or "populist economics."

## Effect on Political Discourse

| Before 1965 | After 1965 (New Order) | Reformasi Era |
|-------------|------------------------|---------------|
| Left–right spectrum openly debated | "Left" = taboo / criminal | "Left" = political weapon / accusation |
| PKI was a mainstream party | Communism = existential threat | Anti-communist laws still on books |
| Sukarno blended leftism, nationalism, religion | All left-adjacent ideas purged | Left-adjacent policies exist but are unnamed |

Today, Indonesia's real political cleavage is not economic left vs. right but **pluralist-nationalist vs. Islamist-conservative** — see [[indonesian-politics-pluralist-vs-islamist-spectrum]].
