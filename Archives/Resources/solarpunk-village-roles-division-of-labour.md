# Solarpunk Village: Roles & Division of Labour

A solarpunk village of 50–150 people needs coverage across four domains: physical infrastructure, food & ecology, social & governance, and economy. Below is the role map, synthesised from ecovillage case studies [@Kasper2008Redefining; @Lockyer2018Community; @HarvardGEN].

## How Labour Is Structured

In most ecovillages, labour is a mix of:
- **Specialised roles** (filled by skilled individuals)
- **Rotating community duties** (every adult contributes 4–8 hrs/week)
- **Work parties** (collective projects: building, harvesting, restoration)

Five of eight ecovillages studied by Kasper (2008) require a minimum labour contribution from members [@Kasper2008Redefining].

---

## 1. Physical Infrastructure (20–25% of adults)

| Role | # Needed (per 50 pop.) | Key Responsibilities |
|------|------------------------|---------------------|
| Green builder / architect | 1–2 | Natural building (straw bale, cob, timber), code compliance |
| Renewable energy specialist | 1 | Solar PV, wind, micro-hydro, battery storage, microgrid management [@NewcastleFindhorn] |
| Water systems manager | 1 | Rainwater catchment, greywater, composting toilets, well/pump |
| Electrician / plumber | 1–2 | Wiring, plumbing, shared infrastructure maintenance |
| Maintenance crew (rotating) | 2–4 | Tool repair, building upkeep, road/path maintenance |

---

## 2. Food & Ecology (25–35% of adults)

| Role | # Needed (per 50 pop.) | Key Responsibilities |
|------|------------------------|---------------------|
| Permaculture farm manager | 1 | Whole-system design, crop rotation, soil regeneration [@UKERCLammas] |
| Market gardeners | 3–6 | Vegetable production (6–20% food on-site early; >50% aspirational) [@Kasper2008Redefining] |
| Animal husbandry | 1–2 | Rotational grazing, poultry, eggs, dairy (optional by ethics) |
| Food processing / preservation | 1–2 | Canning, fermenting, dehydrating, community kitchen |
| Forest garden / orchardist | 1 | Perennial food systems, nut/berry production |
| Ecosystem restoration lead | 1 | Native habitat, invasive removal, controlled burns [@Lockyer2018Community] |

---

## 3. Social & Governance (10–15% of adults)

| Role | # Needed (per 50 pop.) | Key Responsibilities |
|------|------------------------|---------------------|
| Facilitator / conflict resolution | 2–3 | Consensus process, meeting facilitation, mediation [@Kasper2008Redefining] |
| Membership & outreach | 1–2 | Visitor tours, new-member intake, website, workshops [@Lockyer2018Community] |
| Education coordinator | 1–2 | Children's education (co-op model), adult skill-sharing |
| Healthcare / wellness | 1–2 | First aid, community health, connections to external providers |
| Childcare / elder care (rotating) | 2–4 | Shared care schedule (all adults contribute hours) |

---

## 4. Economy & Livelihood (10–15% of adults)

| Role | # Needed (per 50 pop.) | Key Responsibilities |
|------|------------------------|---------------------|
| Finance / land trust administrator | 1–2 | Leasehold fees, budgeting, legal compliance, insurance [@Lockyer2018Community] |
| Cottage industry leads | 2–4 | Bakery, café, craft workshop, value-added products [@Kasper2008Redefining] |
| Local currency / LETS manager | 1 | Complementary currency, timebanking, mutual credit |
| External work coordinator | 0–1 | Links to nearby town economy, remote work infrastructure |

---

## 5. Rotating Community Duties (Every Adult)

These are not full-time roles but rotated among all members:

- **Cooking shifts** — community meals happen 1–7×/week depending on the community [@Kasper2008Redefining]
- **Cleaning** — common spaces, laundry, shared facilities
- **On-call support** — emergency response, visitor hosting
- **Garden work days** — weekly collective labour parties
- **Committee participation** — every adult serves on at least one committee (land use, finance, membership, etc.)

---

## Self-Sufficiency Over Time

In early years, few ecovillages exceed 20% food self-sufficiency; the median is around 1–5% [@Kasper2008Redefining]. Only Ecovillage A produced >50% of its own food, achieved through high communalism (shared income, shared kitchens). The trajectory is:

- **Year 0–3**: 1–5% food from site (establishing gardens)
- **Year 3–5**: 6–20% (perennial systems maturing)
- **Year 5–10**: 21–50% (full permaculture, animal systems)
- **Year 10+**: >50% possible with dedicated farm manager [@Kasper2008Redefining]

## Skill Sharing & Training

Ecovillages are "living and learning centres" — members cross-train each other. Common training includes:
- Permaculture Design Certificate (PDC) courses [@UKERCLammas]
- Natural building workshops
- Consensus facilitation apprenticeships
- Ecosystem restoration practicums (e.g., prescribed burns) [@Lockyer2018Community]
