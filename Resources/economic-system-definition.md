An economic system is the organizational framework a society uses to allocate scarce resources. It answers three fundamental questions: what to produce, how to produce, and for whom. Systems range across a spectrum from pure market coordination to pure central command, with most nations operating a mixed model [@morganelli2025].

The four basic types are traditional economy (custom and subsistence), market economy (prices and private decisions), command economy (central government control), and mixed economy (hybrid of market and command). Most modern economies are mixed, including the United States and China [@morganelli2025].
