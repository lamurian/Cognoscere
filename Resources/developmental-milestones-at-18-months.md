## Summary

At eighteen months — a critical AAP screening point — the toddler has transformed into a walking, climbing, communicating individual. Weight averages about 10.9 kg (24.0 lb) and length 82.5 cm (32.5 in). The toddler walks alone, uses 6–20 words, engages in pretend play, and points to show interest — representing major leaps across all developmental domains [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~10.9 kg (range 9.4–12.7 kg); median length ~82.5 cm. Head circumference ~47.5 cm. Growth rate stable. Weight gain ~30–50 g/week. Anterior fontanelle closed in most children by 18 months.

**Emotional Maturity:** Engages in pretend play (feeding doll, talking on toy phone). Shows affection toward familiar people. Points to show others something interesting. Explores alone but with parent close by. May have temper tantrums — emotional regulation still immature. Beginning to recognise own feelings. Hugs/kisses familiar people [@misirliyan2023].

**Psychological Development:** Piaget substage 5 (tertiary circular reactions, 12–18 months). The toddler actively experiments with objects; trial-and-error problem solving. Points to one or more body parts when named. Points to things in a book. Knows what ordinary things are for (spoon, cup, comb, phone). Imitates chores (sweeping, putting toys away). Follows one-step commands.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: walks alone well; may walk up steps holding on; runs stiffly; climbs onto and off furniture; pulls toys while walking; tries to kick a ball; throws ball overhand while standing. Fine motor: scribbles spontaneously; builds tower of 2–3 cubes; uses spoon and cup independently (some spilling); helps undress self; turns pages (2–3 at a time).

**Speech and Language Development:** Says 6–20 single words (wide normal range); points to 1–3 body parts; follows one-step commands without gesture; shakes head "yes" and "no"; uses words to make requests; imitates words overheard in conversation; understands 100+ words (receptive vocabulary far exceeds expressive) [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: not walking independently by 18 months; no words at all; does not point to show things; does not point to body parts; does not imitate; no pretend play; does not follow simple commands; does not recognise familiar people; walks on toes persistently; not making eye contact; no interest in other children; loss of previously acquired skills — especially language. This is a key autism screening age per AAP guidelines.
