## Summary

Euphorbia tirucalli (patah tulang) is a slow-growing succulent shrub or tree that forms dense, impenetrable hedges ideal for security barriers in drylands [@northcarolinastateuniversityextension2024]. Growth rate is slow (mature height 2-30 ft, spread 1-10 ft). It is extremely drought-tolerant, thrives in full sun, and has few pests. Propagation is simple: cut 6-inch stems, allow to callus for 4-7 days, then plant in well-drained soil [@perez2021]. The pencil-like green branches form clusters at branch ends, creating a dense thicket that livestock and humans cannot easily penetrate. Best uses: impenetrable security barrier around kitchen gardens, boundary demarcation in dry and eroded areas. It tolerates poor soils and severe drought once established. Avoid planting near pathways or where people work due to highly toxic, caustic sap that causes skin irritation and temporary to permanent corneal damage [@northcarolinastateuniversityextension2024]. Frost-sensitive (USDA Zone 11-12). Not suitable as animal fodder. Symbiotic benefits: drought adaptation and soil conservation in semi-arid areas. The succulent stems store water, creating microhabitats. Despite toxicity, it provides shelter for birds and small wildlife. Its presence on degraded land helps stabilize soil and reduce erosion. Used extensively in Haiti and across the tropics for living fences [@martin2012].

Key sources: @northcarolinastateuniversityextension2024, @perez2021, @martin2012.
