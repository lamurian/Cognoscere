Several determinants shape whether a company-community relationship becomes symbiotic or remains extractive. Geographic rootedness matters: companies tied to specific locations have stronger incentives for local integration than footloose firms [@westskog2024]. Value chain position is critical: firms capturing higher local value-add create more spillover benefits [@cuevasvalenzuela2022]. Governance structures, particularly local ownership and long-term family stewardship, foster deeper community commitment [@cyr2022]. Pre-existing social capital determines community receptiveness: municipality place attachment and collective efficacy create conditions for effective partnership [@garczarekbk2026]. Local Community Impact (LCI) is the strongest mediator between group membership and pro-social consumer behaviors, meaning perceived contribution to community drives mutual value creation [@garczarekbk2026]. The institutional environment, including legal context and community norms, shapes trust development [@cyr2022].
