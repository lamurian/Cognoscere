# Solarpunk Village: Population & Scaling

Optimal population sizes for a solarpunk village, based on academic research on human group sizes and ecovillage data.

## The Research Base

**Dunbar's number (~150)** is the cognitive limit for stable reciprocal relationships in humans. Historical Hutterite farming communities split once they reach 150. This is the ceiling for trust-based, consensus-governed groups.[^1][^2]

**Natural community sizes** in historical small-scale agricultural societies cluster at **50, 150, and 500**. These are deeply rooted in human social cognition — 50 is the band/hamlet, 150 the village, 500 the tribe.[^1]

**Ecovillage data** (Kasper 2008) confirms the GEN definition: "human-scale settlement (usually between **50 and 500 members**, though there are exceptions)" intended to be full-featured.[^3]

## Phased Growth Model

| Phase | Population | Timeframe | Character |
|-------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Core founding** | 5–15 | Year 0–1 | Legal entity, land purchase, vision & values |
| **Seed village** | 20–40 | Year 1–3 | Basic division of labour, first infrastructure |
| **Stable village** | 50–150 | Year 3–8 | Full-featured, resilient economy, mature governance |
| **Mature federation** | 150–500 | Year 8+ | Split into sub-communities or federate[^2] |

### Why 50–150 Is the Sweet Spot

- **Below 50**: hard to maintain diverse skills; too few people for a full-featured settlement. Dancing Rabbit started with ~10 and found ~60 comfortable but still seeking more specialists.[^4]
- **50–150**: enough for viable division of labour (25+ distinct roles), resilient food production, and vibrant social life. EcoVillage at Ithaca has ~160, Sieben Linden ~140.[^3][^5]
- **Above 150**: consensus governance becomes unwieldy; communities typically evolve representative councils (as Dancing Rabbit did when it grew).[^4]
- **At ~400**: Ecovillage E (un-named in research) found this to be the "magic number" — still intimate but big enough for a strong internal economy.[^3]

## Land Requirements

| Population | Land estimate | Green space preserved |
|------------|--------------|----------------------|
| 20–40 (seed) | 50–150 acres | 85–95% conserved[^4] |
| 50–150 (stable) | 150–500 acres | 85–95% conserved |

Dancing Rabbit's 280 acres houses ~60 people with 90% green space preserved.[^4] Architects of conservation communities recommend 50% green space "in town", 75% in suburbs, **90% in rural ecovillages**.[^3]

## The Dunbar Principle in Practice

Garrett Hardin (1988) noted that Hutterites regard ~150 as the limiting size for their farming communities. Once a community reaches this size, steps are taken to split it into two daughter communities.[^2] This principle prevents the breakdown of trust-based governance and keeps the village "human-scale."

A solarpunk village should plan for either:
- **Stable at 50–150** with no further growth
- **Federation model** — grow to 150–500 by creating semi-autonomous neighbourhoods (clusters of 20–50) with shared central infrastructure
