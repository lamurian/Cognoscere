Indonesia operates substantial social programs (PKH, BPNT, PIP, JKN) but their redistributive impact is limited by low funding and targeting inefficiencies. The Gini declined from 0.384 (2021) to 0.375 (2025), partly attributable to expanded social assistance [@unnadhiro2025].

Four World Bank priorities [@worldbank2016]: (1) Improve local health, education, and family planning delivery — 37% stunting is the binding constraint; (2) Revitalize family planning to address contraceptive use decline; (3) Scale skills training — only 1% of youth attend technical training, and less than 40% of large firms train workers vs 70% in East Asia; (4) Strengthen shock protection — only 30% of the poorest have health insurance vs 72% of the richest.

Fuel subsidy reform is a success story: redirecting IDR 200+ trillion annually from regressive energy subsidies to targeted cash transfers and infrastructure improved both fiscal space and equity [@unnadhiro2025].
