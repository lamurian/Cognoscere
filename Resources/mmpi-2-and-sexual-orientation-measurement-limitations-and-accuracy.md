The MMPI-2 (Minnesota Multiphasic Personality Inventory-2) is a 567-item standardized psychometric test designed to assess adult personality and psychopathology — not sexual orientation. Its 10 clinical scales and supplementary scales measure constructs like depression, paranoia, hypochondriasis, social introversion, and gender-role conformity.[^1] Using it to "measure LGBT tendency" is an off-label application that the test was never designed or validated for.

## The Mf Scale (Scale 5): Masculinity-Femininity

The only MMPI-2 scale historically associated with LGBT-related measurement is Scale 5 (Mf), which contains 56 items assessing interests, hobbies, aesthetic preferences, and personal sensitivity.[^2] It was designed to measure conformity to stereotypical masculine and feminine gender roles, not sexual orientation. The original MMPI also included supplemental GM (Gender-Masculine) and GF (Gender-Feminine) scales.

Mid-20th century researchers sometimes used the Mf scale to attempt identifying homosexual individuals, based on the pathologizing assumption that homosexuality was a form of gender inversion. This approach was part of the broader psychiatric framing of homosexuality as a mental disorder, which the APA removed from the DSM in 1973.[^3]

## What Research Shows

A 2015 study comparing MMPI-2 profiles of 55 homosexual and 55 heterosexual men found statistically significant group-level differences on several scales, particularly Mf and Si (Social Introversion).[^4] However, group-level statistical significance does not imply reliable individual classification. Key limitations include:

- Substantial overlap in score distributions between groups — many heterosexual men score in the "atypical" range and many homosexual men score in the "typical" range.
- No validated cut-off scores exist for classifying sexual orientation using the MMPI-2.
- The Mf scale measures gender-role interests (preferences for stereotypically masculine/feminine activities), which are correlated with but not equivalent to sexual orientation.
- The test was normed on general and clinical populations, not on groups defined by sexual orientation.

## Accuracy Assessment

Claims in popular discussion sometimes cite "80% accuracy" for the Mf scale in classifying homosexuality. This figure does not withstand scrutiny. Base-rate effects are critical: with an LGB population prevalence of roughly 5–10%, even 80% sensitivity/specificity would produce a high proportion of false positives. The Mf scale's actual discriminative validity is poor, with significant misclassification rates.[^5]

Modern clinical psychology and the APA do not support using the MMPI-2 for sexual orientation assessment. The MMPI-2's official publishers (University of Minnesota Press / Pearson) list no validated use for this purpose. Appropriate instruments for measuring sexual orientation include the Kinsey Scale, Klein Sexual Orientation Grid, and Sell Assessment of Sexual Orientation — all of which directly assess attraction, behavior, and identity rather than inferring orientation from gender-role interests.

## Ethical Concerns

Using the MMPI-2 to infer LGBT orientation carries ethical risks: it pathologizes sexual orientation by linking it to a psychopathology instrument, it has no established clinical utility for this use, and it could cause harm if used in discriminatory settings such as employment screening or custody evaluations.[^6]
