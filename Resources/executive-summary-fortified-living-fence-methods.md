Research confirms living fences can outperform concrete walls through a multi-layer fortification strategy. Wood compression parallel to grain (~80 MPa) exceeds concrete (17-40 MPa), and living wood self-strengthens through reaction wood formation while concrete degrades [@swedishwood2022; @middleton2019]. Historical evidence proves feasibility at scale: the Great Hedge of India (2,500 miles), Wiechula's 120m living wall, and Baubotanik three-story towers [@moxham2001; @wikipediacontributors2025c; @mylo2023].

The fortification strategy uses five complementary layers: gabion/MSE core for impact resistance, inosculated living posts at 2-4m intervals, pleached diamond-lattice interweave reaching full form in 2-3 years, thorny species (Bougainvillea, Euphorbia) as biological razor wire, and periodic steel/concrete reinforcement during establishment [@tensarinternational2020; @morningchores2025; @positivebloom2025]. Living fences cost $25-50/linear foot vs $63-275/ft for concrete, with lifecycle economics further favoring living systems through self-repair and revenue from carbon credits, fodder, and fruit [@brentwoodfence2025; @ngongo2022].
