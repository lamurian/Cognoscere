# Private-Sector Weekly Economic Reports for Indonesia

## Summary

Indonesian financial institutions publish free weekly reports that synthesize official data releases, market movements, and geopolitical developments into ready-to-use analysis. These reports are useful for triangulating raw official data with market interpretation.

PEFINDO, Indonesia's domestic credit rating agency, publishes the Weekly Economic Update (WEU) every Monday covering global and domestic macro developments, commodity markets, stock markets, exchange rates, and SUN auction results. Free PDF download at https://www.pefindo.com/weekly-economic-update. Samuel Sekuritas publishes Weekly Economic Insights authored by Chief Economist Dr. Fithra Faisal Hastiadi, covering GDP outlook, inflation, trade balance, foreign exchange reserves, consumer confidence, and monetary policy. Available in English and Indonesian at https://samuel.co.id/research-reports/samuel-weekly-economic-insights/.

Trading Economics aggregates all official Indonesia economic data releases with actual values, consensus forecasts, and historical charts at https://tradingeconomics.com/indonesia/calendar. Capital Economics covers Indonesia within its Asia Economics Weekly service at https://www.capitaleconomics.com/taxonomy/term/60.

## Key Points

- PEFINDO and Samuel Sekuritas provide the most consistent free weekly analysis reports
- Reports contextualize raw official data with market interpretation and forward-looking analysis
- Trading Economics provides the calendar-based view with consensus forecasts from multiple analysts
- These reports cover both domestic developments and global spillover effects on Indonesia
- Useful for cross-referencing with official data to detect divergences between data and market sentiment
