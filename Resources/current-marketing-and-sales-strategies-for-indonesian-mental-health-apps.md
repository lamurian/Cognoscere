## Summary

Indonesian mental health apps employ a mix of B2B corporate sales (EAP programs), content marketing, social media engagement, community events, and freemium-funnel conversions. B2B is the primary revenue driver for most platforms, while B2C serves as brand awareness and user acquisition [@pamelavaladez2026; @stacyoentoro2022].

## Key Points

**B2B Corporate Sales (Employee Assistance Programs).** This is the most scalable revenue channel. Riliv for Business, KALM for Business, and Satu Persen for Companies all target HR departments and corporate wellness budgets. Services include: mental health assessments, counseling sessions (per-employee or session-based), webinars and workshops, manager toolkits, and analytics dashboards for HR. Pricing is per-employee subscription or custom packages. This channel benefits from Indonesia's growing corporate wellness awareness -- 53% of Indonesian workers report poor mental health affecting productivity [@pamelavaladez2026]. Other EAP competitors include Meditopia, MantraCare, IMPROVE, Mindtera, and EAP Indonesia.

**TikTok and Social Media.** Riliv leads Indonesian mental health apps on TikTok with 386,600 followers and 12.8M likes. Content strategy: short-form educational videos about mental health topics, destigmatization content, relatable Gen Z psychology content. Satu Persen leads on YouTube with 3.63M subscribers, producing long-form psychology and self-development videos, podcast episodes, and shorts. Instagram is used by all players for infographics, reels, and community building [@anddrewrichmondthezo2024; @vidiq2026].

**Content Marketing and Education.** All major players maintain educational blogs with psychology articles. KALM publishes articles on relationships, personality, adulting, and self-help. Satu Persen produces YouTube videos, podcasts (Top 10 in Indonesia), and blog content. This content serves dual purposes: SEO-driven organic discovery and establishing authority/trust in a high-stigma market.

**Community Events and Webinars.** KALM hosts paid KALM Talks (Rp50,000-62,500 per session) covering mental health topics like boundary-setting, attachment styles, and family triggers. Satu Persen runs community collaborations, offline camping events, and Instagram Lives. These events generate leads for paid services [@ptsanubarisenantiasasejahtera2026].

**Pricing and Freemium Models.** Riliv: Rp100,000/session (low-cost leader), freemium with free meditation content. KALM: Rp33,000/day ongoing chat (installment model). Bicarakan.id: varies by psychologist. Satu Persen: content is free, paid for coaching/courses/assessments. Qalboo: freemium with free Islamic mindfulness content. The common pattern: free content for funnel top, paid counseling/coaching for conversion [@anddrewrichmondthezo2024].

**Key Growth Channels.** (1) Google Play Store / App Store Optimization; (2) Referral programs (KALM, Satu Persen); (3) Corporate HR partnerships; (4) University and campus collaborations (Satu Persen with OSIS and student organizations); (5) Government and NGO partnerships for awareness campaigns (Riliv with Puskesmas); (6) Accelerator and VC network effects (Bicarakan.id in Startup Studio Indonesia; Qalboo in Antler networks).

**Weaknesses in Current Strategies.** (1) Over-reliance on organic/viral growth rather than paid acquisition; (2) Low conversion from free content to paid counseling; (3) No dominant player has achieved network effects or defensible moat; (4) B2B sales cycles are long (HR budget allocation); (5) User retention beyond initial counseling episodes is unclear.
