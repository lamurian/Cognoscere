## Summary

The Generalized Anxiety Disorder-7 (GAD-7) is a 7-item self-report instrument designed to screen for generalized anxiety disorder and assess its severity. Items assess: feeling nervous or anxious, unable to stop or control worrying, worrying too much about different things, trouble relaxing, restlessness, irritability, and fear of something awful happening. Each item is rated 0 (not at all) to 3 (nearly every day) over the past two weeks, producing a total score range of 0-21. Severity thresholds: 5-9 mild, 10-14 moderate, 15-21 severe anxiety.

The original criterion-standard validation study by Spitzer et al. (2006) was conducted in 15 primary care clinics in the United States (n=2,740). Using mental health professional telephone interview as the criterion standard within one week of the self-report, a cutoff >=10 optimized sensitivity (89%) and specificity (82%) [@spitzer2006]. The GAD-7 demonstrated good reliability and factorial validity, with factor analysis confirming that GAD and depression symptoms are distinct dimensions despite frequent co-occurrence. Increasing scores were strongly associated with functional impairment across all six Medical Outcomes Study Short-Form General Health Survey scales and with disability days.

Systematic review evidence from Martin-Key et al. (2022) reports GAD-7 sensitivity ranging from 0.75 to 0.87 and specificity from 0.55 to 0.78 for GAD detection across different settings and populations [@martinkey2022]. The GAD-7 has been translated into multiple languages with generally preserved psychometric properties. It is the most widely used brief anxiety screening instrument in clinical practice and research.

## Key Points

- 7 items, 0-3 Likert scale, 2-week recall, total 0-21
- Original validation (Spitzer 2006): Se=0.89, Sp=0.82 at cutoff >=10 [@spitzer2006]
- Systematic review ranges: Se 0.75-0.87, Sp 0.55-0.78 [@martinkey2022]
- Two-factor structure (somatic and cognitive-affective anxiety) supported
- Strong construct validity: increasing scores correlate with functional impairment and disability days
- Most widely used brief GAD screening tool globally
