Adaptive coping strategies, particularly cognitive reappraisal and active coping, show positive associations with challenge appraisal and resilience, and negative associations with perceived stress.

Thomas and Zolkoski (2020) found: Cognitive Reappraisal vs BRS r = 0.35 (p < .05); Cognitive Reappraisal vs PSS-10 r = -0.25 (p < .05). Path analysis confirmed reappraisal as a significant predictor of resilience (β = 0.40, p < .05), which in turn predicted lower stress. Chmitorz et al. (2018) reported BRS positively correlated with active coping, positive reframing, acceptance, and humor coping strategies.

Within the challenge-hindrance framework [@cavanaugh2000; @searle2015], individuals who use adaptive coping strategies are more likely to appraise stressors as challenges rather than hindrances. Challenge appraisal activates approach-oriented coping, which enhances resilience and reduces the negative impact of stress. Reappraisal specifically reframes threat-based interpretations into growth-oriented ones, breaking the helplessness cycle.
