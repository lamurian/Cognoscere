Communism is a socioeconomic system aiming for common ownership of the means of production and a classless, stateless society. In theory, production is organized for use rather than profit, and distribution follows the principle "from each according to his ability, to each according to his needs." The concept was articulated by Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels in The Communist Manifesto (1848) [@thulin2026].

In its implemented forms (Marxism-Leninism), communism meant single-party state control through central planning, nationalized industry, and collective agriculture. These systems achieved rapid industrialization and basic welfare provision but suffered from allocative inefficiency, information problems in planning, and political repression. The Soviet Union, Maoist China, and Eastern Bloc countries were the primary historical examples.
