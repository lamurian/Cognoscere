## Summary

At fourteen months, the toddler walks well and increasingly uses walking as the primary mode of locomotion. Weight reaches approximately 10.0 kg (22.0 lb) and length 77.5 cm (30.5 in). The toddler experiments with cause-and-effect during play, actively testing how objects and people respond to their actions [@misirliyan2023].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~10.0 kg (range 8.7–11.8 kg); median length ~77.5 cm. Weight gain ~40–60 g/week; growth rate stable but slow compared to infancy.

**Emotional Maturity:** Increasing independence — insists on doing things without help. May show frustration when unable to communicate or perform a task. Engages in social play with caregivers (rolling ball back and forth). Shows affection through hugs and kisses. Beginning of empathy — may try to comfort a distressed caregiver.

**Psychological Development:** Toddler actively explores object properties — fitting, stacking, sorting. Engages in early pretend play (pretends to drink from toy cup). Searches for hidden objects systematically. Points to pictures in books when named. Recognises self in photographs. Follows simple commands without gestures.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: walks well with few falls; carries toys while walking; stoops and recovers without falling; climbs onto furniture; may attempt to climb stairs with help. Fine motor: scribbles with crayon; stacks 2 blocks; turns pages of board book one at a time; feeds self with spoon (messy); drinks from cup with more control.

**Speech and Language Development:** Uses 3–5 words consistently; understands 50+ words; follows simple commands without gesture ("bring me your shoes"); points to 2–3 body parts; says "no" with head shake; uses jargon (strings of babble with adult-like intonation) [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: not walking independently; not imitating actions; no words at all; does not point to indicate wants; does not respond to simple requests; does not recognise familiar people; persistent mouthing of objects beyond expected exploration; no eye contact during interaction.
