## Summary

Despite being smaller than Maryland, the Netherlands is the world's second-largest agricultural exporter by value, after the United States. This is achieved through a combination of high-tech greenhouse agriculture, world-class research at Wageningen University (a global hub for agricultural sciences), and exceptional logistics infrastructure. Dutch greenhouses cover approximately 10,000 hectares and use advanced hydroponics, LED lighting, and precision climate control. Farmers use up to 90% less water for greenhouse crops compared to traditional open-field farming.

The country exports approximately €65 billion in agricultural goods annually. Key products include tomatoes, cucumbers, bell peppers, flowers (the Netherlands dominates the global flower trade), potatoes, onions, and dairy. Over 60% of land is used for agriculture, but Dutch farmers produce with yields far exceeding global averages due to technology. The sector employs less than 2% of the labor force yet generates enormous export surpluses. The OECD notes the Netherlands holds a leading position in agricultural sciences globally.

## Key Points

- World's 2nd largest agricultural exporter (after US)
- ~€65 billion in annual agricultural exports
- 90% less water used in greenhouse farming vs. open-field
- Wageningen University: world-leading agricultural research center
- Exports not just products but technology: greenhouse design, sensors, agtech
- 10,000 hectares of advanced greenhouses
