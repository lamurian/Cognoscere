The Healthy People domain captures the health status of the population through five subdomains and 21 indicators. **Mortality (Pe.1)** includes life expectancy and avoidable deaths (standardised number of deaths from causes amenable to prevention or treatment). **Physical health conditions (Pe.2)** measures prevalence of six chronic conditions at UTLA level: dementia, musculoskeletal conditions, respiratory conditions, cardiovascular conditions, cancer, and kidney disease [@frenisterrantino2022].

**Difficulties in daily life (Pe.3)** covers disability that impacts daily activities, difficulty completing activities of daily living (ADLs), and frailty. **Personal well-being (Pe.4)** measures four subjective wellbeing indicators: life satisfaction, life worthwhileness, happiness, and anxiety (the ONS4 national wellbeing questions). **Mental health (Pe.5)** includes suicide rates, depression prevalence, and self-harm prevalence [@frenisterrantino2022].

Data sources include ONS mortality registration data, the Quality and Outcomes Framework (QOF) from GP practices, the Annual Population Survey (APS) for wellbeing, and the General Practice Patient Survey. Indicators are measured as rates per 100,000 population or as percentages, then normalized to z-scores. Higher scores indicate better health outcomes [@frenisterrantino2022].
