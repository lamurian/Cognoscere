In March 2025, the World Health Organization launched new guidance to help all countries reform and strengthen mental health policies and systems, addressing the urgent gap where up to 90% of people with severe mental health conditions in some countries receive no care [@worldhealthorganization2025]. The guidance provides a framework for rights-based, person-centered, and recovery-oriented mental health policies that ensure equitable access to quality services within Universal Health Coverage (UHC).

The guidance identifies five key policy areas requiring urgent reform: (1) leadership, governance, and enabling conditions; (2) service organization and development, shifting from institutional models to community-based care; (3) workforce and human resource development, addressing severe shortages particularly in LMICs; (4) person-centered, recovery-oriented, rights-based interventions; and (5) addressing social and structural determinants of mental health including poverty, housing, education, and employment [@worldhealthorganization2025].

A central theme is protecting and upholding human rights by aligning mental health policies with international human rights standards, including the UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities. The guidance emphasizes promoting holistic care encompassing lifestyle, physical health, psychological, social, and economic interventions. Crucially, it calls for ensuring people with lived experience are empowered to participate in policy planning and design [@worldhealthorganization2025]. The guidance builds on the WHO QualityRights initiative and is designed to be flexible across low-, middle-, and high-income countries depending on national context and resources.
