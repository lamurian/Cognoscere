To make slow-simmered cherry tomato pasta for 4 people [@kimball2019], you need ⅓ cup extra-virgin olive oil, 4 garlic cloves (thinly sliced), ¼ tsp red pepper flakes, 2 bay leaves, 1 lb cherry or grape tomatoes (halved), ½ tsp sugar, 1 tsp salt, 1 cup water, ¾ tsp smoked paprika, 2 tbsp fresh sage (chopped, divided), 12 oz bucatini or fettuccine, and grated pecorino or Parmesan for serving. Heat the oil in a 12-inch skillet over medium-high. Add garlic, pepper flakes, and bay leaves; cook 30 seconds until fragrant. Add tomatoes, sugar, salt, and water. Bring to a simmer and cook until tomatoes begin breaking down, about 4 minutes. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer uncovered until tomatoes completely break down and sauce thickens, 45-55 minutes, stirring every 10 minutes. Add a splash of water if the sauce dries before tomatoes fully break down. Off heat, discard bay leaves, stir in smoked paprika and half the sage; cover. Cook pasta in salted boiling water until al dente; reserve ½ cup pasta water before draining. Toss pasta with sauce, adding pasta water to loosen if needed. Serve topped with remaining sage, cheese, and olive oil. The sauce keeps refrigerated for up to 5 days and can easily be doubled.
