Cities are adding 2.5 billion dwellers by 2050, making destructive urban sprawl unsustainable. The vertical city paradigm offers a denser alternative, but conventional skyscrapers cause severe environmental harm: all-glass towers are 'incredibly inefficient' due to energy loss, and the building sector accounts for approximately 40% of global CO2 emissions [@alkodmany2018]. Eco-friendly skyscraper design addresses three intersecting crises: climate change (buildings are major emitters), biodiversity collapse (urban areas destroy habitat), and public health (poor air quality and lack of nature access harm well-being). The COVID-19 pandemic underscored the need for cleaner air and reconnection with nature in dense urban settings [@alkodmany2023]. Green skyscrapers can transform urban landscapes from sources of environmental degradation into regenerative systems that actively restore ecosystems and improve quality of life.
