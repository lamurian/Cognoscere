## Summary

At six years, the child is firmly in the early school-age period. Weight averages about 21.5 kg (47.4 lb) and height 117 cm (46.1 in). The six-year-old reads simple books, writes sentences, performs basic arithmetic, navigates complex peer dynamics, and demonstrates logical thinking about concrete problems — reflecting the transition into Piaget's concrete operational stage [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~21.5 kg (range 17.8–26.0 kg); median height ~117 cm. Weight gain ~2–3 kg/year; height ~5–6 cm/year. First permanent molars present; lower central incisors may be lost and permanent ones emerging. Mixed dentition begins. Head circumference adult size nearly achieved.

**Emotional Maturity:** Develops stronger sense of right and wrong. Experiences guilt when breaking rules. Shows empathy consistently. May be highly critical of self — perfectionism can emerge. Needs encouragement and praise for effort. Peer approval becomes increasingly important. Can work and play cooperatively in groups of 3–5 children. Understands and respects rules of games; may be upset by unfairness. Developing sense of humour — tells and understands jokes.

**Psychological Development:** Concrete operational stage (Piaget, 7–11 years, though onset varies). The child can now perform logical operations on physical objects — understands conservation of number, mass, and liquid volume. Can classify objects hierarchically (animal → dog → poodle). Understands reversibility (3 + 4 = 7, so 7 − 4 = 3). Reads simple books independently. Writes simple sentences with phonetic spelling (invented spelling). Adds and subtracts numbers up to 10. Tells time to the hour and half-hour. Understands left and right on others. Attention span 20–30 minutes [@misirliyan2023].

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: skips, gallops, hops, and jumps with coordination and rhythm; rides bicycle (many without training wheels); throws and catches balls with accuracy; participates in organised sports (soccer, T-ball, gymnastics, swimming); jumps rope; balances on one foot for 20+ seconds; walks on balance beam. Fine motor: writes all letters and numbers legibly; draws detailed person, house, trees, animals; colours neatly within lines; uses scissors to cut complex shapes; ties shoelaces; uses keyboard and mouse; builds complex LEGO structures from instructions; uses tools (hammer, screwdriver) capably [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

**Speech and Language Development:** Speech completely intelligible — most articulation errors resolved (r, l, s, th may still be developing); vocabulary 4000–6000+ words; uses complex and compound sentences with adult grammar; tells elaborate stories with structured plots; reads aloud with expression and comprehension; understands and uses figurative language; can explain rules of games and give directions; participates in classroom discussions; writes paragraphs with topic sentences; understands wordplay (puns, riddles) [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: significant clumsiness or frequent falls; cannot hop on one foot; cannot throw or catch a ball; speech still contains major articulation errors affecting intelligibility; cannot read simple words by end of first grade; cannot write legible letters; cannot follow three-step instructions; cannot focus on a task for 10+ minutes; no friends or persistent peer rejection; extreme aggression or withdrawal; cannot dress self independently; cannot use toilet independently; persistent enuresis or encopresis beyond expected norms; cannot separate from parent for school; loss of previously acquired skills.
