## Summary

At twelve months — the first birthday — the infant transitions toward toddlerhood. Weight has roughly tripled from birth to approximately 9.6 kg (21.2 lb) and length reaches about 75.5 cm (29.7 in). Walking, first meaningful words, and deliberate social communication mark this milestone [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~9.6 kg (range 8.3–11.3 kg); median length ~75.5 cm. Birth weight typically tripled by 12 months. Head circumference ~46 cm. Growth velocity continues to slow: weight gain ~40–70 g/week, length gain ~1 cm/month.

**Emotional Maturity:** Cries when familiar people leave. Shows fear in some situations. Hands you a book when wants to hear a story. Repeats sounds or actions to get attention. Puts out arm or leg to help with dressing. Shows affection to familiar people. Beginning of self-awareness — recognises self in mirror [@misirliyan2023].

**Psychological Development:** Piaget's substage 4: coordination of secondary circular reactions. The infant now purposefully coordinates actions to achieve novel goals (pushing a stool to climb onto chair). Finds hidden objects easily. Explores objects by trial and error (shape sorter). Imitates gestures and actions. Looks at correct picture when object is named. Follows simple directions.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: stands alone; may walk independently (2–3 steps); walks holding onto furniture (cruising); may take several independent steps. Fine motor: precise pincer grasp; puts objects into containers and takes them out; bangs two objects together; lets go of objects voluntarily; pokes with index finger; tries to use spoon; drinks from cup with assistance.

**Speech and Language Development:** Says 1–3 words with meaning (beyond "mama/dada"); tries to imitate words; understands simple commands ("come here," "give me"); responds to "no"; uses simple gestures (waving bye-bye, shaking head "no"); looks at object/person when named. Babbles with inflection patterns of native language [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: not crawling or moving independently; cannot stand when supported; does not sit independently; does not use pincer grasp; not pointing to objects of interest; does not say single words (including "mama/dada"); does not respond to name; does not use gestures like waving; does not look where you point; loss of previously acquired skills; not seeking caregiver when distressed.
