## The Challenge

Indonesia has over 81,000 villages where roughly 40% of the population lives. Rural poverty (13.2% in 2018) is nearly double the urban rate (7%). The core modernization challenge is transforming subsistence agricultural economies into diversified, value-added, digitally-connected communities that generate rising incomes and better living conditions.

## Key Levers for Modernization

Seven interconnected strategies drive village modernization in Indonesia:

1. **Dana Desa (Village Fund)** -- Since the 2014 Village Law, the central government has allocated IDR 609.9 trillion (roughly USD 40 billion) directly to villages, the largest decentralized development fund in the world.

2. **Smart Village Framework** -- A 6-pillar SDG-based approach (Smart People, Smart Living, Smart Environment, Smart Government, Smart Economy, Smart Mobility) that localizes sustainable development at the village level.

3. **Agricultural Value Chain Upgrading** -- Moving from raw commodity sales to post-harvest processing, branding, and digital market access through BUMDes (village-owned enterprises).

4. **Land Reform and Tenure Security** -- Addressing the structural problem where 68% of land is controlled by 1% of the population, redistributing land to give households confidence to invest in long-term improvements.

5. **Community-Driven Development** -- Working through existing cultural institutions (gotong royong, adat councils, musyawarah deliberation) rather than imposing top-down solutions, which historically failed.

6. **Digital Transformation** -- Bridging the urban-rural connectivity gap, deploying village information systems for transparent governance, and enabling e-commerce and fintech access.

7. **Women's Economic Inclusion** -- Unlocking the labor force by recognizing women's dominant role in agriculture, removing barriers to credit and extension services, and ensuring joint land titling.

## What Works

The combination that produces the best outcomes is: secure land tenure + cooperative enterprise structures + digital market access + transparent governance + investment in human capital. Infrastructure alone does not raise GDP -- it must be paired with productive enterprise and skills.

## Key Challenges

- Corruption: 851 village fund corruption cases involving 973 individuals (half are village heads).
- Capacity gaps: Many village officials lack skills for financial management, digital systems, and enterprise development.
- Digital divide: Thousands of villages still lack reliable electricity and internet.
- Land inequality: Agrarian conflicts continue to displace smallholder farmers.
