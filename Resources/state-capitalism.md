State capitalism is a system where the state acts as the dominant economic player, using markets primarily for political goals. The government owns or controls strategic industries through state-owned enterprises (SOEs), sovereign wealth funds, and directed investment. The state functions as entrepreneur, investor, and planner while maintaining capitalist market mechanisms [@thulin2026].

Modern examples include China's socialist market economy, Singapore's government-linked companies, Norway's oil-funded sovereign wealth fund, and Russia's state-controlled energy sector. State capitalism enables rapid catch-up growth and infrastructure development but risks cronyism, allocative inefficiency, and political capture of economic decisions.
