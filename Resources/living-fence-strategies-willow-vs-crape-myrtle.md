Willow is the fastest and most flexible species for living fences, rooting easily from cuttings and growing up to 6 ft/year. Diamond lattice fedges reach full form in 2–3 years and last 30–50 years as living structures [@albert2024; @keiren2023; @greayer2024; @penn2024]. Willow requires heavy maintenance: 2–3 structural pruning sessions per year, consistent moisture, and siting at least 33 ft from structures due to aggressive roots [@albert2024; @penn2024]. Crape myrtle living fences take 5+ years to establish and are less flexible for weaving, but offer exceptional ornamental value with 100+ days of summer blooms, attractive peeling bark, and drought tolerance once established [@moore2006]. Crape myrtle requires annual pruning and pest management (powdery mildew, bark scale, aphids) [@moore2006]. The choice depends on priority: willow for rapid structural complexity, crape myrtle for flowering ornamental living fences.
