Joglo and Limasan in Central Java are built on the ground, associated with nobility. Joglo features a pyramid roof resembling a sacred mountain with a pendopo pavilion and separated service areas [@widayati2019]. Minangkabau Rumah Gadang has upsweeping gonjong roof ends symbolising buffalo horns.

Torajan Tongkonan is dwarfed by exaggerated saddle roofs on piles. Balinese architecture uses paduraksa gates, meru towers, and bale pavilions derived from Majapahit Hindu-Buddhist traditions [@wikipediacontributors2025]. Nias flexible nail-less construction provides proven earthquake durability, making structures almost impregnable in former tribal warfare [@wikipediacontributors2025].
