Maoism applies Marxism-Leninism to agrarian societies, developed by Mao Zedong in China. Unlike classical Marxism which centers the urban proletariat, Maoism identifies the peasantry as the main revolutionary class. It emphasizes protracted people's war, rural base areas encircling cities, and continuous class struggle even after revolution [@thulin2026].

Key features include the Great Leap Forward (rapid industrialization through mass mobilization), the Cultural Revolution (purges of capitalist-roaders), and the concept of self-reliance. Maoism influenced revolutionary movements in Asia, Africa, and Latin America. After Mao's death, China transitioned toward state capitalism under Deng Xiaoping's reforms.
