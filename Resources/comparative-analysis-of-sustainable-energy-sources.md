## Most Cost-Effective Solution: Ideal Scenario

In an ideal scenario (ample sun, good wind, suitable geography, sufficient capital), the most cost-effective portfolio combines:

**1. Solar PV** ($20-40/MWh) -- The cheapest electricity source ever built. Use fixed-tilt or single-axis tracking in sunny regions. Add bifacial panels for 5-15% boost at minimal extra cost. Useful for daytime peaking.

**2. Onshore Wind** ($26-50/MWh) -- Complements solar by generating at night and in winter. Largest turbines in best wind sites achieve lowest costs. Combined solar-wind at a 60:40 ratio minimises storage requirements in most climates.

**3. Hydropower (existing)** -- Already depreciated hydro provides the cheapest stored energy. New run-of-river or small hydro is competitive where water resources exist.

**4. Battery Storage** (lithium-ion, $100-150/kWh installed) -- Add 2-4 hours of storage to firm solar and capture price arbitrage. Costs continue to fall 15-20% per year.

**5. Geothermal** (base-load) -- Where hydrothermal resources exist, geothermal provides 24/7 clean power at moderate cost with minimal land use.

## Key Takeaway

Solar PV plus onshore wind, supplemented by battery storage and existing hydropower, is the most cost-effective combination in most regions globally. No single technology dominates -- a diversified portfolio minimises system cost, ensures reliability, and reduces environmental impact. For off-grid applications, solar PV plus batteries is the clear winner at current prices.
