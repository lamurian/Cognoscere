## Summary

At twenty-seven months (2 years 3 months), the toddler consolidates language, motor skills, and social awareness. Weight averages about 13.1 kg (28.9 lb) and height 91 cm (35.8 in). The child now speaks in three-word phrases, runs with agility, and begins to engage in interactive play with peers — moving beyond parallel play toward cooperative exchanges [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~13.1 kg (range 11.2–15.2 kg); median height ~91 cm. Weight gain ~2–3 kg/year during preschool years; height gain ~7–8 cm/year. Growth tracked on CDC 2–20 year charts. Head circumference ~48.5 cm.

**Emotional Maturity:** Shows a wider emotional range — pride, guilt, and embarrassment are observable. May exhibit possessiveness over toys; learning to share with adult guidance. Separates more easily from parents in familiar settings. Shows affection openly. Expresses defiance as part of autonomy development. Beginning to understand the concept of taking turns.

**Psychological Development:** Piaget's preoperational stage (2–7 years). The child uses symbols — words represent objects, pretend play becomes symbolic. Egocentric thinking predominates — difficulty seeing others' perspectives. Sorts by shape and colour. Engages in make-believe play with dolls, animals, and toy figures. Completes 3–4 piece puzzles. Points to self in photos.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: runs easily; jumps with both feet leaving ground; climbs well; walks up and down stairs alternating feet with rail; kicks ball with direction; throws ball overhand 3–5 feet. Fine motor: builds tower of 6–8 blocks; turns pages one at a time; scribbles with circular strokes; uses spoon and fork well; washes and dries hands with help; begins to dress self with assistance [@misirliyan2023].

**Speech and Language Development:** Uses 100–250 words; speaks in 2–3 word phrases; speech 60–70% intelligible to strangers; uses pronouns (I, me, you, mine); asks "what" and "where" questions; names most common objects; follows two-step commands; uses some plurals and past tense; tells about immediate experiences; uses k, g, f, t, d, n sounds [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: not walking steadily; not running; fewer than 30 words; not combining two words; speech largely unintelligible to family; no pretend play; not following simple commands; does not show interest in other children; no eye contact; persistent drooling; not pointing to body parts; loss of previously acquired skills; limited range of emotions.
