## Summary

Although only 3 of 33 intervention studies in a major systematic review used organisational-level (rather than individual-level) approaches, those that did showed significant quantitative reductions in burnout [@cohen2023].

**Workload reduction** (Gregory et al., 2018): Employing a certified medical assistant to handle clinical tasks for physicians significantly reduced emotional exhaustion (MBI-EE) compared to the control group. The reduction in emotional exhaustion was sustained at the 6-month follow-up, though the workload subscale of the Areas of Worklife Scale showed signs of reverting toward baseline, suggesting a potential dose-response effect [@cohen2023].

**Job crafting** (Gordon et al., 2018): A 3-week intervention where healthcare professionals set weekly goals to improve teamwork and quality of care showed significantly higher work engagement (Utrecht Work Engagement Scale) and lower emotional exhaustion (Oldenburg Burnout Inventory) compared to a waitlist control group. Both generalised and individual job crafting groups showed comparable positive outcomes [@cohen2023].

**Peer support networks** (Wahl et al., 2018): A three-tiered programme (departmental peer supporters, trained peer support team, mental health experts) showed improved compassion satisfaction (Compassion Practice Instrument) but not statistically significant reductions in burnout. The small sample (n=20) and lack of control group limit causal inference.

**Professional coaching** (Dyrbye et al., 2019): In an RCT (n=88 physicians), the proportion with high emotional exhaustion decreased by 19.5% in the coaching group and increased by 9% in the control group. The intervention group also had significant improvements in quality of life and resilience (CD-RISC) [@cohen2023].

The key finding is that organisational interventions targeting the structural causes of stress (workload, job design) produce significant and sustained burnout reductions, yet they remain under-researched compared to individual-level interventions.

## Key Points

- Workload reduction: significant EE reductions sustained at 6-month follow-up
- Job crafting (3 weeks): significant improvements in work engagement and reduced EE
- Professional coaching (5 months): EE decreased 19.5% in intervention vs increased 9% in control
- Peer support: improved compassion satisfaction but non-significant burnout reduction
- Only 3/33 studies used organisational-level approaches — a critical research gap
