## Summary

At six months, the infant reaches a pivotal juncture. Weight averages 7.9 kg (17.4 lb) and length 67 cm (26.4 in), roughly double birth weight. Sitting independently, the emergence of stranger anxiety, and the introduction of complementary foods mark this as a major transition [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~7.9 kg (range 6.7–9.3 kg); median length ~67 cm. Birth weight should have doubled. Introduction of solid foods begins around this age alongside continued breastfeeding/formula feeding. Head circumference ~43 cm.

**Emotional Maturity:** Stranger anxiety emerges — the infant distinguishes familiar from unfamiliar people and may show wariness or distress. Knows familiar people from strangers. Likes to look at self in mirror. Responds to other people's emotions, often appearing joyful, and enjoys social play like peek-a-boo.

**Psychological Development:** Piaget's substage 3 (secondary circular reactions, 4–8 months). The infant purposefully repeats actions on objects that yield interesting results. Explores objects comprehensively — mouthing, shaking, banging, dropping. Looks for dropped objects — early object permanence. Shows curiosity about surroundings.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: sits with little or no support; rolls both ways; may begin to rock back and forth on hands and knees (pre-crawling); bears full weight on legs when held standing. Fine motor: reaches with one hand; transfers objects between hands; uses raking grasp; brings objects to mouth; bangs objects together; begins self-feeding with hands.

**Speech and Language Development:** Babbles using consonant strings ("bababa," "dadada"); responds to name; makes sounds to show pleasure and displeasure; responds to sounds by making sounds; strings vowels together when babbling. Follows sounds with eyes. Understands "no" by tone of voice [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: not sitting with support by 6.5 months; not rolling; does not bear weight on legs; does not reach for objects; no consonant babble; no response to name; no laughter or joyful expressions; persistent primitive reflexes (Moro, ATNR); poor head control; head circumference crossing percentiles downward; not showing interest in surroundings.
