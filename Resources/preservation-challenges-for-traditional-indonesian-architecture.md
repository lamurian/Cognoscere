Rumah adat numbers are declining, a trend from Dutch colonial demolition programmes that replaced traditional homes with Western buildings. Post-independence, the government promoted modern housing over traditional styles [@wikipediacontributors2025]. High labour costs and hardwood scarcity make traditional construction expensive.

Preservation faces challenges including loss of crafts knowledge, though vernacular architecture comprises over 75% of the world's buildings [@pardo2023]. Minangkabau preservation uses database integration with information technology [@noviarti2013]. Positive examples include Soekarno-Hatta Airport using Javanese pendopo concepts and resorts incorporating traditional roof forms over modern structures.
