## Summary

At ten months, the infant consolidates mobility and communication skills. Weight averages about 9.1 kg (20.1 lb) and length 73 cm (28.7 in). The infant now actively explores the environment, pulls to stand with confidence, and uses a growing repertoire of gestures and sounds to communicate intentionally [@misirliyan2023].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~9.1 kg (range 7.8–10.7 kg); median length ~73 cm. Weight gain continues at 60–90 g/week. Length gain ~1.2 cm/month. Head circumference ~45.5 cm.

**Emotional Maturity:** Shows definite preferences for people, toys, and foods. Separation anxiety may still be strong. Displays a range of emotions including jealousy, affection, and frustration. May wave bye-bye spontaneously. Seeks caregiver approval and attention. Beginning of social referencing — looks to caregiver's face for cues about how to respond to ambiguous situations.

**Psychological Development:** Piaget's substage 4. The infant can coordinate means and ends — pulls a string to obtain attached toy, removes barrier to reach desired object. Searches for objects hidden under one of two covers. Imitates gestures and sounds with increasing accuracy. Explores containers — puts objects in and takes them out.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: crawls rapidly; pulls to stand and lowers with control; cruises along furniture with increasing skill; may stand momentarily without support. Fine motor: pincer grasp refined; picks up small objects with thumb and forefinger; releases objects voluntarily (though still clumsy); puts objects into containers; points with index finger.

**Speech and Language Development:** Understands simple words in context ("no," "bye-bye," familiar object names); may have 1–2 words used with meaning; jabbers expressively with sentence-like patterns; responds to "where is (object/person)?" by looking; imitates new sounds; uses gestures more deliberately to communicate [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: not crawling or bottom-shuffling; not pulling to stand by 10.5 months; not using pincer grasp; no consonant-vowel babble; not responding to own name or simple words; no gestures (waving, pointing, reaching to be picked up); does not search for hidden objects; loss of previously acquired skills.
