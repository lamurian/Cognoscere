The simulated PSS-10 distribution (M=14.652, SD=4.303, range=[3.000, 27.000], skew=0.085, kurtosis=-0.040) sits close to but slightly above established population norms. General population PSS-10 means range from 12-14: US 1983 M=14.2 (SD=6.5), 2006 M=16.6 (SD=6.5), 2009 M=16.0 (SD=6.7) [@cohen2012]; Germany M=12.57 (SD=6.42) [@klein2016]; Sweden M~13.0 (SD=6.6) [@nordin2013]. The simulated mean of 14.65 is ~1-2.5 points above the typical 12-14 normative range, placing it at the lower end of moderate stress.

The simulated SD of 4.303 is 1.5-1.7x narrower than empirical SDs (6.4-7.3), compressing the range such that scores span [3, 27] rather than the full 0-40. The near-symmetric skew (0.085) differs from the typical empirical PSS-10 pattern of slight right-skew (more people at lower stress). Liu et al. [@liu2020] found item-level skew ranging from -0.32 to +0.51 in Chinese adolescents (N=1,574). The elevated mean and compressed SD suggest the simulated population averages slightly more stressed than typical general populations (closer to young adult or recession-era norms), while the reduced variance limits stress-driven behavioural differentiation between agents in coping choice and social interaction patterns.
