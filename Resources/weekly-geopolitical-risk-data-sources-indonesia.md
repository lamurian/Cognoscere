# Weekly Geopolitical Risk Data Sources for Indonesia

## Summary

Geopolitical risk data for Indonesia is available through an open-source daily index and through contextual analysis in weekly economic reports. These sources capture political and security dimensions that complement economic data.

The Indonesia Geopolitical Risk (GPR) Index is an open-source Python pipeline (https://github.com/billycemerson/indo-gpr-index) that scrapes five Indonesian news sources — Antara, Detik, Kompas, Tempo, and Tribunnews — daily at 08:00 WIB. It calculates GPR across five dimensions: war threat, peace threat, military buildup, war acts, and terror acts, using the Caldara & Iacoviello methodology: (keyword_match_count / total_articles) x 100. Results export to Google Sheets automatically. MacroMicro provides a monthly Indonesia-specific GPR since 1985 at https://en.macromicro.me/series/20425/geopolitical-risk-index-indonesia-since-1985 for historical baselines.

Weekly economic reports from PEFINDO and Samuel Sekuritas regularly cover geopolitical developments affecting Indonesia — US-China tensions, rupiah pressure from global monetary shifts, and domestic political risks — translating raw geopolitical signals into economic context.

## Key Points

- The Indonesia GPR Index provides a daily, automated, open-source measure of geopolitical risk
- Caldara & Iacoviello's GPR offers historical baselines since 1985 for context
- Weekly economic reports contextualize geopolitical developments for economic analysis
- No single source covers both economic and geopolitical dimensions — triangulate across sources
- All sources listed are freely accessible
