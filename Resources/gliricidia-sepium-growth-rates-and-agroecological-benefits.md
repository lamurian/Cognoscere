## Summary

Gliricidia sepium is a fast-growing leguminous tree (16-50 ft) with moderate growth rate from cuttings. Cuttings 20"+ (up to 8 ft for posts) root readily. In low rainfall (21 inches/year), establishment takes 7 months with longest branches reaching 40 inches. Hydrogel crystals improve establishment success by 69% [@ogoshi2023]. At 1000 plants/ha it achieves 89% nitrogen recovery efficiency [@figueiredo2023]. Its lignin-rich leaves decompose slowly, maintaining soil cover through dry season. Best uses: soil conservation (organic matter 2.87%, infiltration 0.95 cm/hr in Kupang trials outperforming Leucaena and Sesbania), green manure, high-protein fodder, firewood from pruning every 3 years, and cacao/coffee shade [@matheus2009; @ogoshi2023]. Plant at wet season onset from cuttings 20% of length deep. Ideal conditions: altitude 0-4000 ft, rainfall 25-200 inches/year, pH 5.0-8.5. Avoid where invasive potential exists (assessed invasive in Comoros, Singapore, Queensland). Needs regular pruning to prevent competition with adjacent crops. Symbiotic benefits: fixes atmospheric nitrogen (47 kg N/ha/year through litter), deep roots recycle leached nutrients, canopy reduces soil temperature and evaporation, bark contains rotenone used as rat poison, flowers attract pollinators [@bah2001].

Key sources: @ogoshi2023, @figueiredo2023, @matheus2009, @bah2001.
