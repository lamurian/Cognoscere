## Summary

Orna is a GPS pixel-art RPG with turn-based combat, class trees, raids, and deep RPG systems driven by walking. Its key GPS-based alternatives use real-world location as the game map, each making different tradeoffs in combat style and availability. SIGIL is the closest available GPS RPG alternative — fast action combat rather than turn-based, but equally deep crafting, hex, and faction systems with procedurally generated content at any location including rural areas. Dragon Quest Walk matches Orna's turn-based JRPG formula most faithfully (Square Enix production, turn-based combat, authentic monsters) but is region-locked to Japan with no official international release.

Monster Hunter Now offers action combat with gear crafting on Niantic's GPS map infrastructure — combat requires real-time dodging rather than Orna's turn-based approach, and progression is gated by pay-to-win at higher tiers. Ingress Prime provides the deepest territorial strategy among GPS games (portal linking, faction warfare) but has no traditional RPG progression. All GPS games drain 10-15% extra battery daily. No app matches Orna's exact combination of GPS-based turn-based combat with deep class trees, crafting, and raids — SIGIL comes closest in pure GPS RPG fun, Dragon Quest Walk in turn-based mechanics, and Orna itself remains the best turn-based GPS RPG available in English worldwide.
