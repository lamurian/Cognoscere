## Overview

Host.ID is an Indonesian hosting and VPS provider with over 10 years of operation. Their Cloud VPS line uses NVMe storage with a free backup image feature. User sentiment is limited — Host.ID has fewer independent reviews compared to larger competitors. Based on available information from their website, HowToHosting.Guide, and general hosting comparison sites.

## What Users Love

**Free backup image** is Host.ID's most differentiated feature. All plans include a backup image that users can restore via the VPS panel. This is unique among budget Indonesia VPS providers — most competitors charge extra for snapshots or offer provider-managed backups that users cannot access.

**NVMe storage at entry level.** The Lite 1.1 plan (Rp75,000/month) includes 20 GB NVMe storage. This is competitive with Nevacloud and DomaiNesia on storage technology.

**10 Gbps local network** with direct peering to Indonesian IXPs (OpenIXP, IIX-APJII, JKT-IX). Users serving Indonesian audiences benefit from low local latency.

**IPMI-level control** via the enduser VPS panel (power on/off, OS reinstall, VNC) gives users granular hardware-level control.

## The Major Pain Points

**1. Higher Price.** At Rp75,000/month for the entry plan, Host.ID is significantly more expensive than Nevacloud (Rp42,240), DomaiNesia (Rp48,000), Biznet Gio (Rp59,000), and Rumahweb (Rp50,000).

**2. TOS Restrictions on VPN/Proxy.** Host.ID's terms of service explicitly prohibit using VPS for VPN, proxy, tunnel, or bouncer purposes. This is a significant limitation for developers who need VPN access for remote work or secure tunnels.

**3. Lower SLA.** The published SLA is 99.5%, compared to 99.9% from DomaiNesia and Biznet Gio. 99.5% allows ~3.6x more downtime per year (43 min vs 8.7 hours).

**4. Limited International Bandwidth.** International bandwidth is capped at 200 Mbps shared, compared to 1 Gbps+ from competitors.

**5. Few Independent Reviews.** Host.ID has minimal presence on review platforms and tech blogs. Independent verification of their claims is limited.

## Verdict

Host.ID offers a compelling feature (free backup image) but at a premium price with restrictive TOS. The backup feature is genuinely valuable for users who prioritise disaster recovery. However, the Rp75,000 entry price, 99.5% SLA, and VPN restrictions make it difficult to recommend over cheaper, more flexible alternatives. Best suited for users who specifically need the built-in backup capability and do not require VPN access. For most users, DomaiNesia or Biznet Gio offer better value at lower or similar prices.
