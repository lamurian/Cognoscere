## Summary

The correlation between PSS-10 (perceived stress) and perceived social support (MSPSS) is consistently negative but small in magnitude. Reported Pearson r values range from -0.08 (non-significant) to -0.22, depending on the source of support.

Acoba @acoba2024 reported differential correlations by support source: PSS-10 with family support (r = -0.180, p < 0.01), with significant other support (r = -0.119, p < 0.05), and with friend support (r = -0.075, non-significant). Yang et al. @yang2020 found r = -0.223 (p < 0.01) between PSS-14 and overall MSPSS in a clinical sample.

These uniformly small negative correlations (r ≈ -0.08 to -0.22) indicate that perceived stress and perceived social support are weakly related constructs sharing at most 5% variance.

## Key Points
- PSS-10 and social support: r ≈ -0.08 to -0.22 (small negative)
- The correlation varies by support source (family/significant other strongest, friend weakest/non-significant)
- The asserted bound |r| < 0.6 is far wider than empirical evidence supports
- A more appropriate bound would be |r| < 0.3
- These values suggest distinct constructs rather than meaningful overlap
