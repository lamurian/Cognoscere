Indonesian traditional architecture embodies values beyond mere shelter. The house is a cosmic symbol linking earth and sky, reflecting social hierarchy and spiritual beliefs. The Joglo house uses a pyramid-like roof resembling a sacred mountain [@widayati2019].

Tidore vernacular architecture achieves a harmony between spiritual, human, and environmental realms [@rahim2023]. The house provides the focus for family and community life, binding villagers through customs, social relations, and traditional laws [@wikipediacontributors2025].
