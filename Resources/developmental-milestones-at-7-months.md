## Summary

At seven months, the infant becomes mobile through rolling and begins pre-crawling behaviours. Weight reaches roughly 8.2 kg (18.1 lb) and length 69 cm (27.2 in). The infant now explores objects with both hands, transfers items between hands, and deeply investigates cause-and-effect relationships by shaking, banging, and dropping [@misirliyan2023].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~8.2 kg (range 7.0–9.7 kg); median length ~69 cm. Weight gain continues at 80–120 g/week. Growth velocity decelerates compared to the first 6 months. Head circumference ~44 cm.

**Emotional Maturity:** Stranger anxiety intensifies — the infant may cling to caregiver when approached by unfamiliar people. Enjoys social play (peek-a-boo, pat-a-cake). Discriminates emotions in others' facial expressions and tone of voice. Shows clear preferences for familiar people and may protest separation.

**Psychological Development:** Deep in Piaget's substage 3 (secondary circular reactions). The infant purposefully manipulates objects to observe effects — drops toy repeatedly to watch caregiver pick it up. Searches briefly for a partially hidden object. Explores objects comprehensively — mouthing, shaking, banging, turning.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: sits without support; can pivot on stomach to reach objects; may begin creeping backwards; rocks on hands and knees; bears full weight on legs and may bounce. Fine motor: transfers objects smoothly between hands; uses radial-palmar grasp; bangs two objects held in hands together; feeds self a cracker or similar finger food.

**Speech and Language Development:** Babbles strings of consonants ("babababa"); uses voice to express joy and displeasure; responds to own name consistently; begins to recognise common words ("cup," "bye-bye"); turns to look at speaker when name is called; uses different consonant-vowel combinations [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: not sitting independently by 7.5 months; uses only one side of body; does not transfer objects; no consonant babble; does not turn toward sounds or voices; no emotional expression variety; stiff or very floppy tone; poor weight gain with crossing percentiles; no interest in peek-a-boo or interactive games.
