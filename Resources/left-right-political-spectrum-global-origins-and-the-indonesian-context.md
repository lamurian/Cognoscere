This is an **index note** that connects a set of atomic notes about the left–right political spectrum, its global variation, and its complex application to Indonesia. Each note below covers a distinct subtopic and is linked in a Zettelkasten network.

## The Notes

### 1. Origin & Core Philosophy
**[[political-left-right-spectrum-origin-and-core-philosophy]]**

The French Revolution origin of the left–right metaphor and the core distinction that holds across cultures: **equality vs. hierarchy**. Includes the key thinkers table and a clear explanation of why attitudes toward equality are the most consistent dividing line.

### 2. Global Variation
**[[global-variation-of-the-left-right-political-spectrum]]**

Why the same labels mean different things in different countries. Covers four factors: different historical foundations (Europe vs. US vs. post-colonial), religious vs. secular axes, post-authoritarian trauma, and the cross-cutting effect of 21st-century populism.

### 3. Indonesia: Erasure of Left-Wing Politics After 1965
**[[indonesia-erasure-of-left-wing-politics-after-1965]]**

The historical trauma that makes left–right discourse difficult in Indonesia: the 1965 mass killings, Suharto's New Order purge, criminalisation of the word *kiri*, and the lingering effects in the Reformasi era.

### 4. Indonesia: Pluralist vs. Islamist Spectrum
**[[indonesian-politics-pluralist-vs-islamist-spectrum]]**

The real political cleavage in contemporary Indonesia — not economic left vs. right, but **pluralist-nationalist vs. Islamist-conservative**. Includes party mapping, ISEAS survey data on Islamic populism (16.3% pro vs. 33.9% anti), and analysis of why the economic axis is weak.

### 5. Marhaenism (existing note)
**[[marhaenism-philosophy-history-and-political-significance]]**

Indonesia's indigenous left tradition: Sukarno's adaptation of Marxism for the *Marhaen* (small farmer/artisan). Already covers the philosophy, history, and contemporary legacy of this alternative framework.
