## Summary

This research confirms that biomimicry provides a scientifically grounded framework for designing off-grid urban homesteads. The evidence base — drawn from comprehensive reviews of 53+ academic papers, 184 documented strategies, and 25+ case studies — supports the conclusion that emulating natural systems enables urban buildings to achieve energy, water, and waste autonomy while regenerating local ecosystems [@verbrugghe2023; @borham2024; @pedersenzari2020]. WHY confidence: HIGH (3 peer-reviewed sources converge). Cities consume 70% of global resources and generate 75% of GHG emissions. Nature's 3.8-billion-year evolution of closed-loop systems solves exactly the challenges of off-grid living, enabling a shift from less-harmful to regenerative design.

HOW confidence: HIGH. Two design approaches (top-down and bottom-up) are well-established with frameworks like Design Spiral and BioGen. A relational database of 184 strategies across 9 city systems provides a practical toolkit [@borham2024]. Key strategies for off-grid autonomy include passive ventilation (S039), rainwater harvesting (S150), and the documented 'self-sustaining off-grid system' strategy (S184). Documented case studies show 50-65% energy reduction through passive biomimetic strategies alone, and water autonomy is achievable through distributed systems. Gaps remain: few residential-scale biomimetic projects documented, limited long-term performance data, and no standardised biomimetic certification yet exists.
