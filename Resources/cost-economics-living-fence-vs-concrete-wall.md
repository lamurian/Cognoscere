Concrete walls cost $63-100/linear foot for basic installations, rising to $275/ft for 7-8 foot walls [@naturalartfence2026; @thesitemath2025]. Living fences with layered fortification including gabion elements range from $25-50/ft installed [@brentwoodfence2025; @greengianttrees2025].

Lifecycle economics favor living systems even more. Concrete requires periodic repairs for cracking and rebar corrosion, costing 30-50% of initial construction within 30 years. Living fences, after 2-5 year establishment, require only annual pruning and inspection. Self-repair is biological and free.

Load-bearing reinforced soil walls with vegetation cut construction costs by up to 75% and halve build times vs traditional retaining walls [@tensarinternational2020]. Living fences generate revenue concrete cannot: carbon sequestration credits, fodder production (Gliricidia, Leucaena), fruit/nut harvests, and increased property values through aesthetics and microclimate [@ngongo2022].

## Key Points
- Living fence: $25-50/ft installed.
- Concrete wall: $63-275/ft installed (height-dependent).
- MSE walls: up to 75% cheaper than concrete.
- Living fences generate carbon credits, fodder, fruit; concrete only costs.
