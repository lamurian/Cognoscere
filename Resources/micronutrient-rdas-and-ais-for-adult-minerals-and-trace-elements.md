## Summary

The IOM has established RDAs or AIs for essential minerals. For adult men aged 19–70, key values include: calcium 1,000–1,200 mg AI, iron 8 mg, magnesium 400–420 mg, phosphorus 700 mg, zinc 11 mg, selenium 55 mcg, copper 900 mcg, iodine 150 mcg, chromium 35 mcg AI, manganese 2.3 mg AI, potassium 4,700 mg AI, and sodium under 2,300 mg [@instituteofmedicineuscommitteeonuseofdietaryreferenceintakesinnutritionlabeling2003]. For adult women aged 19–70: calcium 1,000–1,200 mg AI, iron 18 mg (dropping to 8 mg post-menopause), magnesium 310–320 mg, phosphorus 700 mg, zinc 8 mg, selenium 55 mcg, and potassium 4,700 mg AI. During pregnancy, iron requirements rise to 27 mg and zinc to 11–12 mg [@instituteofmedicineuscommitteeonuseofdietaryreferenceintakesinnutritionlabeling2003]. Mineral bioavailability is affected by dietary composition; phytates in plant-based diets reduce absorption of iron, zinc, and magnesium [@gombart2020]. The FDA Daily Values for minerals include calcium 1,300 mg, iron 18 mg, magnesium 420 mg, phosphorus 1,250 mg, zinc 11 mg, potassium 4,700 mg, and sodium 2,300 mg [@usfoodanddrugadministration2024].
