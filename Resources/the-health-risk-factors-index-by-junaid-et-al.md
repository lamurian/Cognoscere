Junaid et al. (2025) developed a Health Risk Factors Index (HRFI) for 100 countries stratified by Human Development Index groups over 2015-2019. The index covers three sub-domains derived from the WHO Global Reference List: Nutrition (stunting, wasting, overweight in children, anaemia), Environmental Risk Factors (safe water, sanitation, clean fuels, air pollution), and Non-Communicable Diseases (alcohol, tobacco, adult overweight). Data was sourced from WHO GHO, UNICEF, and UN SDG databases [@junaid2025].

The methodology follows the UNDP normalization approach (min-max with aspirational targets), PCA-based weighting to assign weights based on variance explained, and inverse normalized Euclidean distance for aggregation. Results showed very high HDI countries excel in nutrition and environmental domains but struggle with NCD risk factors. Singapore achieved the highest HRFI in both 2015 and 2019. A very strong positive correlation was found between HDI and the overall HRFI (rho=0.897, p<0.001) [@junaid2025].

The study demonstrates the feasibility of constructing a global health risk index from publicly available data, though data availability constraints forced exclusion of infections and injury domains. The authors recommend sensitivity analysis using alternative weighting schemes in future work [@junaid2025].
