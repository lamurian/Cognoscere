## Summary

At four and a half years, the child refines motor skills, deepens peer relationships, and expands cognitive abilities. Weight averages about 17.5 kg (38.6 lb) and height 106 cm (41.7 in). The child now skips with practice, stands on one foot for 8 seconds, draws recognisable figures, and understands basic time concepts — yesterday, today, tomorrow — reflecting maturing prefrontal and cerebellar coordination [@misirliyan2023].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~17.5 kg (range 14.8–20.6 kg); median height ~106 cm. Growth steady. Posture becomes more upright; spinal curves approach adult configuration. Physical activity level typically high.

**Emotional Maturity:** Develops real friendships — has a "best friend." Shows increased self-regulation — can wait for a turn, follow rules in group games. Understands and follows rules more consistently. May show protective behaviour toward younger children. Expresses feelings more verbally — can describe why they are upset. May lie or blame others when caught misbehaving (normal at this age as theory of mind and self-preservation develop). Awareness of gender roles may intensify.

**Psychological Development:** Preoperational stage. The child asks "why" constantly — genuine curiosity about how the world works. Understands sequences of events (first, then, last). Counts objects with one-to-one correspondence up to 10. Recognises some written numbers and letters. Draws a person with 4–6 body parts, a house, a tree. Understands analogies ("a shoe goes on a foot; a hat goes on a ____"). Longer attention span — can focus on an activity for 10–15 minutes. Understands past and future in concrete terms.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: skips on one foot with practice; stands on one foot for 5–8 seconds; gallops; catches a bounced ball reliably; walks along a line heel-to-toe for 4–5 steps; jumps forward with both feet 10–15 cm. Fine motor: copies a triangle and square; draws a person with 4–6 body parts; colours within lines with moderate success; cuts along a curved line with scissors; buttons and unbuttons; uses fork, spoon, and sometimes table knife with supervision; writes some letters of own name [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

**Speech and Language Development:** Speech fully intelligible; sentences of 5–7 words; tells long, detailed stories; uses future tense; asks meaning of unfamiliar words; vocabulary exceeds 1500 words; understands quantity concepts (more, less, same, different); uses comparatives and superlatives (-er, -est); can define simple words ("what is a ball?"); retells stories with key details; enjoys jokes and riddles (though may not understand them); may still have difficulty with l, r, s, v, th sounds [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: cannot hop; cannot stand on one foot for 2 seconds; cannot catch a large ball; speech unintelligible to strangers; cannot use sentences of 4+ words; does not interact with peers; does not engage in pretend play; cannot follow two-step commands; not interested in drawing or colouring; extreme shyness or aggression; flat affect; cannot dress self independently; cannot name colours; loss of previously acquired skills.
