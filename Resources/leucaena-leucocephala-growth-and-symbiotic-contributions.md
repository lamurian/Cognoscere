## Summary

Leucaena leucocephala achieves DBH of 11.3-11.8 cm and height 9.3-9.9 m after 17 years in valley savanna with only 584 mm annual rainfall [@liu2018]. Its taproot penetrates up to 8 m accessing subsoil moisture [@ngongo2022]. Above-ground biomass averages 30.5-33.6 kg/tree in monoculture, root-to-shoot ratio 0.26-0.28, with most roots in top 40 cm. The psyllid-resistant cv. Tarramba is successfully adopted in Amarasi, West Timor. Best uses: high-protein fodder (25% protein) for cattle, soil conservation through deep root anchoring, nitrogen-rich green manure, integrated cattle feeding systems. Plant at wet season from scarified seed or seedlings. Spacing 1.5-3m depending on use. Thrives in well-drained soils pH 5.0-8.0. Use cv. Tarramba for psyllid resistance. Avoid near Eucalyptus -- mixed planting significantly reduces growth (DBH drops to 8.7-9.1 cm) due to water competition [@liu2018]. Can become invasive. Needs regular pruning to prevent shading of adjacent crops. Symbiotic benefits: substantial nitrogen fixation via Rhizobium, when stressed allocates more biomass to roots (BGB/AGB 0.36-0.39), dense root network at 0-40 cm stabilizes soil, flowers support bee populations. In East Timor it is one of five legumes used simultaneously for food, fodder, medicine, and domestic purposes [@guterres2022].

Key sources: @liu2018, @ngongo2022, @guterres2022.
