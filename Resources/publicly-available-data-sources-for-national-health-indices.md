Several publicly accessible global data repositories provide the indicators needed to construct national health indices. The WHO Global Health Observatory (GHO) is the primary source, offering data on mortality, morbidity, risk factors, health systems, and SDG indicators across 194 member states. Data is accessible through an OData API, country profiles, and downloadable datasets covering life expectancy, healthy life expectancy (HALE), cause-specific mortality, and NCD prevalence [@junaid2025].

The IHME Global Health Data Exchange (GHDx) provides the Global Burden of Disease (GBD) results, including incidence, prevalence, mortality, YLLs, YLDs, and DALYs for 354 diseases across 195 countries. Data is accessible through the GBD Results Tool and visualized via interactive dashboards. GBD also produces healthy life expectancy (HALE) estimates [@junaid2025].

OECD Health Statistics offers the most comprehensive comparable data across 38 OECD member countries, including health expenditure, health system performance, and risk factors. The World Bank Development Indicators provide supplementary data on health expenditure, immunization, and nutrition. The UNDP Human Development Report provides the HDI which includes a health dimension (life expectancy). National health surveys (e.g., NHANES for the US, EHIS for Europe) offer detailed subnational data [@tolonen2021].
