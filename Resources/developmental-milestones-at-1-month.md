## Summary

At one month, the newborn transitions from purely reflexive behavior to early social engagement. Physical growth is rapid: the average infant weighs approximately 4.3 kg (9.5 lb) and measures 54 cm (21.3 in), having regained birth weight by day 10–14. Weight gain proceeds at roughly 150–200 g per week [@worldhealthorganization2006].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~4.3 kg (range 3.4–5.3 kg); median length ~54 cm. Weekly weight gain of 150–200 g is expected. Failure to regain birth weight by 2 weeks signals possible feeding difficulties, metabolic disorders, or cardiac anomalies.

**Emotional Maturity:** The infant communicates distress through differentiated crying (hunger vs. discomfort). Begins to quiet when picked up or spoken to. Shows preference for human faces over objects — a foundational attachment behaviour [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

**Psychological Development:** Predominantly in Piaget's sensorimotor substage 1 (reflexive schemes, 0–1 month). The infant habituates to repeated stimuli and shows a primitive awareness of contingency (sucking pattern changes in response to sound).

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: lifts head briefly when prone; demonstrates head lag when pulled to sit. Fine motor: hands predominantly fisted; exhibits grasp reflex. Movements are largely reflexive (Moro, rooting, palmar grasp).

**Speech and Language Development:** Cries are the primary communication. Produces throaty, reflexive sounds. Startles or quiets to loud sounds. Discriminates mother's voice from others' [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Weight below 3rd percentile on WHO charts; failure to regain birth weight by 14 days; persistent fisting beyond 1 month; no response to loud sounds; excessive lethargy or irritability; absent visual fixation on faces; poor suck with inadequate intake.
