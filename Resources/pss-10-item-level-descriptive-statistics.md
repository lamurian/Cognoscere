The PSS-10 (Cohen, 1988) measures global perceived stress over the past month. Each item is rated on a 5-point Likert scale from 0 (never) to 4 (very often). Four positively-worded items (4, 5, 7, 8) are reverse-scored. Total scores range 0--40.

## Item-Level Descriptive Statistics

The most detailed item-level data come from Liu et al. (2020) [@liu2020] in Chinese adolescents (N = 1,574, age 13--17). Their study used a 1--5 scale (1 = never, 5 = always). The table below converts their values to the standard 0--4 scale (subtract 1 from each mean) for comparability.

| Item | Short Label | M (0--4) | SD |
|------|------------|----------|-----|
| 1 | Been upset because of unexpected events | 1.98 | 0.89 |
| 2 | Unable to control important things | 1.92 | 0.89 |
| 3 | Felt nervous and stressed | 2.10 | 0.93 |
| 4 | Felt confident about handling problems (R) | 1.82 | 0.92 |
| 5 | Felt things were going your way (R) | 2.09 | 0.80 |
| 6 | Could not cope with all the things | 1.94 | 0.78 |
| 7 | Able to control irritations (R) | 1.99 | 0.78 |
| 8 | Felt on top of things (R) | 2.22 | 0.88 |
| 9 | Been angered by things outside control | 1.59 | 0.91 |
| 10 | Difficulties piling up could not overcome | 1.82 | 0.93 |

Note: (R) = reverse-scored items. In the Liu et al. study, items 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 10 form the Perceived Helplessness factor; items 4, 5, 7, 8 form the Perceived Self-Efficacy factor.

## Total Score Normative Data

PSS-10 total score means and SDs from selected large-sample studies:

| Study | Population | N | M | SD |
|-------|-----------|-----|-----|-----|
| Cohen & Janicki-Deverts (2012) [@cohen2012] | US national (1983) | 2,387 | 14.2 | 6.5 |
| Cohen & Janicki-Deverts (2012) [@cohen2012] | US national (2006) | 1,997 | 16.6 | 6.5 |
| Cohen & Janicki-Deverts (2012) [@cohen2012] | US national (2009) | 1,000 | 16.0 | 6.7 |
| González-Ramírez et al. (2013) [@gonzlezramrez2013] | Mexico general | 1,990 | 14.5--17.7 | varies by group |
| Lu et al. (2017) [@lu2017] | Chinese university | 1,096 | 13.7 | 5.6 |
| Liu et al. (2020) [@liu2020] | Chinese adolescents | 1,574 | ~20.5 (1--5 scale) | ~5.2 (note: 1--5 scale) |

Note: Liu et al. used a 1--5 scale (range 10--50). Their total mean on the 1--5 scale corresponds to approximately 10.5 on a 0--40 scale (converted approximately by subtracting 10).
