Pleaching (or plashing) interweaves living and dead branches to form dense, impenetrable hedges and barriers. Julius Caesar recorded the Gallic Nervii tribe using pleached barriers against Roman cavalry around 60 BCE [@wikipediacontributors2025b]. The modern diamond lattice (fedge) method uses 1-year willow rods at least 6 feet long, inserted at 45-degree angles into prepared soil, spaced 10 inches apart in opposite-angled pairs. Intersections are bound with gardening tape or twine, checked periodically as stems thicken. Willow fedges reach full form in 2-3 years with 2-3 pruning sessions per year. Suitable species include willow (fastest), hazel, alder, and poplar [@morningchores2025].
