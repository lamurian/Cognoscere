## Summary

Moringa oleifera (kelor) is one of the fastest-growing living fence species, especially in its first year. It handles dry seasons well and establishes from both seeds and large cuttings. AVRDC recommends spacing of 1m or closer for living fence posts, with trees pruned at head height to encourage bushy growth and leaf harvest [@martin2012]. Cuttings grow straight, ideal for palisade-style fences. Leaves contain 16-17% crude protein and are rich in vitamins A, C, calcium, and iron. Best uses: continuous harvest of nutritious leaves for human consumption and animal feed; seeds yield oil (ben oil) for cooking and cosmetics; pressed seed cake serves as water purifier. Plant from cuttings 2m long, 4-6cm diameter at wet season onset. Thrives in well-drained sandy loam with full sun. Cut back to 1-1.5m height for bushy growth. Avoid in waterlogged or heavy clay soils. Susceptible to termites. Soft wood means shorter post lifespan. Cannot tolerate frost. Needs regular harvesting to maintain shape. Symbiotic benefits: deep taproot improves aeration and water infiltration, rapid leaf litter decomposition enriches soil, flowers attract pollinators. Though not a nitrogen-fixer, high-nutrient leaf drop significantly boosts soil fertility along the fence line. Used in traditional Timorese medicine [@guterres2022].

Key sources: @martin2012, @guterres2022.
