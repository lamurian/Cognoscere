Local food systems offer an instructive model for company-community symbiosis. Research on three Norwegian rural municipalities shows local food systems are deeply linked to geographical location, identity, social relations, and community pride [@westskog2024]. Exchange is motivated by mutual moral obligations and value-driven preferences rather than purely transactional logic. Local food creates a sense of \"not only for somewhere, but from here\" — adding meaning and accountability to economic exchange. Non-conventional channels (farmers markets, CSAs, direct sales) enable the social relationships underpinning symbiotic exchange. Barriers include affordability and availability: symbiosis must be inclusive to be sustainable. Context-dependent development is necessary — each community requires tailored approaches. The case demonstrates symbiosis works best when the business is rooted in place, exchange has social meaning beyond price, and governance includes community voice [@westskog2024].
