## Summary

At thirty months (2.5 years) — the third AAP-recommended developmental screening point — the child demonstrates marked advances in language, social awareness, and motor coordination. Weight averages about 13.5 kg (29.8 lb) and height 93 cm (36.6 in). The child now uses 200+ words, speaks in short sentences, and shows clear interest in peers [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~13.5 kg (range 11.7–15.8 kg); median height ~93 cm. Growth steady: weight gain ~2–3 kg/year, height ~7–8 cm/year. Body proportions now distinctly child-like rather than toddler-like — limbs elongated, baby fat diminishing.

**Emotional Maturity:** Plays near other children and sometimes with them. Shows concern for a crying friend. Shows a wide range of emotions. May get upset with major changes in routine. Separates from caregiver more easily. Shows defiant behaviour ("no" remains common). Beginning to understand rules and consequences — though compliance remains inconsistent. May exhibit fears (dark, monsters, loud noises).

**Psychological Development:** Preoperational stage: symbolic thinking expands. Engages in elaborate make-believe play — assigns roles to dolls, acts out scenarios. Names items in a picture book (cat, bird, dog). Begins to understand the concept of "two." Completes 4–6 piece puzzles. Understands prepositions (in, on, under). Matches shapes and colours. Memory for routines and past events improving.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: runs smoothly; jumps off the ground with both feet; climbs well on playground equipment; walks up and down stairs alternating feet with rail; throws ball overhand 5–7 feet; kicks ball with accuracy; may begin to pedal tricycle. Fine motor: builds tower of 6–8 blocks; turns pages one at a time; holds crayon with fingers (not fist); copies vertical line and circle; feeds self without assistance; helps dress and undress [@misirliyan2023].

**Speech and Language Development:** Uses 200–400+ words; speaks in 3–4 word sentences; speech 75% intelligible to strangers; uses pronouns correctly most of the time; asks "what," "where," and "why" questions; uses plurals and past tense with growing accuracy; names colours; tells simple stories; understands "one" and "all"; uses k, g, t, d, n, f sounds consistently [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: not running smoothly; not jumping; fewer than 50 words; not using sentences of 3+ words; speech mostly unintelligible; no pretend play; not interacting with other children; no eye contact; not following two-step commands; loss of previously acquired skills; drooling persistently; extreme difficulty separating from parent. This is a formal AAP developmental screening age.
