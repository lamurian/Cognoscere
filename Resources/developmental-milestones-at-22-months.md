## Summary

At twenty-two months, the toddler approaches the two-year mark with rapidly expanding language and social skills. Weight reaches approximately 11.8 kg (26.0 lb) and length 86.5 cm (34.1 in). The toddler now strings multiple pretend play actions together, uses two-word phrases routinely, and negotiates the world with growing competence [@misirliyan2023].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~11.8 kg (range 10.2–13.7 kg); median length ~86.5 cm. Weight gain ~30–50 g/week. Head circumference near adult proportion relative to body.

**Emotional Maturity:** Shows wider emotional vocabulary — can name "happy," "sad," "mad" with help. Tantrums may still be frequent as language catches up to emotions. Shows empathy more reliably. Developing sense of ownership. Shows pride when praised. May exhibit fears (dark, loud noises, animals). Self-regulation improving with caregiver support.

**Psychological Development:** Late substage 6: the toddler uses symbols mentally to represent objects and events. Pretend play becomes narrative — acts out stories with dolls or figurines. Matches shapes and colours. Can follow two-step unrelated commands. Knows where things belong. Understands "soon" and "after nap." Shows interest in potty training readiness.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: runs well with few falls; kicks ball forward with intent; walks up and down stairs with rail (both feet per step); jumps in place with both feet leaving ground briefly; climbs on playground equipment; stands on tiptoes. Fine motor: builds tower of 5–6 blocks; scribbles with circular and vertical strokes; uses fork and spoon; turns doorknobs; unzips large zippers.

**Speech and Language Development:** Uses 30–50+ words; combines 2–3 words; speech 50% intelligible to strangers; uses pronouns (me, mine, you) inconsistently; asks "what's that?" frequently; names body parts and objects; follows two-step commands; begins using plurals and past tense experimentally [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: fewer than 15 words; not combining two words; not walking well; no pretend play; not following simple directions; no eye contact; not interested in other children; not responding to name; loss of previously acquired language; cannot identify body parts; no imitative play.
