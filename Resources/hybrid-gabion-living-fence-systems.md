Living gabion walls combine stone-filled wire cages with living plants for perimeter security barriers. Galvanized double-twisted steel mesh (75x75mm) filled with angular stone (40-80mm) provides immediate mass and impact resistance, while plant roots reinforce soil cohesion. The modular system stacks from 0.5 to 3+ meters with 30+ year lifespan [@tywiremeshco2025].

Gabion walls are inherently flexible, dissipating seismic energy better than rigid concrete -- a key advantage in earthquake zones. Their porous structure allows water passage, eliminating hydrostatic pressure buildup that causes concrete wall failure in wet conditions.

Load-bearing reinforced soil walls with vegetation (MSE walls) cut costs by up to 75% and halve build times vs concrete retaining walls [@tensarinternational2020]. When combined with pleaching and inosculation on the surface, the system provides both immediate structural strength and long-term biological reinforcement.

## Key Points
- Gabion: immediate mass + impact resistance; roots: long-term reinforcement.
- 30+ year lifespan; earthquake-flexible vs rigid concrete.
- Self-draining; no hydrostatic pressure failure.
- MSE walls: up to 75% cheaper than concrete.
