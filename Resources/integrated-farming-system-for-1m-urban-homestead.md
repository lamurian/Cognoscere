For an urban homestead with very limited space (even 1m² balcony or patio), the best practice for integrated farming combines vertical stacking, water-efficient drip irrigation, capacitive soil moisture sensing, and closed-loop nutrient cycling via vermicomposting. This system functions as a miniature ecosystem: food production, nutrient cycling, water harvesting, and microclimate regulation within a single square meter [@pedersenzari2020; @borham2024].

**Core structure:** A 1m x 1m raised bed (30-40 cm deep) or 100L fabric pot as base. A fast-growing Moringa oleifera or Gliricidia sepium cutting at the back serves as living trellis, shade provider, and leaf harvest for food/mulch [@martin2012; @guterres2022]. Climbing beans (nitrogen-fixing) on a trellis, low-growing herbs around the base, and trailing strawberries at the edges create a productive polyculture using vertical space.

**Water and waste management:** A 20-50L rain barrel feeds gravity drip irrigation. An ESP32 with capacitive soil moisture sensor triggers irrigation only when dry, saving water [@tao2024]. A stacking vermicompost bin processes kitchen scraps into worm castings, closing the nutrient loop without outdoor compost space. A TTGO LoRa32 board with BME280 sensor tracks temperature/humidity/pressure, transmitting via LoRa to a Meshtastic gateway for off-grid monitoring [@santos2020; @meshtasticproject2026].

**Key benefits:** Vertical stacking triples growing area [@borham2024]. Smart irrigation saves water via solar-powered ESP32 [@tao2024]. Closed-loop fertility from worm bins and Moringa leaf mulch [@guterres2022]. Off-grid monitoring via LoRa — no WiFi/cloud needed [@santos2020; @meshtasticproject2026]. Polyculture of beans (N-fixers), Moringa (deep taproot, high-N mulch), and herbs (pest repellent) forms a self-supporting community [@ogoshi2023]. This applies ecosystem-level biomimicry following Life's Principles: use sunlight, recycle resources, demand local expertise [@verbrugghe2023].
