## Summary

The Martin-Key et al. (2022) systematic review identified two digital assessment tools directly based on MINI criteria: the electronic Psychological Assessment Screening System (e-PASS) and the Eating Disorder Questionnaire–Online (EDQ-O). Both were validated against MINI-Plus or clinical interview.

## Key Points

### e-PASS (Nguyen et al., 2017)
- Adaptive web-based system based on DSM-IV-TR criteria; >540 items covering 21 disorders
- Australian government-funded; free online with e-therapist support option
- Validated against MINI-Plus 5.0, CIDI, and ADIS-IV (N=616, interviewed=158)
- Mean 10.5 days between e-PASS and reference standard

#### e-PASS Diagnostic Accuracy
| Disorder | Sensitivity | Specificity |
|---|---|---|
| MDD | 0.86 | 0.79 |
| GAD | 0.78 | 0.68 |
| Social phobia | 0.60 | 0.90 |
| Panic disorder | 0.71 | 0.91 |
| PTSD | 0.75 | 0.92 |
| OCD | 0.75 | 0.92 |
| BN | 0.50 | 0.97 |
| AUD | 0.42 | 1.00 |

- Variable accuracy across conditions; good specificity but moderate sensitivity for most
- Adaptive design reduces burden but may miss comorbid presentations

### EDQ-O (Ter Huurne et al., 2017)
- 26-item online questionnaire based on MINI-Plus and DSM-IV-TR eating disorder criteria
- Validated against clinical interview (N=134, mean 9 days interval)

#### EDQ-O Diagnostic Accuracy
| Disorder | Sensitivity | Specificity | AUC |
|---|---|---|---|
| Anorexia nervosa | 0.44 | 1.00 | 0.72 |
| Bulimia nervosa | 0.78 | 0.88 | 0.83 |
| Binge eating disorder | 0.66 | 0.98 | 0.82 |
| EDNOS | 0.87 | 0.72 | 0.79 |

- Fair to good discrimination for BN, BED, EDNOS; poor for AN
- High specificity across all eating disorder categories
