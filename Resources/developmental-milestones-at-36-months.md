## Summary

At thirty-six months — the third birthday — the child has entered the preschool period with competence across all developmental domains. Weight averages about 14.5 kg (32.0 lb) and height 96 cm (37.8 in). The three-year-old speaks in sentences, plays cooperatively with peers, dresses with help, pedals a tricycle, and copies a circle — skills that reflect integration of motor, cognitive, language, and social-emotional growth [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~14.5 kg (range 12.6–16.9 kg); median height ~96 cm. Weight gain ~2 kg/year; height ~7 cm/year. Body fat reaches a lifetime low ("preschool lean period"). Head circumference ~49.5 cm. Growth now steady and predictable.

**Emotional Maturity:** Takes turns in games. Shows concern for a crying friend. Shows a wide range of emotions. Separates easily from parents. May get upset with major changes in routine. Dresses and undresses self with minimal help. Copies adults and friends. Understands the concept of "mine" and "his/hers." Shows affection without prompting [@misirliyan2023].

**Psychological Development:** Preoperational stage continuing. Engages in elaborate fantasy play — may have imaginary friends. Draws a person with 2–4 body parts (head, legs). Understands the concept of counting (may count to 5 or 10 by rote). Names 3–4 colours. Follows three-step commands. Understands "same" and "different." Completes puzzles of 6–8 pieces. Knows own name, age, and gender.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: runs easily; climbs well; pedals tricycle; walks up and down stairs alternating feet; jumps with both feet off ground; balances on one foot for 2–3 seconds; catches a large ball against chest. Fine motor: builds tower of 9–10 blocks; copies a circle and cross; holds pencil with fingers (tripod grasp developing); turns pages one at a time; screws and unscrews jar lids; dresses with help for buttons and zippers; uses toilet during the day (most children) [@misirliyan2023].

**Speech and Language Development:** Uses 500–1000+ words; speaks in 3–5 word sentences; speech 75–80% intelligible to strangers; uses "I," "you," "me" correctly; tells stories; says name, age, and gender; names most familiar things; uses plurals (-s), past tense (-ed), and prepositions; asks questions frequently; understands concepts like "on," "in," "under" [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: not running or climbing; not jumping; persistent toe-walking; drooling; not speaking in sentences of 3+ words; speech unintelligible to family; not engaging in pretend play; no interest in other children; no eye contact; not following two-step commands; loss of previously acquired skills; cannot identify body parts or common objects; not showing a range of emotions; extreme tantrums beyond developmental norms.
