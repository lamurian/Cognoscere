Living willow fences (fedges) require two types of annual pruning. In the dormant season (late October to March), after leaves have fallen, cut back all new vertical growth from the previous year, leaving only the original lattice framework [@rachelthegardener2017; @deesidewillow2024]. This hard pruning maintains the visible trellis structure in winter and prevents individual wands from becoming trees. Cut just above a bud on the framework using sharp secateurs [@deesidewillow2024].

During the growing season (March-September), when new shoots reach sufficient length, weave them diagonally into the existing lattice at low angles to produce a denser structure [@deesidewillow2024]. Alternatively, trim back excessive growth to maintain the desired shape. In the first growing season, keep trimming to a minimum to allow root establishment. Once the fedge is established (2-3 years), simple pruning in both seasons suffices instead of fully weaving new growth [@deesidewillow2024]. The three key rules are: always cut just above a bud, never leave long stubs, and maintain the lattice as the permanent framework [@rachelthegardener2017].
