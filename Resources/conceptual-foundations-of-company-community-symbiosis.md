Company-community symbiosis describes a relationship where a business and its surrounding town or village mutually benefit from each other's growth and wellbeing. The term derives from the Greek \"living together\" and in this context implies exchange of resources that nourish both the company's operations and the community's vitality [@cuevasvalenzuela2022]. The concept synthesizes three traditions: industrial symbiosis (resource exchanges between firms, extended to social dimensions), social embeddedness theory (economic action shaped by social relations), and Creating Shared Value (corporate competitiveness and community wellbeing are interdependent) [@cuevasvalenzuela2022; @cyr2022; @garczarekbk2026]. Three perspectives govern organizational social relations: economic (relations as compromises), organizational (relations as assets), and interactionist (relations as fundamental drivers). The interactionist perspective is most relevant for company-community symbiosis because it treats economic activities and social relations as indissociable [@cyr2022].
