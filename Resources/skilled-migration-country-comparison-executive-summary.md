## Summary

This executive summary compares five countries that actively welcome skilled migrant workers with strong education and healthcare systems: Canada, Australia, Germany, the Netherlands, and Ireland. All five offer free public education for children, universal or near-universal healthcare, and structured immigration pathways for highly skilled professionals in data science and digital health [@pandey2026].

Germany ranks highest for affordability with the lowest cost of living relative to salary, while the Netherlands offers the most favourable tax treatment for skilled migrants through its 30% ruling. Canada and Australia are the most established English-speaking destinations with points-based immigration systems. Ireland provides the fastest path to permanent residency (2 years via Critical Skills Employment Permit) within the EU.

For a professional with expertise in data science, HL7 FHIR, OpenEHR, epidemiology, and psychology, all five countries have active job markets in digital health transformation, healthcare interoperability, and health data science. Median PhD-level data scientist salaries range from EUR 65,000-95,000 in Europe to CAD 100,000-160,000 in Canada and AUD 110,000-175,000 in Australia [@codelabsacademy2025].
