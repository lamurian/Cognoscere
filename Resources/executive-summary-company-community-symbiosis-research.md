This research investigated what determines symbiotic company-community relationships and whether this applies across industries. Evidence reveals symbiosis is real but ambivalent. Geographic rootedness, value chain position, governance structures, and pre-existing social capital are key determinants [@cuevasvalenzuela2022; @cyr2022; @garczarekbk2026]. CSV and social capital form a bidirectional virtuous cycle [@garczarekbk2026]. Symbiosis is most pronounced in geographically tethered industries (extractives, agriculture, manufacturing, tourism) but achievable in any sector through intentional relational practices [@cuevasvalenzuela2022; @westskog2024; @cyr2022]. Extractives show the most ambivalent patterns — high impact paired with conflict. Symbiosis can be asymmetric and extractive; Global value chains create uneven development where local communities bear costs while value accrues elsewhere [@cuevasvalenzuela2022]. Confidence: HIGH for the bidirectional CSV-social capital relationship and industry differences (multiple peer-reviewed sources). MODERATE for measurement frameworks (limited to social capital and SLO approaches).
