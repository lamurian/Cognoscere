## Summary

Evaluating the impact of health system reforms requires a multi-dimensional indicator framework spanning health outcomes, system performance, equity, and wellbeing. Several established frameworks provide validated measures.

**Health outcome indicators**: Life expectancy at birth, infant mortality rate, and the UHC service coverage index are the most commonly used population health metrics [@arhin2022]. The UHC index, combining 14 tracer indicators of health service coverage with financial risk protection, is increasingly preferred as it better reflects health system performance than mortality alone.

**Primary care performance indicators**: The Primary Care Assessment Tool (PCAT) operationalizes the 4Cs framework (first contact, continuity, comprehensiveness, coordination) into measurable indicators. The Commonwealth Fund surveys track nationally comparable metrics including: percentage of adults with a regular doctor, percentage with 5+ year provider relationships, after-hours care availability, home visit rates, and care coordination adequacy [@gumas2024; @fitzgerald2022].

**Workforce indicators**: Primary care physician-to-population ratio, specialist-to-generalist wage gap, and primary care training pipeline metrics track system sustainability. The US national primary care scorecard tracks these across states, showing widening gaps between students choosing primary care and those choosing specialty careers [@krist2025].

**Equity indicators**: Disaggregated outcomes by income, race, geography, and insurance status reveal whether reforms benefit disadvantaged populations. The US scorecard shows 7,700+ primary care shortage areas, with 40% of counties lacking adequate access [@krist2025].

**Wellbeing indicators**: Beyond traditional health metrics, patient-reported outcomes, life satisfaction measures, and social determinants tracking (food security, housing stability) capture broader wellbeing impacts that health system reforms should influence.

## Key Points

- UHC index is the preferred composite measure of health system coverage and financial protection
- PCAT and Commonwealth Fund surveys operationalize the 4Cs into nationally comparable metrics
- Workforce indicators (PCP-to-population ratio, wage gap) track system sustainability
- Equity-disaggregated outcomes are essential for evaluating reform impact on disparities

## Confidence: Moderate (well-established indicators for health outcomes; less mature for wellbeing)
