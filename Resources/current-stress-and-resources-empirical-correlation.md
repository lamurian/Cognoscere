## Summary

The correlation between current stress (state/acute stress measures) and resources (primarily perceived social support) is consistently negative but small, ranging from approximately r = -0.08 to r = -0.25.

This relationship mirrors the PSS-10 ↔ resources association but tends to be slightly weaker because current stress captures transient fluctuations less tied to stable resource perceptions. The transactional stress model predicts a bidirectional relationship: low resources increase stress appraisals, and high stress depletes perceived resource availability.

Empirical evidence from the PSS-10 validation literature [@schneider2020] and mediation studies [@acoba2024] suggests that social support and stress correlate negatively at r ≈ -0.10 to -0.25. The ad-hoc entry in the test suite asserts a range of [-0.9, 0.1] for current_stress ↔ resources, which is excessively wide on the negative end. No published study reports a correlation approaching r = -0.9 between these constructs.

## Key Points
- Current stress and social support: r ≈ -0.08 to -0.25 (small negative)
- The ad-hoc bound [-0.9, 0.1] is far wider than empirical evidence supports
- A more empirically grounded bound would be [-0.3, 0.1]
- The near-zero upper bound (0.1) reflects that the relationship is consistently negative or near-zero, never meaningfully positive
