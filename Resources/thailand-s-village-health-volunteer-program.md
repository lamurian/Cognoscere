## Summary

Thailand's Village Health Volunteer (VHV) program is one of the largest community health worker initiatives globally, with over 1 million volunteers serving nationwide [@narkvichien2020]. Established as part of Thailand's primary health care policy in 1977, the program has been crucial to the country's ability to achieve universal health coverage despite having only 0.8 doctors per 1,000 population.

Each VHV is responsible for approximately 10-15 households in their community. They receive 43 hours of initial classroom training covering comprehensive health management for different age groups, infectious disease surveillance and control, health promotion, mental health, and consumer protection, followed by regular follow-up training sessions [@pongpirul2026]. The program is managed by the Primary Health Care Division of the Department of Health Service Support under the Ministry of Public Health, with supervision from local health workers [@narkvichien2020].

VHVs undertake health surveys, collect data, maintain family health records, conduct disease prevention campaigns, and support public health authorities. During outbreaks, their meticulous community records enable effective contact tracing and health monitoring [@narkvichien2020]. Each volunteer receives a monthly stipend of 1,000 Thai baht (~US $32), supplemented by social recognition, sponsored activities, and national awards [@narkvichien2020]. The volunteer force is predominantly women, who are selected by their communities for being public-minded and willing to help.

## Key Points

- Thailand has 1.04 million VHVs overseen by the Ministry of Public Health, including 15,000 in Bangkok
- Each VHV covers 10-15 households with 43 hours of initial training
- VHVs handle infectious disease surveillance, health promotion, chronic disease management, and data collection
- During COVID-19, VHVs visited 3.3 million households in March 2020 and 8 million more in subsequent weeks
- Thailand's under-5 mortality reduction is partially attributed to VHV efforts [@pongpirul2026]
- The program is one of the key mechanisms enabling UHC at low doctor density
