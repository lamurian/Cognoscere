## Summary

The Acceptable Macronutrient Distribution Range (AMDR) is a set of reference values for energy-containing macronutrients established by the Institute of Medicine in 2002/2005. These ranges are designed to ensure adequate intake of essential nutrients while reducing chronic disease risk. For adults, the AMDRs are: carbohydrate 45–65%, fat 20–35%, and protein 10–35% of total energy intake [@nationalacademiesofsciences2024].

Within total fat, n-6 polyunsaturated fatty acids (linoleic acid) should contribute 5–10% of energy, and n-3 polyunsaturated fatty acids (alpha-linolenic acid) 0.6–1.2%. The RDA for protein is 0.8 g per kg body weight daily. The RDA for carbohydrate is 130 g/day for both sexes, based on the brain's glucose requirements. Intakes above the AMDR are associated with increased chronic disease risk; intakes below increase risk of nutrient insufficiency [@instituteofmedicineuscommitteeonuseofdietaryreferenceintakesinnutritionlabeling2003]. The FDA translates these into Daily Values based on a 2,000-calorie reference diet: total fat 78 g, carbohydrate 275 g, protein 50 g, and dietary fibre 28 g [@usfoodanddrugadministration2024].
