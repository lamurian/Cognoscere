## Economic Growth Comparison: Soeharto vs Prabowo Indonesia

### Soeharto Era (1966-1998)

Soeharto's New Order presided over 32 years of economic transformation. After hyperinflation of 1,000% under Sukarno, the New Order brought inflation down to 19% by 1969 through the 'Berkeley Mafia' economic team. The economy grew at an average of over 7% per year from 1968 to 1981, driven by oil boom revenues after the 1973 oil crisis. Growth slowed to ~4.5% in 1981-1988 due to falling oil prices, then accelerated again to over 7% from 1989 to 1997 following financial sector deregulation (PAKTO 88) and manufacturing export growth. GDP per capita (PPP) rose from US$806 in 1966 to US$4,114 in 1997, a fivefold increase. The 1997 Asian Financial Crisis ended this era with a catastrophic 13.1% GDP contraction in 1998 -- the worst economic crisis in Indonesia's modern history.

### Prabowo Era (2024-present)

Prabowo inherited an economy growing at ~5% from the Jokowi administration. In 2025, GDP grew at 5.11%. The government targets 8% growth through ambitious programs including the Free Nutritious Meal Program (MBG) with a $28 billion annual budget, the Danantara sovereign wealth fund, and plans to retire all fossil fuel power plants within 15 years. The IMF projects Indonesia's nominal GDP at $1.54 trillion and PPP at $5.45 trillion for 2026. Inflation stood at 2.42% in April 2026 -- lower and more stable than the Soeharto era's double-digit inflation.

### Key Differences

- **Growth trajectory**: Soeharto started from near-collapse (negative growth, hyperinflation) and achieved sustained high growth for decades. Prabowo started from a stable ~5% baseline and aims to accelerate.
- **Growth consistency**: Soeharto's high growth was interrupted by commodity price shocks (oil glut 1980s). Prabowo faces challenges of middle-income trap and global uncertainty.
- **Structural transformation**: Soeharto transformed Indonesia from agriculture to manufacturing. Prabowo faces deindustrialization risks with manufacturing shedding jobs since 2023.
- **Crisis management**: Soeharto failed catastrophically during the 1997 Asian Financial Crisis. Prabowo has not faced a major economic crisis yet.

### Similarities

- Both inherited economies with stable growth from previous administrations' reforms.
- Both used large-scale government programs to drive economic outcomes (REPELITA vs MBG/Danantara).
- Both relied on natural resource exports as a pillar of growth.
- Both faced challenges of currency depreciation against the US dollar.
