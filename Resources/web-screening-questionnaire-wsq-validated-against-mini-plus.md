## Summary

Meuldijk et al. (2017) validated the Web Screening Questionnaire (WSQ), a 15-item self-report digital screening tool based on the MINI and AUDIT, against the MINI-Plus 5.0 clinician-administered interview in a Dutch sample of 1,292 adults (1,117 general population, 175 psychiatric outpatients).

## Key Points

### Study Design
- Cross-sectional diagnostic accuracy study
- WSQ completed online; MINI-Plus administered by telephone within 2 weeks
- MINI-Plus treated as gold standard (DSM-IV-TR criteria)
- Analyzed sensitivity, specificity, AUC, PPV, NPV per disorder

### Diagnostic Accuracy Results
| Disorder | Sensitivity | Specificity | AUC | PPV | NPV |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Depressive disorder | 0.58 | 0.94 | 0.83 | 0.33 | 0.97 |
| GAD | 0.66 | 0.90 | 0.89 | 0.24 | 0.98 |
| Panic disorder | 0.81 | 0.95 | 0.98 | 0.41 | 0.99 |
| Social phobia | 0.79 | 0.93 | 0.95 | 0.31 | 0.99 |
| Agoraphobia | 0.81 | 0.95 | 0.80 | 0.10 | 0.99 |
| Specific phobia | 0.52 | 0.73 | — | 0.02 | 0.99 |
| PTSD | 0.79 | 0.52 | 0.86 | 0.11 | 0.97 |
| OCD | 0.67 | 0.91 | 0.82 | 0.10 | 0.99 |
| Alcohol abuse/dependence | 0.56 | 0.92 | 0.82 | 0.18 | 0.98 |

### Key Findings
- High NPVs (≥0.97) across disorders — good at ruling out
- Low PPVs (0.02–0.41) due to low prevalence in general population
- Specificity generally high (0.73–0.95) except PTSD (0.52) and specific phobia (0.73)
- Sensitivity moderate for most disorders (0.56–0.81) but low for depression (0.58)
- WSQ useful as rule-out screening tool; positive screens need clinical follow-up
