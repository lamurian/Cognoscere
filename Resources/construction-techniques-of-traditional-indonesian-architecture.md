Traditional Indonesian construction uses a post-beam-lintel system with mortise-and-tenon joints and wooden pegs. This flexible joinery absorbs earthquake shock waves, a critical adaptation in the seismically active archipelago [@wikipediacontributors2025]. Natural materials include timber, bamboo, thatch (coconut leaves, grass, rice straw), and fibre.

Steep gabled roofs with deep eaves are characteristic. In-house granaries (rangkiang) are universal features. Majapahit builders mastered brick construction using mortar of vine sap and palm sugar [@wikipediacontributors2025]. Tidore architecture uses button-and-bond systems with lightweight local materials [@rahim2023].
