Green skyscrapers enhance urban biodiversity through several mechanisms. Vertical forests with diverse endemic plant species create multi-story habitats: Bosco Verticale's plant selection involved three years of research with botanists, choosing species for orientation, height, and climate adaptation [@alkodmany2023]. Sky gardens at 30-meter intervals on Oasia Downtown provide layered habitats for birds, insects, and pollinators, while creepers offer nectar and pollen sources [@alkodmany2023]. CDL's Tree House in Singapore uses a 2,300 m2 green wall across all four sides of its 24-story tower, with modular panels containing ferns, orchids, bromeliads, and vines — reducing surface temperature by 3°C and cooling energy by 15% [@alkodmany2023]. The key is species diversity and native plant selection: ACROS Fukuoka started with 76 plant varieties and 37,000 plants, expanding naturally to 120 species and 50,000 plants after 25 years as birds spread seeds [@alkodmany2023]. These projects demonstrate that tall buildings can function as vertical ecosystems, not just habitat replacements.
