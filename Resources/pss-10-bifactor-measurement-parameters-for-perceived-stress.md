## Summary

The Perceived Stress Scale-10 (PSS-10) is a validated 10-item self-report instrument measuring global perceived stress [@cohen1983]. Confirmatory factor analysis consistently supports a two-factor structure: Perceived Helplessness (negatively phrased items 1,2,3,6,9,10) and Perceived Self-Efficacy (positively phrased items 4,5,7,8).

Item-level descriptive statistics from large samples (N=1,574 adolescents) show item means ranging from 2.59 to 3.22 (SDs 0.78-0.93) on a 5-point scale [@liu2020]. The bifactor model reveals the two dimensions are correlated at approximately r=-0.3, reflecting the inverse relationship between helplessness and self-efficacy. Factor loadings range 0.40-0.75 for helplessness items and 0.57-0.67 for self-efficacy items.

Bifactor modeling in German validation studies [@reis2017] confirms the general stress factor accounts for substantial common variance while specific factors (helplessness, self-efficacy) capture unique variance. The PSS-10 total score threshold for clinically relevant stress is typically 27-28 (range 10-50). Internal consistency (Cronbach's alpha) ranges 0.79-0.87 across populations.
