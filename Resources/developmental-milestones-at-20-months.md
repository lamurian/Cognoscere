## Summary

At twenty months, the toddler balances between dependence and autonomy — a hallmark of Erikson's autonomy vs. shame stage. Weight reaches roughly 11.3 kg (24.9 lb) and length 84.5 cm (33.3 in). Language comprehension accelerates sharply, and the toddler understands far more than they can say [@misirliyan2023].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~11.3 kg (range 9.8–13.2 kg); median length ~84.5 cm. Growth steady but individual variation widens. Activity level very high — caloric needs remain significant despite slower growth.

**Emotional Maturity:** Autonomy vs. shame and doubt (Erikson stage 2). The toddler asserts independence — "me do it!" — while still needing frequent reassurance. May show shame or embarrassment when corrected harshly. Developing self-awareness — refers to self by name. Beginning to understand and follow simple rules with reminders.

**Psychological Development:** Piaget substage 6 (mental representation). The toddler can now think about actions before performing them — mental problem solving emerges. Pretend play becomes more elaborate with sequences. Imitates others' actions from memory (deferred imitation). Sorts objects by shape and colour with help. Understands "one" and "more."

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: runs well; walks up and down stairs with hand held; climbs on furniture and playground equipment; kicks ball forward; throws ball overhand. Fine motor: scribbles with more cwontrol (circular strokes); builds tower of 4 blocks; feeds self with spoon and fork; begins to help with dressing; turns pages one at a time.

**Speech and Language Development:** Uses 15–30 words; may combine 2 words; speech is 25–50% intelligible to strangers; follows two-step commands; points to 5+ body parts; names familiar objects in books; begins using possessives ("my shoe") [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: fewer than 5–10 words; not walking; no two-word combinations emerging; no pretend play; does not imitate; does not follow simple commands; persistent mouthing of objects; not interested in interactive play; loss of previously acquired language or social skills.
