Marxism-Leninism is the political ideology developed by Vladimir Lenin that adapts Marx's theory to revolutionary practice. It centers on a vanguard party leading the proletariat in revolution, establishing a dictatorship of the proletariat, and nationalizing the means of production. The state directs the economy through central planning under single-party rule [@thulin2026].

This became the Soviet model implemented after the 1917 October Revolution. Key features include democratic centralism (party discipline), state ownership of industry, collective agriculture, and five-year plans for industrial development. The Soviet Union achieved rapid industrialization and wartime resilience but grew stagnant due to planning inefficiencies and lack of democratic accountability. It collapsed in 1991.
