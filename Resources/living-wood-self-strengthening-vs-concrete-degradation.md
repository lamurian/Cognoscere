Living wood structures have a fundamental advantage over concrete barriers: they self-strengthen over time while concrete degrades. Concrete undergoes carbonation, alkali-silica reaction, and rebar corrosion -- typically requiring major repairs within 50-100 years, sooner in marine or aggressive environments.

Living wood actively defends itself through compartmentalization, where sapwood produces chemical barriers against decay. Inosculated joints develop continuous grain flow, creating connections stronger than mechanical fastening. Baubotanik research confirms fused tree tissues form common growth rings creating true structural unity [@mylo2023].

Living trees produce reaction wood in response to environmental loads, increasing density and strength in stressed areas. A living fence subjected to wind or impact loads literally grows stronger -- the opposite of concrete accumulating damage. Living root bridges in Meghalaya have self-strengthened for 500-600 years, supporting 50 people across 30m spans [@middleton2019].

## Key Points
- Concrete: 17-40 MPa compressive strength, degrades over 50-100 years.
- Living wood: ~80 MPa compression parallel to grain, self-repairs indefinitely.
- Reaction wood formation: living trees strengthen under load; concrete cracks.
- Proven 500-600 year lifespan from living root bridges.
