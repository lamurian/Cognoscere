DARPA's Reefense program proved hybrid living+artificial infrastructure can outperform hard structures. A modular reef system at Tyndall Air Force Base -- combining porous concrete modules with living oysters and marsh vegetation -- reduced wave power by >90% and becomes more effective over time as oysters grow [@bushek2025].

The Reefense principle applies directly to fences: an engineered framework (gabion or steel posts) provides immediate function, while living woody plants colonize and reinforce it long-term. The artificial structure handles short-term loads; the living component provides decades of self-strengthening.

Living shorelines (NOFA) confirm this approach is cheaper and longer-lasting than concrete alternatives. Unlike concrete seawalls, living shorelines grow over time, improve water and air quality, and store carbon [@noaafisheries2022].

## Key Points
- DARPA Reefense: >90% wave reduction, self-strengthening.
- Hybrid framework (gabion/steel) + living plants = immediate + long-term strength.
- Living shorelines cheaper, more durable than concrete seawalls.
