## Summary

Rotterdam is Europe's largest seaport, handling approximately 438.8 million tonnes of cargo annually (2023). A 2018 study by Erasmus University Rotterdam commissioned by the Port Authority found that the port contributes €45.6 billion or 6.2% of the Netherlands' added value — twice the previously calculated figure. The increase came from including "forward indirect effects": economic activities made possible by the port's presence, such as re-exports via logistics and distribution, beyond the traditional measurement of direct employment and backward supply-chain effects.

The port serves as the primary gateway for goods entering and exiting continental Europe, particularly for Germany, Belgium, and northern France — a market of over 500 million consumers. The port area spans 12,500 hectares, with the Port Authority employing ~1,440 people directly and supporting many more across logistics, petrochemicals, and maritime services. The "Rotterdam effect" extends across the Dutch maritime cluster, recognized as the most competitive in the world, encompassing maritime business services, commodity trading, and shipbuilding.

## Key Points

- €45.6 billion / 6.2% of Dutch GDP
- 438.8 million tonnes cargo throughput (2023)
- Gateway to 500+ million European consumers
- Strong growth prospects in digitization and energy transition
- Includes petrochemical-industrial complex, one of the world's largest
