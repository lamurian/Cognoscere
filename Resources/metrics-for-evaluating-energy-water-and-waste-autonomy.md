## Summary

Evaluating autonomy requires metrics across energy, water, and waste systems. Borham's database catalogs strategies by city system and scale, providing a framework for assessing which systems a design addresses [@borham2024]. Key aggregate metrics include: net-zero energy (S018, annual energy balance), water neutrality (S148, total self-sufficiency), and zero waste (S121, circular material flows). Ecological Performance Standards (EPS), developed by Biomimicry 3.8, measure how an intact local ecosystem performs across key services and set these as design targets [@borham2024]. The Lloyd Crossing project in Portland used this approach, setting goals for carbon balance, rainfall infiltration, and solar energy fixation based on historical ecosystem data [@pedersenzari2020].

Pedersen Zari's Ecosystem Services Analysis (ESA) provides systematic methodology: establish pre-development ecological baseline, measure current performance, identify gaps, set targets, and evaluate post-occupancy [@pedersenzari2020]. Relevant metrics for off-grid homesteads include: energy use intensity (kWh/m2/yr), water use (L/person/day), waste diversion rate (%), embodied carbon (kgCO2/m2), and biodiversity indices. The French practice review found projects primarily use existing labels (HQE, BREEAM, LEED) but noted urban biomimicry still lacks specific sustainability metrics [@blanco2021]. Future development of standardised biomimetic certification would significantly advance the field.

## Key Points
- Net-zero energy (S018), water neutrality (S148), zero waste (S121) are key autonomy metrics
- EPS uses local ecosystem performance as a design target for buildings
- ESA provides systematic framework: baseline → measure → target → evaluate
- Existing labels don't fully capture biomimetic performance [@blanco2021]
