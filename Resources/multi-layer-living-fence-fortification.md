No single technique makes living fences outperform concrete. The solution is five complementary layers addressing different failure modes, creating a system stronger than any single method.

**Layer 1: Structural core.** Gabion or MSE wall provides mass and impact resistance, replacing concrete's structural role at 40-75% lower cost [@tensarinternational2020]. **Layer 2: Living posts.** Inosculated tree trunks at 2-4m intervals grow structural support over decades. Poplar/willow for temperate, Lannea/Gliricidia for tropical drylands [@reville2017]. **Layer 3: Pleached weave.** Diamond lattice of willow rods at 45-degree angles, spaced 10 inches apart, creates complete barrier in 2-3 years [@morningchores2025].

**Layer 4: Thorny defense.** Bougainvillea, Euphorbia tirucalli, Pyracantha along the fence create biological razor wire -- painful, self-renewing, maintenance-free [@positivebloom2025]. **Layer 5: Periodic reinforcement.** Steel/concrete posts every 20-50m, concrete footings at corners, wire tensioning during 2-5 year establishment period.

The barrier improves over time rather than degrading. Initial cost: $25-50/linear foot vs concrete $63-275/ft. Ecological co-benefits are positive rather than negative.

## Key Points
- Five layers: core, posts, weave, thorns, reinforcement.
- Each layer addresses different failure modes.
- System self-strengthens over decades; concrete degrades.
- Cost: $25-50/ft living vs $63-275/ft concrete.
