## Summary

Pasung — the practice of shackling or confining people with mental illness — represents a severe human rights violation that persists in Indonesia despite decades of policy responses. The practice was first prohibited by a Home Affairs Ministry decree in 1977, yet remains widespread. The Indonesia Free Pasung Program launched in 2010, followed by Mental Health Act No. 18 of 2014, which criminalised pasung with potential imprisonment or fines for perpetrators [@hidayat2023].

However, implementation has been slow and fragmented. Government claims of reducing pasung from 18,880 cases (2010) to 12,220 (2018) are disputed, with data from the Indonesian Centre for Health Research showing prevalence only dropped from 14.3% to 14% — not accounting for population growth of approximately 30 million over that period [@hidayat2023].

The policy analysis reveals deep structural issues: incomplete decentralisation of healthcare, ambiguous messaging across stakeholders, unclear institutional roles, and lack of accountability mechanisms. The Mental Health Act of 2014 was repealed and merged into the 2023 Health Law, raising concerns among professionals about whether community-based protections would be maintained [@hidayat2023].

The case for community-oriented mental health services is fundamentally a rights-based imperative. Families resort to pasung when they lack alternatives — the penal code prohibits both shackling and allowing a mentally ill person to roam unaccompanied, leaving families caught between legal obligations and inaccessible treatment systems [@hidayat2023].

## Key Points
- Pasung persists despite prohibition since 1977 and criminalisation in 2014
- Government reduction claims are disputed: 14.3% to 14% prevalence over 8 years
- Incomplete decentralisation fragments policy implementation
- Families face legal catch-22: cannot shackle, cannot let person roam
- 2023 Health Law repealed Mental Health Act 2014, raising protection concerns
- Community-based services are essential to provide alternatives to pasung
