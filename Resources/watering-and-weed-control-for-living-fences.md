Watering is most critical in the first year of establishment. For willow living fences, water deeply once per week during dry spells, giving the structure a thorough drench each time [@deesidewillow2024]. Willow is thirsty and the more water it receives, the faster it establishes. In subsequent years, supplemental watering is only needed during prolonged drought. Crape myrtle living fences, once established, are drought tolerant and require far less irrigation [@moore2006].

Weed control is essential, especially during establishment. Keep an area 6-10 inches around each base clear of weeds and grass, which compete for water and nutrients [@deesidewillow2024]. Laying water-permeable membrane at planting time suppresses weed growth long-term [@rachelthegardener2017; @albert2024]. For existing living fences without membrane, hand-weed or mulch around the base annually. Removing competition is the single most effective way to accelerate growth in the first 2-3 years.
