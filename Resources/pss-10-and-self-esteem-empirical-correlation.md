PSS-10 correlates negatively with global self-esteem (Rosenberg SES) in the small-to-moderate range, weaker than its links to self-efficacy or optimism. Ibrahim [@ibrahim2021] reported Pearson r = -0.337 (p = 0.001, n = 100) in Indian young adults. Bodys-Cupak et al. [@bodyscupak2022] used a reverse-coded SES and found Spearman rho = 0.214 (p < 0.001, n = 307), corresponding to rho ≈ -0.21; self-esteem was non-significant in regression when optimism and life satisfaction were included.

The range (|r| ≈ 0.21-0.34, ~4-11% shared variance) is smaller than for self-efficacy (~3-32%) or optimism (~15-29%). Self-esteem contributes less unique variance to stress perception, consistent with the resource network model where overlapping protective factors differentially predict stress outcomes. Sources: @ibrahim2021, @bodyscupak2022.
