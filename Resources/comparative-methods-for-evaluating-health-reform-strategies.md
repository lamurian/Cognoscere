## Summary

Several established comparative methods evaluate whether specialized care or policy infrastructure reforms produce better health outcomes. The most robust evidence comes from cross-national comparisons, econometric health production functions, and natural experiments from health system reforms.

**Cross-national comparisons**: The Commonwealth Fund International Health Policy Surveys provide the most comprehensive comparative data across high-income countries, using standardized metrics across four domains: access, continuity, comprehensiveness, and coordination [@gumas2024; @fitzgerald2022]. These surveys consistently show that countries with stronger primary care infrastructure achieve better population health outcomes at lower cost.

**Health production function models**: The Grossman health production model, applied at macro level, estimates the effect of health spending on outcomes while controlling for socioeconomic and environmental factors. Arhin et al. applied fixed and random effects panel regression to 34 Sub-Saharan African countries, finding that PHC expenditure improves UHC index (elasticity 0.14), life expectancy (0.008), and infant mortality (-0.03) [@arhin2022]. This approach allows isolation of PHC spending effects from total health expenditure.

**Natural experiments**: State-level reforms in the US provide quasi-experimental evidence. Maryland's Primary Care Program reduced inpatient hospital use by 21% in its first two years and produced 20% fewer COVID-19 deaths among Medicare beneficiaries compared to matched controls [@phillips2022]. California's emerging multi-payer primary care investment strategy, projected to save $2.4 billion annually in commercial insurance costs, offers another natural experiment [@phillips2022].

**Policy simulation modeling**: Agent-based and system dynamics models can project the population health impact of alternative reform strategies, though these remain underutilized compared to retrospective methods.

## Key Points

- Cross-national surveys (Commonwealth Fund) provide the most comprehensive comparative evidence
- Grossman health production models isolate PHC spending effects at national level
- Maryland's MDPCP natural experiment showed 21% reduction in inpatient use and 20% fewer COVID deaths
- Multiple comparative tools exist but are underutilized in policy design

## Confidence: Moderate (strong methods exist but applied in limited contexts)
