## Summary

At twenty-three months, the toddler stands on the cusp of the two-year milestone. Weight averages about 12.0 kg (26.5 lb) and length 87 cm (34.3 in). Language has become a flexible tool for expressing wants, observations, and early ideas. The toddler now negotiates, protests, and delights with growing verbal sophistication [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~12.0 kg (range 10.4–14.0 kg); median length ~87 cm. Growth steady. Second-year molars may begin erupting.

**Emotional Maturity:** May experience increased separation anxiety temporarily as awareness of self-as-separate deepens. Shows pride in independence (dressing self). Imitates adult emotions and behaviours. Beginning to understand cause-and-effect in social situations ("If I hit, mommy is sad"). May show preference for one parent. Tantrums may decrease as language improves.

**Psychological Development:** Substage 6 maturing. The toddler solves problems mentally before acting — thinks about how to reach a toy on a high shelf. Pretend play includes sequences of 3–4 actions. Sorts objects by shape, colour, and size with increasing accuracy. Identifies several body parts, animals, and common objects. Understands concepts of "one," "big," "little."

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: runs, climbs, and jumps; walks up and down stairs with rail, both feet per step; kicks ball with direction; stands on tiptoes; may begin to pedal tricycle (with assistance). Fine motor: builds tower of 6 cubes; scribbles with varied strokes; turns pages one at a time; uses spoon and fork; helps undress completely; may indicate need to use potty.

**Speech and Language Development:** Uses 50–100+ words; combines 2–3 words in phrases; speech 50–65% intelligible to strangers; uses "I," "me," "you" with growing accuracy; asks "why" and "what" questions; names most common objects; follows two-step commands; uses some plurals; tells about immediate experiences [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: fewer than 20 words; not combining words; not walking steadily; no pretend play; not following two-step commands; no interest in other children; lack of eye contact; not responding to name; not pointing to body parts; loss of previously acquired skills.
