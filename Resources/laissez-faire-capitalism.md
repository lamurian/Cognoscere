Laissez-faire capitalism is the purest form of market capitalism. The state acts only as a night-watchman protecting property rights and enforcing contracts. Markets are self-regulating through the price mechanism described by Adam Smith's invisible hand. Government intervention is minimal or absent [@thulin2026].

This variant achieves maximum allocative efficiency and individual freedom but tends toward high inequality and monopoly formation. Without regulation, firms can accumulate market power, externalities go uncorrected, and economic cycles produce severe boom-bust patterns. It was most closely approximated in 19th-century Britain and the United States during the Gilded Age.
