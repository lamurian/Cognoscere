## Summary

At two months, social engagement deepens markedly. The infant now weighs approximately 5.3 kg (11.7 lb) and measures 58 cm (22.8 in). The hallmark is the emergence of the social smile — a deliberate, reciprocal response to human interaction rather than a reflexive grimace [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~5.3 kg (range 4.4–6.4 kg); median length ~58 cm. Continued gain of 150–200 g/week. Head circumference increases roughly 2 cm/month in the first 3 months.

**Emotional Maturity:** The social smile emerges — the infant smiles responsively when spoken to or smiled at. Calms when picked up. Shows excitement by waving arms and legs. Beginning of co-regulation: the infant uses caregiver proximity to modulate arousal.

**Psychological Development:** Piaget's sensorimotor substage 1–2 transition: primary circular reactions begin. The infant watches their own hands, repeats pleasurable body-centred actions. Looks at a toy for several seconds; follows moving objects past midline. Recognises primary caregiver [@misirliyan2023].

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: holds head up when on tummy at 45°; smoother arm and leg movements, less jerky than newborn. Fine motor: opens hands briefly; hands unfisted half the time. Retains grasp reflex.

**Speech and Language Development:** Makes cooing sounds — vowel-like noises distinct from crying. Reacts to loud sounds by startling or quieting. Turns head toward voice. Differentiated cry patterns for different needs [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags include: not gazing at objects; not responding to loud sounds; no cooing; persistent hand fisting with lack of arm movement; not bringing hands to mouth; lack of symmetrical arm movements; stiff legs with little movement when on tummy; no crying when upset; no smiling at people.
