## Summary

Beyond the primary candidates, several other Dutch PIs work on Indonesia health topics and could potentially host an MSCA Postdoctoral Fellow on digital health implementation in rural Indonesia. This note summarises their profiles, expertise, and relevance.

## Key PIs

### Prof. dr. Ajay Bailey — Utrecht University
- Professor of Geographies of Health, Inclusion, and Development
- Supervises PhD on "Barriers in accessing transport for Older adults in Indonesia"
- Published on older adults' experiences with app-based ride-hailing in Indonesia (Journal of Transport & Health, 2025) — at intersection of digital technology, health, and aging
- Contact: a.bailey@uu.nl — https://www.uu.nl/staff/ABailey
- Digital health relevance: Moderate — focuses on mobility/transport but intersects with digital app usage for health access

### Dr. Robert Sparrow — ISS, Erasmus University Rotterdam
- Associate Professor at International Institute of Social Studies (ISS)
- Member of Rotterdam Global Health Initiative (RGHI)
- Published on Indonesia's JKN national health insurance: "A Decade of National Health Insurance in Indonesia: Achievements and Challenges"
- Field: Health economics, social protection, Indonesia

### Prof. Menno Pradhan — VU Amsterdam / University of Amsterdam
- Professor in Project and Program Evaluation for International Development
- Worked at World Bank Indonesia office for 7 years
- Field: Impact evaluation, health programs, education

### Prof. Frank van Leth — VU Amsterdam
- Full Professor, Faculty of Science, Health Sciences; Associate Professor at APH Global Health
- Extensive experience in infectious disease epidemiology and global health
- Co-author on Indonesia AMR studies with Schultsz group

### Prof. Marleen Bekker / S. Moore Jr. — Wageningen University & Research
- Project: "Whole-of-Society Approach to Health System Resiliency and Pandemic Preparedness in Indonesia" (2024-2026)
- Wageningen's Health and Society group works on digital health and health system resilience

## MSCA Approach for These PIs

For PIs without direct digital health expertise, the MSCA fellow could propose a project combining the PI's Indonesia research network with the fellow's own digital health implementation expertise. This is common in MSCA — the fellow brings complementary skills.
