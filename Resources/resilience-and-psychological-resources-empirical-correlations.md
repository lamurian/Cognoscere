## Summary

Psychological resources — such as sense of coherence, self-efficacy, optimism, hope, wisdom, and emotional intelligence — show moderate-to-strong positive correlations with resilience. These resources collectively form a network of interrelated protective factors.

Schäfer et al. [@schfer2023] used network analysis on 11 resilience factors (RFs) in a prospective study (N = 1,275). The strongest partial correlations among RFs were: sense of coherence (SOC) with sense of mastery (r = 0.35), dispositional resilience with self-efficacy (r = 0.34), and coping using emotional support with social support (r = 0.37). SOC was the most central node, suggesting it integrates multiple psychological resources.

Pellerin et al. [@pellerin2022] found that CD-RISC-10 correlated strongly with trait emotional intelligence (r = 0.63) and moderately with well-being (r = 0.48). Wisdom, optimism, hope, self-efficacy, and peaceful disengagement were significant protective factors predicting resilient (vs symptomatic) trajectories during lockdown.

These findings support the conservation of resources theory: psychological resources form a correlated network where individuals with more resources tend to have higher resilience.

## Key Points
- SOC and sense of mastery: partial r = 0.35 [@schfer2023]
- Dispositional resilience and self-efficacy: partial r = 0.34 [@schfer2023]
- CD-RISC-10 and trait emotional intelligence: r = 0.63 [@pellerin2022]
- Psychological resources predict membership in resilient vs symptomatic trajectories [@pellerin2022]
