## Summary

Five major survey-based datasets provide direct quantitative measures of political trust. **World Values Survey (WVS)** covers 120+ countries across 7 waves (1981-2022) with items on confidence in government, parliament, and political parties on 4-point scales. Free download at worldvaluessurvey.org (CSV, SPSS, Stata, R). Trustworthiness: high — academic governance, rigorous translation protocols, probability sampling, most cited social science survey.

**European Social Survey (ESS)**** covers 30+ European countries biennially since 2002 with an 11-point 'trust in politicians' item plus trust in parliament, legal system, and parties. Free download via europeansocialsurvey.org data portal; R package essurvey for programmatic access. Trustworthiness: very high — ESFRI European Research Infrastructure Consortium (ERIC) status, peer-reviewed methodology, strict translation and sampling protocols.

**Eurobarometer** surveys EU member states since 1973 with items on trust in national government, parliament, political parties, and EU institutions. Free at europa.eu/eurobarometer via interactive portal (CSV, SPSS, Stata). **OECD Trust Survey** follows the OECD Guidelines on Measuring Trust [@oecd2017] across 20+ countries, measuring institutional trust and its drivers (competence, benevolence, integrity). Data via OECD.stat. **TRUEDEM Political Trust Database** harmonises 91 trust measures from ESS, EVS, WVS, ISSP, and Eurobarometer across 44 European countries (1980-2023) at trustobservatory.eu.

All five sources are free to access with published methodologies. WVS and ESS are the gold standard for comparative trust research. TRUEDEM uniquely enables cross-survey comparability. None offer REST APIs — downloads are manual or via R packages (essurvey, wvs). For credibility: all are academically governed or professionally conducted with transparent procedures and sample documentation. The methodological appendix of each provides full question wording, translation protocols, sampling frames, and response rates.
