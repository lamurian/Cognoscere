## Summary

KIT (Royal Tropical Institute) in Amsterdam is an independent centre of expertise on sustainable development with a strong focus on global health [@kitroyaltropicalinstitute2026]. KIT Institute offers a Master of Public Health and Health Equity (MPH-HE) programme in collaboration with Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam, plus advanced short courses including the Global Perspectives on Digital Health (GPDH) course. The GPDH course covers digital health policy, mHealth interventions, health information systems, and implementation challenges in resource-limited settings.

While KIT primarily offers education and does not directly employ postdoctoral researchers, it functions as a gateway institution. KIT collaborates with VU Amsterdam on research and teaching, and its faculty may co-supervise PhD and postdoc researchers. KIT's network spans health professionals from Indonesia and other LMICs, making it a valuable connection point.

## Key Points

- **URL**: https://www.kit.nl/institute/impact-areas/global-health/
- **Salary**: Not applicable directly — KIT does not regularly post postdoc positions
- **Requirements**: For GPDH course (2 weeks blended): health professionals seeking digital health expertise
- **Description**: Offers Master of Public Health (MPH-HE) and digital health short courses; trains health professionals from LMICs including Indonesia
- **Travel to Indonesia**: Through its LMIC network and alumni
- **Remote Work**: Courses offered blended (online + in-person)
- **Approach**: KIT is best used as networking/training rather than employment. Contact KIT faculty for collaboration or supervision connections to VU Amsterdam
