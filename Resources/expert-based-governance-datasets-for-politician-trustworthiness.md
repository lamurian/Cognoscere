## Summary

Expert-based datasets provide indirect trustworthiness measures through governance, corruption, and democratic quality indicators. **Varieties of Democracy (V-Dem)**** is the most comprehensive — 531 indicators and 251 indices across 202 countries from 1789-present, including Political Corruption Index (v2x_corr), Clean Elections Index, and Deliberative Democracy Index. Each rated by 5+ experts with Bayesian IRT measurement modelling. Free at v-dem.net (CSV, STATA, SPSS, R) or via vdemdata R package. Trustworthiness: very high — peer-reviewed, 3,000+ expert coders, formal inter-coder reliability metrics [@coppedge2026].

**Worldwide Governance Indicators (WGI)**** provide annual composites across 200+ countries (1996-present) on Control of Corruption and Voice & Accountability — both inverse trustworthiness proxies. Free at govindicators.org and via World Bank Data API (api.worldbank.org) for programmatic access. R package wbgdata available. Trustworthiness: high — composites from 35+ sources, transparent unobserved components aggregation, standard errors reported [@kaufmann2024]. **Transparency International CPI** ranks 180 countries on perceived public sector corruption (0-100) from 13 expert surveys. Free at transparency.org/en/cpi/ (CSV, Excel). Trustworthiness: medium-high — well-established methodology but perception-based and not year-to-year comparable [@transparencyinternational2024].

**Global Party Survey (GPS)**** (Norris, Harvard) covers 1,859 experts on 1,043 parties in 179 countries, measuring ideology, populist rhetoric, and issue positions. Populist rhetoric scores indirectly capture anti-establishment trustworthiness. Free at dataverse.harvard.edu/dataverse/GlobalPartySurvey (CSV, SPSS, Stata). Trustworthiness: high — peer-reviewed, but single-wave (2019) [@norris2020]. **Manifesto Project (MARPOR)** content-codes party manifestos across 877 parties, 50+ countries, 1920-present. Enables promise-keeping measurement by comparing pledges to policy. Free at manifesto-project.wzb.eu (CSV, SPSS, Stata, Excel) with R package manifestor and manifestata API. Trustworthiness: high — 45+ years of development, systematic coding, inter-coder reliability, annual updates [@lehmann2025].
