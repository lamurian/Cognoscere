## Summary

A 2024 scoping review of 17 studies examining organisational coaching for workplace resilience found consistent quantitative evidence that coaching interventions improve resilience across diverse occupational settings [@sipondo2024]. The review followed a five-stage PRISMA-ScR methodology.

Quantitative findings from key studies:

- **Grant et al. (2014)**: Coaching enhanced resilience with significant increases on the Cognitive Hardiness Scale [t(30)=1.79, p<0.05]
- **Grant et al. (2017)**: Significant increases in resilience [t(30)=2.50, p<0.05]
- **Auer et al. (2022)**: Large quasi-experimental study (n=1,005) found significantly larger resilience gains in the coaching group than the control group
- **Dyrbye et al. (2019)**: Pilot RCT (n=82) with physicians found CD-RISC scores improved more in the coaching group [mean (SD) = 1.3 (5.2) vs 0.6 (4.0)]
- **Sherlock-Storey et al. (2013)**: Post-intervention PsyCap resilience significantly higher [t(10)=3.24, p=0.045]
- **De Haan et al. (2019)**: Randomised study (n=180) found coaching increased resilience (Brief Resilience Scale), with correlations to psychological well-being and self-efficacy
- **Fontes & Dello Russo (2021)**: Experimental field study (n=56) found coaching increased Psychological Capital (PsyCap), which includes resilience

The most common coaching approach was cognitive-behavioural and solution-focused. Measures included the Brief Resilience Scale (BRS), the Connor-Davidson Resilience Scale (CD-RISC), the Cognitive Hardiness Scale, and the Psychological Capital Questionnaire (PCQ-12). All 17 studies reported improvements in resilience following coaching.

However, the review noted several limitations: most studies lacked active control groups, had small sample sizes, and varied widely in intervention duration (from 6 weeks to over a year).

## Key Points

- Coaching consistently improves resilience across 17 studies
- Effect sizes range from small to moderate
- Cognitive-behavioural and solution-focused coaching is the most evidence-based approach
- Key mechanisms include goal-setting, resource development, coach-coachee relationship, and between-session homework
- The most used validated measures: BRS, CD-RISC, Cognitive Hardiness Scale, PCQ-12
