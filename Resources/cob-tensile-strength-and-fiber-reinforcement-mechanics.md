Cob's modulus of rupture (tensile strength) ranges from 0.08 to 0.24 MPa (avg 0.17 MPa), compared to 2-5 MPa for concrete [@pullen2009]. Straw fibers provide tensile reinforcement analogous to rebar in concrete: they bridge cracks and resist pull-out after the matrix cracks. Research shows fibers shorter than 4-5 inches pull out before reaching their tensile limit, making fiber length critical for effective reinforcement [@pullen2009]. Cob's tensile-to-compressive strength ratio (~0.25) exceeds concrete's (~0.10), indicating cob is relatively more capable in tension due to fiber action. For rammed earth, tensile strength is typically estimated at 10-20% of compressive strength [@peri2021].
