## Summary

Conservation Agriculture practices (zero-tillage, reduced nitrogen fertilizer, avoided residue burning) reduce GHG emissions by 1.23-1.97 tCO2e per hectare in wheat systems, generating 1.23-1.97 credits/ha [@cariappa2024]. At USD 25/credit, farmers earn USD 18-30/ha after developer fees — 2-7% additional income per season for typical smallholders (0.6-4.9 ha).

Verra's VM0042 methodology requires three conditions: (1) <20% adoption rate in baseline area (additionality), (2) no >5% yield penalty, (3) >5% fertilizer reduction for nutrient management projects [@cariappa2024]. These conditions mean not all sustainable practices qualify — practices already common are excluded.

Agroforestry integrating trees with crops or livestock sequesters more carbon than monoculture. Drylands forestation offers substantial long-term potential. For maximum credits, combining multiple eligible practices (e.g., zero-tillage + reduced irrigation + optimized nitrogen) within integrated farming systems generates stacked credit streams.
