## Summary

The correlation between resilience (measured by CD-RISC) and resources (primarily perceived social support via MSPSS, but also other psychological resources) is consistently moderate-to-strong. Reported Pearson r values for CD-RISC with MSPSS social support cluster around r = 0.48 to r = 0.51. For other psychological resources (self-efficacy, hope, optimism, self-esteem), the range extends from r = 0.20 to r = 0.63.

Chen et al. @chen2023 found r = 0.507 (p < 0.01) between CD-RISC-10 and MSPSS social support in Chinese advanced cancer survivors (n = 286). Yang et al. @yang2020 reported r = 0.486 (p < 0.01) between CD-RISC-25 and MSPSS in a Chinese substance use disorder sample (n = 415). Pellerin et al. @pellerin2022 found CD-RISC-10 correlated strongly with trait emotional intelligence (r = 0.63) and moderately with well-being (r = 0.48). A meta-analysis by Zhao et al. @zhao2022 (66 studies, N > 10,000) found that positive psychological resources correlate with quality-of-life outcomes at r ≈ 0.30-0.71, reflecting the interconnectedness of the resource network.

The test suite asserts bounds of 0.10 < r < 0.80 (test #12) and 0.10 < r < 0.85 (test #13). The empirical data confirms that resilience ↔ resources correlations typically fall within r ≈ 0.20 to 0.63, making the lower bound of 0.10 too permissive (actual correlations are almost always above 0.20). The upper bound of 0.80-0.85 is generous but not violated by available data.

## Key Points
- CD-RISC ↔ MSPSS social support: r ≈ 0.48 to 0.51 (moderate positive)
- CD-RISC ↔ self-efficacy/hope: r ≈ 0.35 to 0.53 (moderate positive)
- CD-RISC ↔ emotional intelligence: r ≈ 0.63 (moderate-to-strong)
- Overall range across resource types: r ≈ 0.20 to 0.63
- The asserted lower bound 0.10 is too permissive; empirical minimum is ≈ 0.20
- The upper bound 0.80-0.85 is generous enough to accommodate all known correlations
