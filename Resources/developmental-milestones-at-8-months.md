## Summary

At eight months, many infants begin crawling, which fundamentally transforms their interaction with the environment. Weight averages 8.5 kg (18.7 lb) and length 71 cm (28.0 in). The combination of mobility and improved manual dexterity fuels a sharp increase in exploratory behaviour [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~8.5 kg (range 7.3–10.0 kg); median length ~71 cm. Weight gain slows further to roughly 70–100 g/week. Continued head growth ~0.5 cm/month. Teething continues — upper central incisors often emerge.

**Emotional Maturity:** Separation anxiety may begin — distress when caregiver leaves the room. Uses caregiver as secure base for exploration (beginning of Ainsworth's secure-base behaviour). Shows clear preferences for familiar toys and people. May show fear of loud noises or sudden movements.

**Psychological Development:** Substage 3 maturing. The infant combines secondary circular reactions — sequences actions to achieve goals (crawling to reach a toy). Understands means-end relationships. Searches for a completely hidden object — beginning of object permanence. Imitates actions after seeing them modelled.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: crawls or creeps on hands and knees; may crawl backwards or commando-style first; sits well without support; transitions from sitting to crawling position; pulls to standing with support; may cruise sideways holding furniture. Fine motor: develops inferior pincer grasp (using thumb and side of index finger); picks up small objects with raking motion; bangs two cubes together; holds own bottle.

**Speech and Language Development:** Babbles with intonation resembling adult speech; may say "mama" or "dada" nonspecifically; understands "no" through tone; responds to simple verbal requests with gestures (e.g., "come here" with outstretched arms); uses gestures to communicate (reaching, pushing away). Begins to understand object names in context [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: not sitting independently; not bearing weight on legs; no effort to move (rolling, creeping, crawling); does not transfer objects between hands; does not babble with consonant sounds; no response to name; does not show recognition of familiar people; no interest in interactive play; persistent hand fisting; head circumference not growing.
