## Summary

At seventeen months, the toddler's language comprehension accelerates while expressive vocabulary grows more slowly. Weight averages about 10.7 kg (23.6 lb) and length 81 cm (31.9 in). The toddler actively imitates household routines and shows an emerging sense of ownership — "mine" becomes a frequent concept [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~10.7 kg (range 9.2–12.5 kg); median length ~81 cm. Growth rate stable. Canine teeth and first molars may begin erupting — teething discomfort may affect appetite and sleep temporarily.

**Emotional Maturity:** May show possessiveness with toys. Imitates adult emotional expressions and behaviours. Beginning to understand the concept of rules and limits (though compliance is inconsistent). Shows pride in achievements. May show empathy — offers comfort to distressed peer or adult.

**Psychological Development:** Substage 5 continues. Active experimentation with objects. Recognises and sorts familiar objects by category (balls, cars). Engages in more elaborate pretend play sequences. Can follow two-step commands. Points to multiple body parts. Identifies objects in picture books. Shows sustained interest in activities for 2–3 minutes.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: walks well, runs stiffly; climbs onto and off furniture without help; walks up stairs holding rail with both feet on each step; tries to kick ball; throws ball while standing. Fine motor: scribbles vigorously; stacks 3–4 blocks; feeds self with spoon with less spilling; helps with dressing (pushes arms through sleeves).

**Speech and Language Development:** Uses 6–15 words; points to 3 or more body parts; follows simple one-step commands without gesture; names familiar objects; uses words to request; imitates words heard in conversation; jargon begins to give way to more recognisable words [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: not walking independently; not using any words by 17–18 months; does not point to body parts; does not follow simple commands; does not imitate; no pretend play; limited eye contact; does not respond to name; not interested in other people; loss of previously acquired language or social skills.
