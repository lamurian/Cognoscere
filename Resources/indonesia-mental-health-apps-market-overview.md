## Summary

Indonesia's mental health apps market is growing rapidly, driven by high smartphone penetration, rising mental health awareness post-COVID, and a severe shortage of mental health professionals. The market was valued at $37.2 million in 2023 and is projected to reach $100.16 million by 2030, growing at a 15.2% CAGR [@insights2024]. The wider Indonesia mental health market (including services) was valued at $38.2 billion in 2025 with an 8.16% CAGR through 2034 [@tracxn2026].

## Key Points

**Market Size.** Indonesia Mental Health Apps Market: $37.2M (2023) to $100.16M (2030), 15.2% CAGR [@insights2024]. Indonesia Mental Health Market (broader): $38.2B (2025) to $79B (2034), 8.16% CAGR. Indonesia Digital Health Market: $2.6B in 2024, Southeast Asia's second-largest [@tracxn2026]. Global Digital Mental Health Market: $32.06B (2026) to $58.67B (2030), 16.3% CAGR.

**Key Drivers.** (1) Smartphone penetration in Indonesia projected to increase 6.1 percentage points between 2024-2029, expanding the addressable user base. (2) Rising incidences: adolescent mental disorders at 5.5% prevalence, anxiety disorders affecting 3.7% of adolescents [@insights2024]. (3) Shortage of health workers: Indonesia has only 0.3 psychiatrists per 100,000 people vs WHO recommended 3.3 per 100,000, creating demand for digital alternatives. (4) Pandemic acceleration: Riliv reported 800% surge in online consultation demand post-COVID [@eastventures2023].

**Restraints.** (1) Data security and privacy concerns; (2) Limited awareness of digital mental health solutions; (3) Persistent stigma around mental health in Indonesian culture; (4) Uneven internet access across the archipelago [@insights2024].

**Competitive Landscape.** The market includes global players (HeadSpace, Calm, Happify, Woebot, Daylio) and local Indonesian players (Riliv, KALM, Bicarakan.id, Satu Persen, Qalboo, Teduh, Tenang AI, Psikologimu, Diceritain, Ami). The local players segment is fragmented, with Riliv leading at Tracxn score 52/100 [@tracxn2026]. East Ventures is the most active investor, backing Riliv, Bicarakan.id, Intellect, and Mindtera [@stacyoentoro2022].

**Reimbursement Context.** BPJS Kesehatan covers mental health treatment costs (psychiatric medication and counseling with a formal diagnosis), which facilitates the broader market but creates competition from subsidized public sector services [@insights2024].
