## Summary

ArborStructures grows young trees in custom 2x4x8-foot molds over 3 years until trunks fill the mold dimensions. The resulting panels are inosculated (naturally grafted) together to form solid, living structural components. The company claims this method can create domes, pyramids, and buildings up to 300ft (91m) tall, sufficient for the 20m target.

The species used is an unnamed fast-growing hardwood with natural rot, insect, and decay resistance, tolerant of -20F to drought. After inosculation, interior bark is sandblasted off to halt interior growth, enabling doors, windows, and furnishings. Total timeline: 4 years (3 nursery + 1 on-site). Full utilities (plumbing, electricity, radiant heat) can be integrated. Silica infusion is claimed to create a partially petrified living material with enhanced strength and fire resistance. Historical precedent: same hardwood species used in 1600s barns still standing.
