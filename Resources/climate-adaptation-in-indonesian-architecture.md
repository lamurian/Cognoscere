Most rumah adat are built on stilts (except Java and Bali) to moderate tropical temperatures, elevate above stormwater, protect against dampness and termites, and avoid malaria-carrying mosquitos. Sharply inclined roofs shed heavy rain while large eaves provide shade [@wikipediacontributors2025].

Traditional dwellings use natural materials: timber, bamboo, thatch, and fibre. The post-beam-lintel system uses mortise-and-tenon joints with wooden pegs instead of nails, providing earthquake resilience [@wikipediacontributors2025]. Indonesian vernacular embodies sustainability principles aligning with modern green building criteria [@khumaira2025].
