## Summary

At fifteen months, the toddler is a determined walker and increasingly skilled communicator. Weight averages about 10.3 kg (22.7 lb) and length 79 cm (31.1 in). The toddler now uses 3–5 words consistently, follows simple directions, and engages in early pretend play — feeding a doll or talking into a toy phone [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~10.3 kg (range 8.9–12.0 kg); median length ~79 cm. Weight gain ~40–60 g/week. Head circumference ~47 cm. Growth velocity remains slow, steady, and highly individual.

**Emotional Maturity:** Shows affection with hugs and kisses. Expresses a broader emotional range — pride, embarrassment, and empathy precursors appear. May have tantrums when frustrated; relies heavily on caregiver for emotional regulation. Engages in parallel play — plays alongside other children but not yet with them.

**Psychological Development:** Late substage 4 / early Piaget substage 5 (tertiary circular reactions, 12–18 months). The toddler actively experiments — drops objects from different heights, throws toys to see what happens. Imitates adult activities (sweeping, talking on phone). Points to one body part. Looks at correct picture when named. Uses objects functionally.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: walks well independently; stoops to pick up toys; climbs onto furniture; may walk up stairs with hand held; kneels without support; throws ball underhand while seated. Fine motor: scribbles in imitation; stacks 2–3 blocks; uses spoon and cup with moderate success; turns pages in book; places shapes in shape-sorter [@misirliyan2023].

**Speech and Language Development:** Uses 3–5 words; points to one body part; follows one-step commands with gesture; uses jargon extensively; says "no" meaningfully; understands names of familiar objects and people; imitates words overheard in conversation [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: not walking independently by 15–16 months; not using any words; does not point to show things; does not imitate actions; does not follow simple directions with gesture; does not look to where you point; not showing interest in other children; stiff or very floppy muscle tone; not using both hands; limited facial expressions.
