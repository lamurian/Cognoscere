## Summary

At six and a half years, the child consolidates first-grade academic skills and expands social competence. Weight averages about 22.8 kg (50.3 lb) and height 120 cm (47.2 in). Reading fluency increases markedly; the child writes short stories, performs mental arithmetic, and navigates increasingly complex social relationships — including managing conflict independently [@misirliyan2023].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~22.8 kg (range 18.7–27.8 kg); median height ~120 cm. Growth steady. Permanent incisors and first molars present; more primary teeth being shed. Legs grow proportionally faster than trunk — "stretching out" appearance.

**Emotional Maturity:** Peer relationships deepen — has a best friend and a wider friendship circle. Understands social dynamics (inclusion, exclusion, popularity). May experience social anxiety or peer pressure. Developing sense of industry vs. inferiority (Erikson stage 4) — feels competent when succeeding at tasks, inferior when failing. Needs balanced feedback — praise for effort, constructive guidance for improvement. Shows loyalty to friends. Can resolve peer conflicts with minimal adult help. Understands and appreciates fairness and justice.

**Psychological Development:** Concrete operational stage progressing. Masters conservation tasks (number, mass, liquid). Understands reversibility and classification. Reads with increasing fluency and comprehension — reads chapter books independently. Writes stories with beginning, middle, and end. Masters addition and subtraction within 20. Understands place value (tens and ones). Tells time to the quarter hour. Understands calendar (days, weeks, months). Beginning to understand maps and spatial relationships. Attention span 25–35 minutes.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: coordinated and athletic in movements; rides bicycle without training wheels; swims with strokes; jumps rope with skill; participates in team sports with understanding of rules and positions; balances on one foot indefinitely; performs dance or martial arts sequences. Fine motor: handwriting becomes smaller and more consistent; writes within lines; draws with detail and proportion; uses scissors with precision; builds complex models; types on keyboard with growing speed; plays simple tunes on musical instruments; weaves, sews, or does other fine crafts [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

**Speech and Language Development:** Adult-like grammar and syntax; vocabulary 5000–7000+ words; reads aloud with fluency, expression, and comprehension; writes coherent paragraphs and short stories; uses descriptive language and dialogue in writing; understands and uses more abstract vocabulary; gives clear oral presentations; engages in sustained group discussions; understands multiple meanings and figurative language; may begin learning a second language [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: motor coordination significantly below peers; cannot ride bicycle or skip; handwriting illegible; reading level significantly below grade expectations; cannot write simple sentences; cannot perform basic addition and subtraction; cannot maintain friendships; social isolation or persistent peer conflict; extreme perfectionism or school refusal; cannot focus for 15+ minutes on school tasks; persistent daytime wetting or soiling; significant behaviour problems at school; loss of previously acquired academic or social skills.
