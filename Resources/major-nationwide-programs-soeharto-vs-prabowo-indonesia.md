## Major Nationwide Programs: Soeharto vs Prabowo Indonesia

### Soeharto's Flagship Programs

**REPELITA (Five-Year Development Plans, 1969-1998)**: Six five-year plans covering agriculture (Green Revolution for rice self-sufficiency by 1984), infrastructure outside Java, labor-intensive manufacturing, capital goods, telecommunications/education, and foreign investment. **SD Inpres (Presidential Instruction Schools, 1973)**: Massive school construction program achieving ~90% primary enrollment by 1983. **Puskesmas (Community Health Centers)**: Nationwide network of primary health centers. **Family Planning (KB)**: Highly successful program reducing population growth. **Transmigration**: Moving people from Java to outer islands (controversial). **Palapa Satellite (1976)**: Telecommunications infrastructure unifying the archipelago. **Swasembada Pangan (Rice Self-Sufficiency, 1984)**: FAO-recognized achievement. **TABANAS**: National Development Savings program.

### Prabowo's Flagship Programs

**Makan Bergizi Gratis (MBG / Free Nutritious Meal Program, 2025)**: Ambitious program providing free meals to 82 million beneficiaries including students, pregnant women, and nursing mothers. Budget of ~Rp 100 trillion ($6.7 billion) in additional allocation. Reached nearly 30 million beneficiaries within 11 months. Faces implementation challenges including food safety and governance issues. **Danantara (Daya Anagata Nusantara Sovereign Wealth Fund, 2025)**: State investment management agency consolidating state-owned enterprises, modeled on Temasek. Estimated to manage assets of ~US$900 billion. **Budget Efficiency (Presidential Instruction No. 1/2025)**: US$18.8 billion in budget cuts across ministries. **Energy Transition**: Promised to retire all fossil fuel power plants within 15 years. **Defense Modernization**: Acquisition of Rafale jets, new submarines, and defense industry consolidation under Defend ID. **IKN Nusantara**: Continued development of new capital city in East Kalimantan.

### Key Differences

- **Philosophy**: Soeharto's programs were developmentalist authoritarian -- top-down, controlled, and used for regime legitimation. Prabowo's programs blend populist welfare (MBG) with technocratic investment (Danantara).
- **Funding scale**: Soeharto used oil windfalls and foreign aid (IGGI/CGI). Prabowo relies on budget reallocation and efficiency savings, plus sovereign wealth mechanisms.
- **Implementation**: Soeharto's military-bureaucratic apparatus could enforce programs nationally. Prabowo operates with a democratic system requiring coordination across levels of government.
- **Legacy**: Soeharto's SD Inpres and Puskesmas created lasting infrastructure. Prabowo's MBG is too early to assess long-term impact.
- **Megaprojects**: Soeharto built Palapa and Trans-Java toll road foundation. Prabowo continues IKN and adds defense modernization.

### Similarities

- Both use/d large-scale nationwide nutrition/welfare programs (Soeharto's Puskesmas/family planning vs Prabowo's MBG).
- Both rely/d on flagship 'legacy' infrastructure projects.
- Both employ/ed state-owned enterprises as instruments of national development.
- Both centralize/ed major programs under presidential authority.
- Both claim/ed food security and self-sufficiency as a key goal.
