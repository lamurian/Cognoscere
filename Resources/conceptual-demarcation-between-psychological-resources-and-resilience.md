A persistent theoretical tension in the literature concerns whether resilience is distinct from psychological resources or one of them. The consensus, articulated across multiple frameworks, draws the following demarcation.

**Temporal precedence.** In Bonanno's trajectory model, resilience is defined as a longitudinal outcome — maintaining stable, healthy functioning during and after exposure to adversity [@bonanno2004]. Psychological resources, by contrast, exist prior to adversity and predict which trajectory an individual follows. Pellerin and Raufaste's longitudinal study operationalised this cleanly: psychological resources (wisdom, optimism, hope, self-efficacy, peaceful disengagement) measured at baseline predicted membership in resilient vs symptomatic trajectories, mediated by psychological flexibility [@pellerin2022].

**Causal asymmetry.** In COR theory, resources are the currency of adaptation. Resource loss drives distress (r = 0.66 with PTSD symptoms), while resource gain correlates modestly with resilience (r = 0.30) [@egozifarkash2022]. Resilience, in this framework, is not a resource itself but an emergent property of having sufficient resources and deploying them effectively under pressure. Hobfoll's key insight is that resource loss is more impactful than resource gain — a finding that distinguishes the resource mechanism from the resilience outcome.

**Nested conceptualisation in PsyCap.** Luthans et al.'s Psychological Capital framework treats resilience as one of four first-order resources within a higher-order PsyCap construct [@luthans2017psychological]. This creates ambiguity: resilience here is measured as a personal capacity to "bounce back" (trait-like), not as a trajectory of adaptation (process-like). This dual usage — resilience-as-resource vs resilience-as-outcome — persists across the literature and is a known source of confusion. The CD-RISC, the most widely used resilience scale, also measures resilience as a cluster of personal qualities (resource-like), conflating the two constructs.

**Emerging resolution.** The clearest theoretical resolution frames resources as the *inputs* or *protective factors* that an individual possesses, while resilience is the *process* or *observed outcome* of successful adaptation under adversity. Psychological flexibility (the capacity to adapt cognition and behaviour to situational demands) has been proposed as the mediating mechanism connecting resources to resilient outcomes [@pellerin2022]. This suggests resources enable flexibility, which in turn produces resilience — a three-part causal chain that preserves the distinction.
