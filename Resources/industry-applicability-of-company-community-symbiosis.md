Company-community symbiosis is not equally applicable across all industries. It is most pronounced in industries with high geographic fixedness: natural resources, agriculture, manufacturing, tourism, and port/logistics operations tethered to specific locations [@cuevasvalenzuela2022; @westskog2024]. These industries have intrinsic incentives to invest locally because they cannot readily relocate. Extractive industries show the most ambivalent patterns: high economic impact paired with high environmental and social costs, creating \"sacrifice zones\" where communities bear disproportionate burdens [@cuevasvalenzuela2022]. Local food systems demonstrate naturally symbiotic characteristics due to place-based identity and moral exchange motivations [@westskog2024]. Knowledge-intensive and digital service industries tend toward weaker local embeddedness due to footloose talent markets and global supply chains. However, the interactionist perspective suggests any organization can cultivate symbiosis through intentional relational practices — ongoing interaction, shared meaning-making, and institutional alignment [@cyr2022].
