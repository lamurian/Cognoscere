## The Invisible Backbone

Women perform the majority of agricultural labor in rural Indonesia, especially in livestock, food processing, and household food production. In dairy farming -- a crucial income source for many families -- women are primarily responsible for:
- Feeding, grazing, and health management of livestock
- Milking and rearing calves
- Feed preparation, cleaning barns, maintaining equipment

Women in East Java's dairy regions dedicate nearly two hours daily to dairy activities. This labor absorbs surplus household capacity and provides crucial supplementary income.

## The Recognition Gap

Despite their economic contribution, women face systematic exclusion:
- Rural women's roles are often invisible and undervalued in official statistics
- Women have limited influence in decision-making processes related to innovation and investment
- Sociocultural and institutional factors sustain gender inequality in agriculture
- Women's informal roles do not bring the legal, political, and social status that similar work brings to men
- Women's contributions within cooperatives are frequently not formally acknowledged or rewarded

## Barriers to Economic Participation

- Formal credit access is much lower for women -- land titles (primary collateral) are disproportionately held by men
- Extension services (agricultural training, veterinary advice) historically target men
- Women are underrepresented in cooperative boards and BUMDes management
- Time poverty: women's domestic responsibilities (childcare, cooking, water collection) reduce time for economic activity
- Cultural restrictions on women's mobility in some regions

## What Works: Policy Solutions

### Joint Land Titling
Successful land redistribution cases show that around 400 out of 1,050 land certificates were given to women. Joint titling (husband-wife) leads to:
- Higher household investment in land improvements
- Better child nutrition and education outcomes
- Greater household resilience to shocks
- Women farmers are often the most active in advocacy processes for land reform

### Targeted Credit and Cooperative Membership
- Microcredit programs designed for women (e.g., PNPM Mandiri Perdesaan, KUR)
- Reserved seats for women in BUMDes and cooperative leadership
- Mobile banking agents (often women) serving as last-mile financial access points
- Training and certification programs scheduled around women's time constraints

## Economic Impact

Closing the gender gap in agricultural productivity in Indonesia could increase agricultural output by an estimated 10-20%. Women are more likely to spend income on household welfare, child education, and nutrition -- directly improving quality-of-life metrics beyond raw GDP.
