The simulated psychological resources distribution (M=0.798, SD=0.040, range=[0.665, 0.891] on [0,1]) shows a mean consistent with PsyCap research, where most individuals report moderately high levels of self-efficacy, optimism, and hope [@luthans2017]. The simulated skew of -0.380 (left-skewed) correctly matches the empirical pattern for psychological resources: distributions are near-normal but negatively skewed, meaning most people cluster above the midpoint.

However, the SD of 0.040 is the tightest of all four simulated variables and approximately 5x narrower than empirical estimates. COR theory predicts right-skew for socioeconomic resources but near-normal spread for psychological resources [@hobfoll2002]. The PSS-10 self-efficacy subscale (measuring perceived coping confidence) shows item-level SDs of 0.78-0.92 on 0-4 [@liu2020], corresponding to ~0.20-0.23 on [0,1]. The simulated kurtosis of 0.005 (mesokurtic) aligns with the empirical description. The extreme homogeneity means agents below 0.665 resources do not exist, preventing resource loss spirals — a central COR theory prediction about how stress propagates through resource depletion [@hobfoll2002].
