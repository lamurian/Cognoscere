Perceived social support and the frequency or quality of social interactions show consistent positive associations with resilience.

Chmitorz et al. [@chmitorz2018] reported that the BRS was positively correlated with social support on the Oslo Social Support Scale-3 (OSS-3) in a German population sample. Smith et al. [@smith2008] found BRS positively correlated with social support on the Interpersonal Support Evaluation List (ISEL). Acoba [@acoba2024] found family support r = 0.25 and significant other support r = 0.27 with positive affect, a resilience-related outcome.

In the Ye et al. [@ye2022] analysis, the CD-RISC captures resource-based resilience. It includes items about close relationships and knowing where to turn for help, making social interaction a component of resilience itself. Social interaction provides emotional regulation support, practical assistance, and belongingness, all of which strengthen the capacity to bounce back from adversity.
