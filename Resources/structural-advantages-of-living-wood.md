Living wood structures have mechanical advantages over timber: they resist decay through compartmentalization, where sapwood actively defends against rot. This contrasts with dead wood which decays from the outside in [@wikipediacontributors2025a]. Grafted joints in living structures are stronger than manufactured joinery because the wood grain flows continuously through connections rather than being chopped and glued. Living root bridges demonstrate structures that self-strengthen over centuries as roots thicken [@wikipediacontributors2025a]. Inosculated trees tested by the Baubotanik research group showed common growth rings forming at fusion zones, with tissue that cannot be assigned to either parent plant -- creating true structural unity [@mylo2023].
