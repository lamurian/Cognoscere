Capitalism is an economic system defined by private ownership of the means of production and operation for profit. Prices are determined by markets through supply and demand, capital is accumulated privately, and labor is hired on a wage basis. The profit motive and competition drive innovation and efficiency [@morganelli2025].

Capitalism achieves high dynamic efficiency through market competition and consumer sovereignty. However, it generates cyclical crises and persistent inequality. The system has evolved significantly since the Industrial Revolution through financialization, globalization, and state intervention [@thulin2026].
