Inosculation is the natural biological process where separate trees grow together into a single mechanical and physiological unit. The Research Group Baubotanik at the Technical University of Munich tested two simple methods on 32 tree pairs over 8 years: crosswise connection (stems crossed at one point) and bending connection (stems bent to touch), fixed with rope or screw. Unlike horticultural grafting, no bark cutting is needed [@mylo2023]. Evidence of successful union comes from the pipe model theory: when stems share water, diameter growth above the connection differs from below. For Salix alba this difference was significant (p < 0.05), confirming merger [@mylo2023]. This technique forms the structural foundation for living architecture that strengthens over time.
