Crape myrtle living fences require less frequent but more pest-focused maintenance than willow. Annual pruning in late winter or early spring before new growth begins maintains shape and encourages flowering [@moore2006]. Remove suckers, crossing branches, and spent flower heads. Unlike willow which is woven and pruned hard to a framework, crape myrtle is trained by bending and tying trunks around frames over several seasons — pruning is selective rather than structural [@moore2006].

Pest management is the main maintenance burden. Crape myrtle is susceptible to powdery mildew (especially on new growth after topping), bark scale, Japanese beetles, and aphids [@bartletttreeresearchlaboratories2020; @moore2006]. These pests produce honeydew that leads to sooty mold on leaves and surrounding surfaces. Monitoring and treatment are required: insecticidal soap or neem oil for aphids and scale, fungicides for powdery mildew if severe. Siting in full sun with good air circulation reduces disease pressure. Choosing powdery mildew-resistant hybrids also reduces maintenance needs [@bartletttreeresearchlaboratories2020].
