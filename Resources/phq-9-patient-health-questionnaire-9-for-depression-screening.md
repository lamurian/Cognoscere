## Summary

The Patient Health Questionnaire-9 (PHQ-9) is a 9-item self-report instrument for screening, diagnosing, and monitoring depression severity. Each item corresponds directly to one of the nine DSM-5 criteria for major depressive disorder. Items assess anhedonia, depressed mood, sleep disturbance, fatigue, appetite changes, guilt or worthlessness, concentration difficulty, psychomotor changes, and suicidal thoughts. Each item is rated 0 (not at all) to 3 (nearly every day) over the past two weeks, yielding a total score range of 0-27. Severity thresholds: 5-9 minimal, 10-14 moderate, 15-19 moderately severe, 20-27 severe.

The original validation study by Kroenke et al. (2001) in 6,000 patients from 15 primary care clinics found that a PHQ-9 score of >=10 had a sensitivity of 88% and a specificity of 88% for major depressive disorder against a mental health professional interview as the criterion standard [@kroenke2001]. A meta-analysis by Manea et al. (2012) pooling 18 studies (n=7,180) reported pooled sensitivity of 0.85 (95% CI 0.75-0.91) and pooled specificity of 0.89 (95% CI 0.83-0.92) at cutoff 10 [@manea2011]. The updated individual participant data meta-analysis by Levis et al. (2019) across 29 studies found sensitivity of 0.88 and specificity of 0.85 at the same cutoff [@levis2019].

## Key Points

- 9 items directly aligned with DSM-5 MDD criteria, 0-3 Likert scale, 2-week recall
- Original validation (Kroenke 2001): Se=0.88, Sp=0.88 at cutoff >=10 [@kroenke2001]
- Meta-analysis (Manea 2012, 18 studies): pooled Se=0.85, Sp=0.89 at cutoff 10 [@manea2011]
- IPD meta-analysis (Levis 2019, 29 studies): Se=0.88, Sp=0.85 at cutoff 10 [@levis2019]
- Optimal cutoff range 8-11 with no significant difference in diagnostic properties between cutoffs within this range
- Performance varies by setting: hospital settings show lower sensitivity (0.74) than primary care (0.89)
- PHQ-9 is a screening tool, not a standalone diagnostic instrument; positives require clinical follow-up
