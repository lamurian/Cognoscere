## Summary

Canada attracts skilled migrants through Express Entry (Federal Skilled Worker Program) and the Global Talent Stream (2-week processing). Data scientist and health informatics roles are eligible occupations. Toronto, Vancouver, and Montreal are major hubs for digital health, with employers like Shopify, RBC, Deloitte, IBM Canada, and provincial health authorities actively hiring for FHIR implementation, health data analytics, and digital transformation [@pandey2026]. Regular job openings include data scientist, FHIR consultant, health informatics specialist, clinical data analyst, and digital health transformation lead. HL7 FHIR and OpenEHR skills are increasingly sought after as Canada progresses with its digital health interoperability agenda.

## Key Details
Strong public education system with free K-12 schooling for PRs. Universal healthcare (provincially administered, e.g., OHIP, MSP) covers medically necessary services free at point of use [@numbeo2026c]. PhD data scientist salary range: CAD 95,000 - CAD 160,000 (national average ~CAD 100,000 base, senior roles up to CAD 160,000). Healthcare IT and consulting roles trend toward the higher end [@yosifova2025; @codelabsacademy2025]. Living cost: CAD 3,300 - 3,800/month for a single person including rent. Rent for one-bedroom city-centre averages CAD 1,810; excluding rent Numbeo estimates CAD 1,434/month. Toronto and Vancouver are most expensive; Calgary, Ottawa, Montreal are more affordable [@numbeo2026c; @numbeo2026e].
