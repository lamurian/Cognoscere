## Summary

Micronutrients—vitamins and minerals—are integral to every stage of the immune response, from maintaining physical barriers to enabling adaptive immunity through antibody production. Vitamin A and zinc are essential for skin and mucosal cell integrity, forming the first line of defence. Vitamin D regulates antimicrobial peptide expression in epithelial cells, while vitamin C is necessary for collagen synthesis and neutrophil function [@gombart2020].

Vitamins C, E, and selenium act as antioxidants protecting immune cells from oxidative damage during the respiratory burst. Iron and copper are required for NADPH oxidase activity that generates reactive oxygen species to kill pathogens. Zinc is indispensable for the development, differentiation, and activation of T lymphocytes, and zinc deficiency impairs phagocytosis and increases infection susceptibility. Marginal micronutrient deficiencies, even without clinical symptoms, can impair immune function—populations with inadequate intakes show increased incidence of respiratory tract infections. The evidence is strongest for vitamins C and D and zinc, where supplementation at levels above current RDAs may reduce infection risk in deficient populations [@gombart2020].
