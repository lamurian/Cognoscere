## Summary

At sixteen months, the toddler walks, climbs, and explores with relentless curiosity. Weight reaches roughly 10.5 kg (23.1 lb) and length 80 cm (31.5 in). Vocabulary expands gradually, and the toddler increasingly uses vocalisations and gestures to direct adult attention — a key social-cognitive milestone [@misirliyan2023].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~10.5 kg (range 9.1–12.3 kg); median length ~80 cm. Weight gain ~40–50 g/week. Body proportions shift: legs grow proportionally longer; toddler figure emerges.

**Emotional Maturity:** Independence struggles intensify — may refuse help while also needing reassurance. Tantrums may increase as frustration meets limited communication. Shows clinginess alternating with bold exploration. Beginning signs of self-conscious emotions (embarrassment). Responds to caregiver's emotional tone.

**Psychological Development:** Piaget's substage 5 (tertiary circular reactions). The toddler deliberately varies actions to observe outcomes — drops ball from different heights, throws objects in different directions. Trial-and-error problem solving emerges. Points to several body parts. Engages in simple pretend play (feeds doll, puts stuffed animal to bed).

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: walks fast, may begin to run stiffly; climbs onto chairs and sofas; walks upstairs with one hand held; squats to play; tries to kick ball forward. Fine motor: scribbles spontaneously; stacks 2–3 blocks; turns pages (2–3 at a time); feeds self with spoon increasingly well; removes socks.

**Speech and Language Development:** Uses 5–10 words; points to 2–3 body parts; follows simple commands; shakes head "no"; uses vocalisations with pointing to direct attention; understands object names; begins to use words to request ("more," "up") [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: not walking independently by 16 months; not using any words; not pointing to body parts; does not imitate sounds or actions; does not respond to name; does not engage in any pretend play; no eye contact; loss of previously acquired skills; extreme passivity or hyperactivity.
