## Summary

The Beck Anxiety Inventory (BAI) is a 21-item self-report instrument measuring anxiety severity, with a particular focus on somatic symptoms of anxiety. Items assess symptoms such as numbness or tingling, feeling hot, wobbliness in legs, inability to relax, fear of worst happening, dizziness or lightheadedness, heart pounding or racing, unsteadiness, terror, nervousness, choking sensation, hand trembling, shakiness, fear of losing control, difficulty breathing, fear of dying, scared, indigestion, faintness, facial flushing, and hot or cold sweats. Each item is rated 0 (not at all) to 3 (severely) reflecting the past week, with total scores ranging from 0-63. Severity thresholds: 0-7 minimal, 8-15 mild, 16-25 moderate, 26-63 severe.

The BAI was developed by Beck and Steer (1990) to discriminate between anxiety and depression, addressing the limited discriminant validity of earlier instruments [@beck1990]. Factor analysis consistently reveals two factors: somatic anxiety (e.g., numbness, palpitations, trembling) and subjective anxiety (e.g., nervousness, fear, inability to relax). The BAI demonstrates high internal consistency (alpha 0.90-0.94) and test-retest reliability (0.67-0.93).

Studies of diagnostic utility in older adult psychiatric outpatients found that the BAI could discriminate patients with current anxiety disorders from those without, though no single cutoff proved optimal due to sensitivity/specificity trade-offs [@kabacoff1997]. The BAI correlates moderately with the BDI-II (r ~0.50-0.60) and more strongly with other anxiety measures. AUC values for detecting anxiety disorders range from 0.70 to 0.85 across populations. The BAI's emphasis on somatic symptoms can produce elevated scores in populations with medical comorbidities.

## Key Points

- 21 items, 0-3 scale, past week recall, total 0-63
- Original manual (Beck & Steer 1990): developed specifically to discriminate anxiety from depression [@beck1990]
- Two-factor structure: somatic anxiety and subjective anxiety factors
- Internal consistency alpha 0.90-0.94, retest 0.67-0.93
- Discriminates anxiety disorder patients from non-anxiety patients, but optimal cutoff varies
- Correlation with BDI-II: r ~0.50-0.60 (discriminant validity target achieved)
- Somatic focus limits accuracy in medical populations
