## Summary

At twenty-one months, the toddler is increasingly verbal and physically capable. Weight averages about 11.5 kg (25.4 lb) and length 85.5 cm (33.7 in). The toddler now asks for things by name, follows two-step commands consistently, and may begin to resist naps or bedtime — testing boundaries is developmentally appropriate [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~11.5 kg (range 10.0–13.4 kg); median length ~85.5 cm. Growth velocity stable. Canine teeth typically erupt around this age.

**Emotional Maturity:** Defiance and boundary-testing increase — the toddler is learning what is and is not acceptable. Shows pride and pleasure in new accomplishments. May show jealousy when caregiver attention is directed elsewhere. Beginning to label own emotions with help. Responds to praise and approval.

**Psychological Development:** Substage 6: mental representation solidifying. Memory improves — remembers where objects are kept. Engages in increasingly complex pretend play with multiple steps (prepares "meal," serves to doll, "cleans" up). Begins to sort by simple categories. Shows interest in other children but play is still primarily parallel. Notices daily routines.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: runs smoothly; walks up stairs holding rail (may alternate feet with help); squats for long periods during play; kicks ball with direction; throws ball overhand with more accuracy. Fine motor: scribbles in circular patterns; builds tower of 4–5 blocks; feeds self with utensils; helps dress and undress; washes hands with help.

**Speech and Language Development:** Uses 20–40 words; combines 2 words regularly; speech ~25–50% intelligible; asks for items by name; follows two-step commands; uses words to express needs and desires; names familiar objects and pictures; begins using action words ("go," "eat," "run") [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: fewer than 10 words; not combining two words; not walking well; no pretend play; does not imitate; not responding to name; not pointing to body parts; persistent drooling beyond teething; not showing interest in other children; lack of eye contact; loss of previously acquired skills.
