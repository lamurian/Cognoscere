## Education

Dr. Caroline Figueroa holds an **MD** (medical doctorate) and a **PhD in the neuroscience of depression** from the **University of Amsterdam** and the **University of Oxford** [@figueroa2026]. She has several years of clinical experience in psychiatry as a licensed medical doctor. Before joining TU Delft, she was a postdoctoral scholar at the **University of California, Berkeley**. She is currently a **2025-26 Dutch Commonwealth Fund Harkness Fellow in Health Care Policy and Practice** at **Stanford University**, where she is a visiting scholar in Pediatrics.

## Current Role

- **Assistant Professor in Digital Health**, Faculty of Technology, Policy and Management, Delft University of Technology (TU Delft)
- **Lead**, RISE Digital Health research group (Research on Inclusive Solutions and Empowerment in Digital Health)
- **Co-Director**, Mental Health and Tech Special Interest Group, EMHIC global consortium
- **Harkness Fellow**, Stanford University (2025-26)

Her Google Scholar profile shows **2,801 total citations**, **h-index of 27**, and **i10-index of 40** [@figueroa-googlescholar].

## Current Ongoing Research

Dr. Figueroa's research centers on developing, testing, and implementing personalized digital health tools aimed at improving wellbeing, with a strong emphasis on underserved populations such as ethnic minorities and lower socioeconomic groups. Key research streams:

1. **Digital health equity and inclusive design** — Developing and evaluating digital mental health solutions (apps, AI-chatbots) that are safe, effective, and accessible for marginalized populations, using feminist intersectionality frameworks
2. **Just-in-time adaptive interventions** — Using machine learning and microrandomized trials to optimize digital intervention delivery for adolescent wellbeing and physical activity promotion
3. **Implementation science for digital health** — Studying how digital health tools can be implemented in real-world settings for populations with limited digital literacy
4. **AI for mental health equity** — Co-designing equitable AI-driven mental health tools with community stakeholders, incorporating participatory design methods
5. **Health policy for digital transformation** — Her Harkness Fellowship at Stanford focuses on policy recommendations for safe and equitable digital health deployment

## Relevance to LoRaWAN/FHIR Mesh Network for Rural Digital Health

Dr. Figueroa's expertise directly supports the **digital health equity and implementation** dimensions of the proposed research:

- **Digital health equity focus**: Her framework for inclusive digital health design is critical for ensuring that a LoRaWAN-based health monitoring system in rural Indonesia is designed with and for the communities it serves, accounting for low digital literacy and infrastructure constraints.
- **Participatory design methodology**: Her experience in co-designing digital interventions with underserved populations provides the methodological foundation for community-engaged design of the ESP32-LoRa-Protobuf-FHIR pipeline in Indonesian villages.
- **Implementation science**: Her work on understanding how digital health tools reach marginalized groups applies directly to studying the adoption of remote health monitoring via mesh networks in rural settings.
- **Just-in-time adaptive interventions**: Her microrandomized trial methodology could be adapted to evaluate the effectiveness of the LoRa-based health data system in improving clinical outcomes.

**Gap**: She currently has no Indonesia-specific research. An MSCA proposal would need to establish a new collaboration — potentially with Dr. Roosenboom-Kwee's PARAATHEID consortium at TU Delft + Universitas Indonesia, which already has an NWO-funded partnership. Her role would be digital health design and implementation lead.
