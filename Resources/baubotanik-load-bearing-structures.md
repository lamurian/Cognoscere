Baubotanik, developed at the University of Stuttgart and Technical University of Munich (2007-present), is a systematic building method using living trees as structural material through inosculation. Multiple trees are joined at connection points, eventually merging into single organisms [@mylo2023]. Key projects: the Plane Tree Cube (Nagold, 2012), a three-story living tower of London plane trees with crosswise-connected stems; the Baubotanik Footbridge, demonstrating pedestrian load-bearing capacity; and a 9-meter tower of white willows at Stuttgart, predicted self-supporting in 5-10 years as root systems develop. Steel scaffolding supports the structures during growth and is removed once self-supporting [@mylo2023].
