The Healthy Lives domain captures health-related behaviours, risk factors, and protective measures through seven subdomains and 23 indicators. **Physiological risk factors (Li.1)** includes diabetes prevalence, overweight and obesity in adults, and hypertension prevalence. **Behavioural risk factors (Li.2)** measures alcohol misuse, drug misuse, smoking prevalence, physical activity levels, and healthy eating indicators [@frenisterrantino2022].

**Unemployment (Li.3)** tracks the unemployment rate as a key social determinant. **Working conditions (Li.4)** covers job-related training access, low pay, and workplace safety. **Risk factors for children (Li.5)** includes infant mortality, childrens social/emotional/mental health, overweight and obesity in children, low birth weight, teenage pregnancy, child poverty, and children in state care. **Children and young peoples education (Li.6)** measures young peoples education/employment/training status, pupil absence, early years development, and GCSE achievement. **Protective measures (Li.7)** covers cancer screening coverage, vaccination coverage, and sexual health indicators [@frenisterrantino2022].

Data sources include the Health Survey for England (HSE), Active Lives Survey, National Child Measurement Programme, and NHS Digital datasets. Indicators are expressed as prevalence rates or percentages and normalized to z-scores. Higher scores indicate healthier behaviours and lower risk [@frenisterrantino2022].
