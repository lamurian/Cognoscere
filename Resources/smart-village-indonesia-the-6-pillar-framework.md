## Origin

Indonesia's Smart Village approach, championed by the Ministry of Villages (Kemendesa) in partnership with the International Telecommunication Union (ITU), aims to localize the 17 UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) down to the village level. Unlike the Smart City concept, Smart Villages respond to the specific challenges of rural areas: low connectivity, dispersed populations, limited infrastructure, and seasonal economies.

## Six Pillars

### 1. Smart People
Transforming society through 21st-century education, digital literacy, and vocational training.
- Example: Purbalingga, Central Java trained 752 village youth in digital marketing (Instagram/Facebook Ads, copywriting, COD transactions). More than 250 local businesses collaborated. Youth earnings increased from IDR 1.4 million to IDR 4 million per month.

### 2. Smart Living
Improving social and digital inclusion to raise quality of life.
- Example: Beraban Village, Bali runs the "I Luh Mantul IS DeBest" program: one family one degree, free early childhood education, doctor-on-call, emergency transport, health assistance, and temple restoration grants.

### 3. Smart Environment
Using technology and innovation to manage natural resources sustainably.
- Example: Karyamekar Village, West Java created a waste-bank system where residents exchange garbage for staple food or cash via WhatsApp through their BUMDes.

### 4. Smart Government
Simplifying government information systems through new technology for efficiency, transparency, and accountability.
- Example: Punggul Village, Bali implemented free WiFi, a population administration system (SIAK), village administration system (SiADek), geographic information system (SiGADIS), village development system (SiPEMDES), and public CCTV.

### 5. Smart Economy
Intelligent approaches and technology to strengthen economic prosperity.
- Example: West Detusoko Village, Ende, East Nusa Tenggara launched dapurkita.com, a digital economic platform that shortens supply chains, connecting farmers directly to consumers. Customers order via WhatsApp, BUMDes handles delivery.

### 6. Smart Mobility
Efficient and quality rural transportation for cheaper, faster, environmentally friendly movement of people and goods.
- Example: Villages in Mojokerto, Pasuruan, and Bangkalan districts partnered with PT Pos Indonesia for last-mile delivery services to remote areas.

## Principles

- No one-size-fits-all solution: Each village adapts the framework to its own strengths and needs
- Technology as tool, not goal: ICT serves education, health, governance, and economic inclusion
- Cost efficiency: Smart approaches reduce expenses while increasing service quality
- Continuous improvement: Services evolve based on citizen feedback and changing conditions
