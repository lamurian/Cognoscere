Integrated sustainable farming achieves resource autonomy by mimicking nature's circular flows. In closed-loop systems, waste from one process becomes input for another: kitchen scraps feed vermicomposting bins, producing worm castings that fertilize plants, while rainwater is harvested and distributed via gravity drip irrigation controlled by capacitive soil moisture sensors [@tao2024]. This eliminates the need for external fertilizers and reduces water waste. Examples include the 1m² urban homestead with its stacking worm composter and rain barrel [@pedersenzari2020; @borham2024], and the Sahara Forest Project where saltwater-cooled greenhouses produce fresh water from condensation [@verbrugghe2023]. The key insight is that autonomy results from designing linked cycles for nutrients, water, and energy, not from optimizing each resource in isolation.
