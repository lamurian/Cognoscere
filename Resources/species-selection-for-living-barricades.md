Optimal species for living barricades balance growth speed, flexibility, and durability. Willow (Salix spp.) is fastest (3-10 ft/year), most flexible, and roots easily from cuttings for diamond lattice fences, but requires 30-40ft setback from structures due to aggressive roots [@morningchores2025]. Hazel (Corylus spp.) offers flexibility when young and food (hazelnuts), but grows slower at 15 inches/year. Alder (Alnus spp.) thrives in wet conditions and coppices well. Poplar (Populus canadensis) was used by Wiechula for his 120m living wall [@wikipediacontributors2025c]. For tropical regions, Lannea coromandelica, cassava, and bamboo offer rapid growth. Thorny species (Bougainvillea, Jatropha, Euphorbia tirucalli) add security value to living barriers [@morningchores2025].
