## Summary

The Institute of Medicine has established RDAs or AIs for all essential vitamins. For adult men aged 19–70, key RDAs include: vitamin A 900 mcg RAE, vitamin C 90 mg, vitamin D 5–15 mcg (increasing with age), vitamin E 15 mg, thiamin 1.2 mg, riboflavin 1.3 mg, niacin 16 mg NE, vitamin B6 1.3 mg (1.7 mg over age 50), folate 400 mcg DFE, vitamin B12 2.4 mcg, and vitamin K 120 mcg AI [@instituteofmedicineuscommitteeonuseofdietaryreferenceintakesinnutritionlabeling2003].

For adult women aged 19–70: vitamin A 700 mcg RAE, vitamin C 75 mg, thiamin 1.1 mg, riboflavin 1.1 mg, niacin 14 mg NE, vitamin B6 1.3 mg (1.5 mg over age 50), folate 400 mcg DFE, and vitamin B12 2.4 mcg. Women capable of pregnancy should consume 400 mcg supplemental folic acid in addition to dietary folate to prevent neural tube defects. Older adults over 50 may need supplemental B12 due to reduced absorption. The FDA Daily Values for labelling provide single reference values per 2,000-calorie diet, including vitamin A 900 mcg, vitamin C 90 mg, vitamin D 20 mcg, vitamin E 15 mg, and vitamin K 120 mcg [@usfoodanddrugadministration2024].
