## Summary

At thirty-three months (2 years 9 months), the child approaches the third birthday with rapidly maturing language, increasingly complex social play, and growing independence. Weight averages about 14.0 kg (30.9 lb) and height 95 cm (37.4 in). The child can now carry on simple conversations, negotiate with peers, and express a rich inner world through imaginative play [@misirliyan2023].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~14.0 kg (range 12.1–16.3 kg); median height ~95 cm. Growth continues at steady preschool rate. All 20 primary teeth typically erupted by now. Appetite may fluctuate — normal and expected at this age.

**Emotional Maturity:** Shows affection for playmates. Understands "mine" and "his/hers." Takes turns in games with prompting. May show jealousy. Expresses a wide range of emotions verbally — "I'm sad," "I'm angry." Learning to use words rather than physical actions when upset (with adult coaching). Shows pride in achievements. Increasingly independent — wants to do things without help.

**Psychological Development:** Preoperational stage: symbolic thought now dominant. Engages in fantasy play — pretends to be animals, superheroes, or parents. Draws with representational intent — may tell you what the scribble represents. Understands concepts of size (big/little) and quantity (more/less). Knows own name, age, and gender. Counts 2–3 objects. Follows three-step commands.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: runs easily with changes in speed and direction; jumps off low steps; climbs playground equipment independently; pedals tricycle; walks up and down stairs alternating feet without support (may still use rail); walks backwards; balances briefly on one foot. Fine motor: builds tower of 8–10 blocks; copies circle and horizontal line; holds crayon with fingers; turns pages one at a time; dresses self with help for fasteners; may show interest in toilet training completion.

**Speech and Language Development:** Uses 400–600+ words; speaks in 3–5 word sentences; speech 75–80% intelligible; uses pronouns consistently; asks "who," "what," "where," "why"; uses prepositions (in, on, under); describes events and tells simple stories; uses plurals and past tense; names colours and counts to 3; holds simple conversations [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: not running; not jumping; fewer than 100 words; not speaking in sentences; speech unintelligible to family; no make-believe play; no interest in other children; poor eye contact; not following simple instructions; persistent tantrums beyond expected frequency; loss of previously acquired skills; extreme passivity or aggression; cannot name any colours or body parts.
