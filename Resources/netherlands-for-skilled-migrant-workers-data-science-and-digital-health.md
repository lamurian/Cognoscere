## Summary

The Netherlands offers the Highly Skilled Migrant (Kennismigrant) visa with the 30% tax ruling (30% of salary tax-free for 5 years), providing a significant net income advantage. The digital health ecosystem is strong, centred on Amsterdam, Eindhoven, Utrecht, and Rotterdam. Major employers include Philips, ASML, Booking.com, Health-RI (national health data infrastructure), and academic medical centres. The Netherlands is a leader in FHIR adoption for national health data exchange [@pandey2026]. Regular openings: data scientist, digital health transformation consultant, FHIR/OpenEHR specialist, clinical data architect, and health AI/ML engineer.

## Key Details
Free public education for children through secondary school. Mandatory private health insurance (EUR 100-150/month for basic coverage), well-regulated and comprehensive. Children covered under parent's policy at no extra cost. Healthcare system ranks among Europe's best. PhD data scientist salary: EUR 60,000 - EUR 95,000 (average ~EUR 96,540 with 30% ruling enhancing net take-home) [@pandey2026; @codelabsacademy2025]. Living cost: EUR 2,200 - 2,800/month including rent. Numbeo estimates EUR 1,025/month excluding rent; city-centre one-bedroom rent averages EUR 1,440. Amsterdam is most expensive; Eindhoven, Utrecht more affordable [@numbeo2026b; @numbeo2026e].
