The Healthy Places domain captures environmental and social determinants of health through five subdomains and 14 indicators. **Access to green space (Pl.1)** measures public green space availability and private outdoor space. **Local environment (Pl.2)** covers air pollution (PM2.5, NO2), transport noise, neighbourhood noise, road safety, and road traffic volume [@frenisterrantino2022].

**Access to housing (Pl.3)** includes household overcrowding, rough sleeping, and housing affordability. **Access to services (Pl.4)** measures distance to GP services, distance to pharmacies, and distance to sports or leisure facilities. **Crime (Pl.5)** tracks personal crime rates. Data sources include the Index of Multiple Deprivation, UK Air Quality Statistics platform, police recorded crime data, and Ordnance Survey data on green spaces [@frenisterrantino2022].

This domain showed negative correlation with both Healthy People and Healthy Lives in the initial index, revealing an urban-rural trade-off. London boroughs score high on People and Lives but low on Places (poor air quality, less green space, higher crime). Rural areas show the reverse pattern. The 2019 LTLA-level revision resolved this negative correlation, with all domains becoming positively correlated [@frenisterrantino2022].
