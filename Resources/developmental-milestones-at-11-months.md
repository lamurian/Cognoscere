## Summary

At eleven months, the infant stands independently and may take first steps. Weight averages about 9.4 kg (20.7 lb) and length 74 cm (29.1 in). Communication becomes increasingly intentional — pointing to indicate desire and using consistent sound patterns for specific meanings [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~9.4 kg (range 8.1–11.0 kg); median length ~74 cm. Weight gain ~50–80 g/week. Growth rate continues to decelerate. Body proportions shift — limbs lengthen, baby fat begins to redistribute.

**Emotional Maturity:** Shows assertiveness — protests when frustrated, resists unwanted actions. Separation anxiety may begin to ease for some infants, while others continue to struggle. Imitates emotional expressions of others. Beginning of empathy precursors — may look concerned when another child cries. Shows pride in accomplishments (looks to caregiver after standing).

**Psychological Development:** Substage 4 continues. The infant experiments with object properties — fits objects together, stacks rings, nests cups with help. Finds hidden objects reliably. Imitates novel behaviours hours or days after observation (deferred imitation). Understands functional use of common objects (comb to hair, cup to mouth). Points to desired object.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: stands alone momentarily; may take first independent steps; cruises quickly and confidently; climbs onto low furniture; bends to pick up objects while standing. Fine motor: precise pincer grasp; puts objects in and out of containers; turns pages of a board book (several at once); begins to use spoon with assistance.

**Speech and Language Development:** Says 1–2 words with meaning (beyond "mama/dada"); jabbers with adult-like intonation; understands simple commands with gestures ("give me"); responds to "no"; imitates speech sounds with increasing accuracy; uses pointing to draw attention to objects of interest (proto-declarative pointing) [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: not crawling or moving independently; not pulling to stand; cannot sit independently; does not use pincer grasp; no pointing; does not say "mama" or "dada" with any consistency; does not respond to name; does not show recognition of familiar people; not exploring environment; loss of previously acquired skills.
