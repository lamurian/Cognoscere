## Summary

Pomegranate (Punica granatum, delima) is a deciduous shrub or small tree (10-12 ft tall, 8-10 ft wide) with medium growth rate and excellent drought tolerance, making it well-suited for West Timor's dry tropical climate [@northcarolinastateuniversityextension2024c]. It tolerates most soil types as long as drainage is good, and prefers warm, arid conditions. The shrub produces showy orange-red flowers from spring to fall, followed by nutritious fruits high in potassium, vitamin C, and antioxidants. Best uses: dual-purpose boundary hedge providing both barrier function and edible fruit production. Works well as specimen plants within a living fence or as a fruiting hedge. Drought tolerant once established, requiring minimal irrigation. Plant from cuttings or nursery stock at wet season onset. Space 2-3m apart for hedge. Avoid in frost-prone areas. Does not form an impenetrable barrier on its own -- best combined with thornier species for security. Fruits need protection from birds. Deciduous in dry season, so provides no windbreak during driest months. Symbiotic benefits: showy flowers attract hummingbirds and bees, supporting local pollinator populations. Fruits provide food for both humans and wildlife, with seeds dispersed by animals. Adds nutritional diversity to the farming system. Can be used as a nurse plant for slower-growing species within the living fence. Tolerates alkaline and calcareous soils common in parts of West Timor.
