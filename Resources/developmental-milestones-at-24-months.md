## Summary

At twenty-four months — the two-year mark — the toddler has achieved foundational competency in walking, talking, and social engagement. Weight has roughly quadrupled from birth to about 12.2 kg (26.9 lb) and length reaches approximately 87.5 cm (34.4 in). The two-year-old uses 50+ words, speaks in two-word phrases, runs, kicks a ball, and engages in parallel play — a key social milestone [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~12.2 kg (range 10.6–14.3 kg); median length ~87.5 cm. Birth weight quadrupled. Head circumference ~48 cm (brain ~80% of adult size). Growth will now be tracked on CDC 2–20 year charts rather than WHO 0–2 year charts in U.S. practice.

**Emotional Maturity:** Shows defiant behaviour — "no" is a favourite word, reflecting developing autonomy. Gets excited when with other children. Copies others, especially adults and older children. Shows increasing independence but still looks to caregiver for reassurance. May exhibit empathy — comforts a distressed peer. Temper tantrums still occur but decreasing in frequency as language develops.

**Psychological Development:** Piaget substage 6 completion; transitioning to preoperational stage (2–7 years). The toddler uses symbols mentally — words stand for objects, one object can represent another in play. Finds hidden objects even after multiple displacements. Begins to sort shapes and colours. Completes simple puzzles (3–4 pieces). Points to things in a book when named. Follows two-step instructions ("pick up your shoes and put them in the closet") [@misirliyan2023].

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: runs well; kicks a ball; throws ball overhand; walks up and down stairs holding rail, two feet per step; stands on tiptoes; climbs onto and off furniture without help. Fine motor: builds tower of 4–6 blocks; copies drawing a line with crayon; turns pages one at a time; uses spoon and fork; removes an article of clothing; turns doorknobs [@misirliyan2023].

**Speech and Language Development:** Uses 50–200+ words (wide normal range); speaks in two-word combinations ("more cookie," "daddy go"); speech 50–65% intelligible to strangers; points to things in a book; points to 5+ body parts; uses pronouns (I, me, you, mine); follows two-step instructions; names familiar objects; asks simple questions [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: not walking steadily by 2 years; not using at least 25 words spontaneously; not using two-word phrases; does not point to body parts; does not imitate actions or words; does not follow simple instructions; not engaging in pretend play; does not show interest in other children; persistent toe-walking; loss of previously acquired language or social skills; avoids eye contact. This is the second AAP-recommended autism screening age (18 and 24 months).
