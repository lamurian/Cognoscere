The PSS-10 has a two-factor structure comprising Perceived Self-Efficacy (4 items) and Perceived Helplessness (6 items). Self-efficacy reflects the challenge dimension — the belief that one can handle demands. Helplessness reflects the hindrance dimension — the sense that demands exceed resources.

Boluarte-Carbajal, Navarro-Flores, and Villarreal-Zegarra [@boluartecarbajal2021] reported a Spearman correlation of rs = 0.57 (p < .001) between the two dimensions in a general population sample (n = 210). This moderate correlation means they are related but distinct, consistent with the challenge-hindrance stress model where both positive and negative stress appraisals coexist.

Together the two dimensions form a complete picture of the stress experience. Helplessness is more strongly linked to negative outcomes such as anxiety and negative affect. Self-efficacy is more strongly linked to positive outcomes such as positive affect and proactive coping.
