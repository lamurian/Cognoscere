Willow (Salix spp.) is structurally inadequate for harvested timber. Black willow ranks among the lightest North American hardwoods (27 lb/ft³), with very low bending and crushing strength and no natural decay resistance [@krinard1985; @cassens2007]. Crape myrtle (Lagerstroemia spp.) is a moderate-density hardwood with significantly better mechanical properties: Janka hardness 1,090 lbf, Modulus of Rupture 14,120 psi, and moderately durable decay resistance [@wooddatabase2010]. For load-bearing timber applications, crape myrtle is the clearly superior choice, but both are outperformed by naturally durable woods like black locust, cedar, or pressure-treated southern pine for ground-contact posts [@krinard1985; @cassens2007].
