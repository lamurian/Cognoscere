## Summary

Dr. Ruth Peters is Assistant Professor in Global Health at the Athena Institute, Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam. She is one of the strongest candidates to host an MSCA Postdoctoral Fellow researching digital health implementation in rural Indonesia. Her PhD (cum laude, 2015) was on leprosy-related stigma in Indonesia, and she speaks basic Bahasa Indonesia [@peters2026]. Her research focuses on health-related stigma using transdisciplinary approaches and participatory action research. She is PI on projects in multiple countries including Indonesia, with partners including Universitas Indonesia and Soetomo Hospital.

## Key Details

- **Institution**: Athena Institute, Faculty of Science, Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam
- **Role**: Assistant Professor in Global Health; Member of Amsterdam Public Health (APH) Global Health programme
- **Field**: Health-related stigma, participatory methods, Global Health, leprosy, NTDs, mixed methods
- **Email/Contact**: via VU Amsterdam research portal
- **MSCA suitability**: High — she has supervised PhD students, has ongoing Indonesia projects, and has received Rubicon grant (NWO) indicating funding track record

## Top 3 First-Author Publications

1. Peters, R. M. H., et al. (2015). PhD dissertation on leprosy-related stigma in Indonesia (cum laude)

## Industry Collaboration Possibility

Limited direct industry collaboration. Her work focuses on community-based participatory research with NGOs (WarChild, CBM International) and academic partners. Industry collaboration would need to be built around digital health tools for stigma reduction or community health worker support.
