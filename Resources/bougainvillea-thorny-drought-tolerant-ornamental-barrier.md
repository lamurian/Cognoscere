## Summary

Bougainvillea (both B. spectabilis and B. glabra) is a rapid-growing, evergreen woody vine that forms dense, impenetrable barriers using its curved thorns [@northcarolinastateuniversityextension2024b]. It is extremely drought-tolerant once established, thrives in full sun, and produces spectacular colorful bracts (pink, purple, red, orange, white) for much of the year. Growth rate is rapid, reaching 15-40 ft in tropical conditions. Propagation is from semi-hardwood tip cuttings. Best uses: security hedges around property boundaries, impenetrable barriers along kitchen gardens, and highly ornamental entrance fences. Performs best when kept slightly dry -- too much water reduces flowering and encourages vegetative growth. Thrives in well-drained, slightly acidic soils. Avoid planting in high-traffic areas because the thorns cause painful injury. Requires training on a support structure for upright growth. Not suitable as livestock fodder. Can become invasive if not managed. Frost-sensitive. Symbiotic benefits: provides exceptional nesting habitat for birds within its thick thorny thickets. The flowers (bracts) attract bees, butterflies, and moths as pollinators. Salt-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal areas in West Timor. When pruned regularly, produces continuous flushes of new growth and flowers, creating a living wall that is both beautiful and functional.
