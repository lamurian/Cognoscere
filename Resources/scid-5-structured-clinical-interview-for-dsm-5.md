## Summary

The Structured Clinical Interview for DSM-5 (SCID-5) is a semi-structured diagnostic interview designed to systematically assess and diagnose mental disorders according to DSM-5 criteria. It is the most widely used gold standard reference instrument in clinical psychiatric research. The SCID-5 is clinician-administered, typically by trained mental health professionals, and covers all major DSM-5 diagnoses including depressive disorders, anxiety disorders, bipolar disorders, psychotic disorders, substance use disorders, and trauma-related disorders. The interview is modular, allowing clinicians to selectively administer relevant diagnostic modules.

The SCID-5-CV (Clinician Version) was published by First, Williams, Karg, and Spitzer (2015) as an updated version of the SCID-I for DSM-IV [@first2015]. The SCID-5-RV (Research Version) provides more detailed diagnostic coverage for research applications. As the semi-structured gold standard, the SCID-5 is typically used as the reference standard against which self-report instruments and screening tools are validated.

Psychometric properties of the SCID-5-CV demonstrate good to excellent test-retest reliability for most diagnoses (kappa 0.44-0.83), with the highest reliability for major depressive disorder and substance use disorders. Inter-rater reliability generally exceeds 0.80. For the earlier SCID-I (DSM-IV), sensitivity ranged from 0.75-0.92 and specificity from 0.90-0.99 against expert clinical diagnosis. The SCID-5 maintains similar psychometric standards but direct sensitivity/specificity data are more limited due to the SCID's role as the reference rather than index test. In validation studies, kappa values for SCID-5-RV ranged from 0.57-1.00 across diagnoses with overall agreement of 89-100%.

## Key Points

- Semi-structured clinician-administered interview, the gold standard for DSM-5 diagnosis
- Modular design covering all major DSM-5 diagnostic categories
- SCID-5-CV (Clinician Version) and SCID-5-RV (Research Version) available
- Test-retest kappa: 0.44-0.83 for most diagnoses [@first2015]
- Inter-rater reliability typically >0.80
- Used as reference standard in validation of self-report instruments (PHQ-9, GAD-7, etc.)
- Requires trained professionals; administration time 30-120 minutes depending on modules
