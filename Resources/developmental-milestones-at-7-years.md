## Summary

At seven years, the child demonstrates well-integrated physical, cognitive, and social-emotional competencies. Weight averages about 24.0 kg (52.9 lb) and height 123 cm (48.4 in). The seven-year-old reads fluently for learning (not just learning to read), writes multi-paragraph compositions, performs multi-step arithmetic, manages complex friendships, and participates in rule-governed team activities — marking readiness for middle childhood's expanding demands [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~24.0 kg (range 19.5–29.8 kg); median height ~123 cm. Weight gain ~2–3 kg/year; height ~5–6 cm/year. Mixed dentition continues — permanent incisors and first molars present; other primary teeth being replaced. Physical growth steady with widening individual variation. Onset of adrenarche (adrenal hormone changes) may begin subtly in some children, though visible signs are still years away.

**Emotional Maturity:** Shows self-control and emotional regulation markedly improved. Handles disappointment, frustration, and anger with words rather than physical outbursts most of the time. Understands moral concepts — fairness, honesty, responsibility. Experiences guilt and shame appropriately. Shows empathy for others' perspectives and situations. Peer group becomes increasingly influential; friendships are based on shared interests and loyalty. Can work collaboratively in groups of 4–6. Developing sense of competence and mastery — takes pride in accomplishments. Self-critical tendencies may increase; benefits from emphasis on effort over innate ability [@misirliyan2023].

**Psychological Development:** Concrete operational stage firmly established. The child uses logical reasoning about concrete situations. Understands conservation of number, mass, length, area, and liquid volume. Can seriate (order objects by size, weight, etc.) and classify hierarchically. Understands cause and effect relationships. Reads to learn — comprehends non-fiction texts, follows written instructions. Writes multi-paragraph compositions with topic sentences and supporting details. Performs addition and subtraction with regrouping; beginning multiplication and division. Tells time accurately on analog clock. Understands money and can make change. Uses maps and understands cardinal directions. Attention span 30–40 minutes.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: physically coordinated and confident in movements; rides bicycle with skill; swims independently; participates in competitive sports with understanding of strategy; dances with rhythm and coordination; develops sport-specific skills (shooting basket, kicking soccer ball accurately, catching fly balls); hand-eye coordination well developed. Fine motor: handwriting is neat, consistent, and automatic; draws detailed, proportional pictures with perspective; uses tools and crafts with precision; may play a musical instrument with growing proficiency; types with proper finger placement; builds sophisticated models and constructions; sews, knits, or does other complex crafts [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

**Speech and Language Development:** Fully mature speech and language for conversational purposes; vocabulary 7000–10000+ words; reads with full comprehension — can summarise, predict, and infer; writes multi-paragraph essays, stories, and reports; uses complex sentence structures, varied vocabulary, and appropriate register; engages in debates and persuasive arguments; understands irony, sarcasm, and figurative language; gives formal oral presentations; learning to adjust communication style for different audiences; may be proficient in a second language [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: significant motor coordination difficulties; unable to ride bicycle or participate in age-typical physical activities; handwriting remains illegible or extremely laboured; reading well below grade level; cannot write a coherent paragraph; cannot perform basic arithmetic (addition/subtraction within 20); significant speech sound errors affecting intelligibility; cannot maintain friendships or is consistently rejected by peers; persistent behaviour problems at school; cannot follow classroom routines; extreme anxiety, withdrawal, or aggression; cannot focus for 15+ minutes; persistent enuresis or encopresis; significant learning difficulties in one or more academic areas; loss of previously acquired skills.
