## Summary

Online peer support for youth mental health works through five interconnected mechanisms, according to a systematic scoping review by Yuan et al. [@yuan2025]. The review analysed 24 studies covering platforms from forums to social media, targeting general and specific mental health problems in young people aged 10-25.

The five mechanisms are: (1) unidirectional and reciprocal interaction — peer support flows through both one-way guidance (early stages) and mutual exchange (established relationships); (2) knowledge about mental health — experiential knowledge from lived experience is more accessible than clinical knowledge, reducing stigma and fear; (3) understanding — shared experience enables self-understanding, understanding others, and feeling understood, creating a sense of solidarity and normality; (4) social support — both informational (coping strategies, suggestions) and emotional (encouragement, acceptance, validation), available 24/7; (5) external online community environment — anonymity, non-judgement, safety, and sense of belonging create a virtual microsystem that facilitates help-seeking.

The review also identified negative aspects: over-empathy and emotional exhaustion from prolonged exposure to triggering content, misinformation from unqualified sources, excessive directive support creating power imbalances, over-reliance on virtual friends leading to real-world disconnection, and community negativity from unsupportive peers. Moderation, content filtering, and balanced online-offline integration are key safeguards.

## Key Points

- Five mechanisms: interaction, experiential knowledge, understanding, social support, community environment
- Experiential knowledge from lived experience is more accessible and destigmatising than clinical knowledge
- Anonymity and non-judgement are critical external conditions enabling help-seeking
- Risks include misinformation, emotional contagion, over-reliance on virtual support, and negative peer interactions
- Effective design requires moderation, user control over content, and balanced online-offline integration
