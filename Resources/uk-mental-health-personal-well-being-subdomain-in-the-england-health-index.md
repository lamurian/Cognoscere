The Personal Well-being subdomain (Pe.4) is a separate subdomain from Mental Health (Pe.5) within the England Health Index Healthy People domain. Pe.4 measures subjective well-being using the ONS4 national wellbeing questions: life satisfaction, life worthwhileness, happiness yesterday, and anxiety yesterday. These questions are collected through the Annual Population Survey (APS) and ask respondents to rate each on a 0-10 scale where 0 is not at all and 10 is completely [@frenisterrantino2022].

The four ONS4 measures capture evaluative well-being (life satisfaction), eudaimonic well-being (worthwhileness), and experienced well-being (happiness and anxiety). In the Health Index, anxiety is reverse-coded (higher scores = lower anxiety) so that all indicators are health-directed. Data is reported at UTLA level as mean scores. After normalization to z-scores anchored to the 2015 baseline year, the four indicators are aggregated using time-series factor analysis weights into the Pe.4 subdomain score [@frenisterrantino2022].

Pe.4 contributes alongside Pe.5 (Mental Health) to the Healthy People domain, but they measure different constructs: Pe.4 captures population subjective well-being while Pe.5 captures mental illness prevalence and mortality. This distinction reflects the WHO health definition encompassing both positive mental health and absence of mental disorder [@frenisterrantino2022].
