The War-Khasi people of Meghalaya, India cultivate living root bridges from Ficus elastica by guiding aerial roots through hollow bamboo tubes across river gaps. The roots take 10-15 years to become fully functional, span over 100 feet (30m), hold up to 50 people, and have an expected lifespan of 500-600 years [@wikipediacontributors2025a]. These bridges are naturally self-renewing and self-strengthening as roots grow thicker. This indigenous technique demonstrates that living tree structures can function as load-bearing infrastructure for centuries, making it relevant for barricade and wall applications [@middleton2019].
