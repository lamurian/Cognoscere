Developer-grade platforms like Cloudinary and ImageKit offer free tiers with on-the-fly compression, automatic WebP/AVIF conversion, global CDN delivery, and URL-based image transformations. These are designed for production websites, not casual sharing [@openreplayteam2026].

Cloudinary provides 25 credits monthly (roughly 25 GB storage, 25 GB bandwidth, 25,000 transformations). Its killer feature is URL-parameter-based manipulation — resize, crop, format conversion, AI background removal — all at request time via CDN. The learning curve is steeper but unmatched for dynamic image workflows [@sant2026].

ImageKit offers 20 GB bandwidth monthly with real-time transformations and automatic format selection, with a more approachable dashboard. Vercel Blob provides simple CDN-backed storage integrated into the Vercel ecosystem (~1 GB storage free). Cloudflare Images has excellent CDN performance but no permanent free storage tier [@openreplayteam2026]. Free tiers suffice for small-to-medium production sites but plan for paid upgrades as traffic grows.
