## Summary

The WHO STARS (Sustainable Technology for Adolescents and youth to Reduce Stress) chatbot is a transdiagnostic digital psychological intervention for young people aged 15-21 experiencing psychological distress in low- and middle-income countries (LMICs). Developed through extensive human-centred design across Pakistan, Palestine, Jamaica, South Africa, Nepal, and Jordan, the chatbot addresses a critical gap: 14% of young people globally live with mental disorders, yet fewer than 25% receive treatment in LMICs [@keyan2025].

The intervention uses a pre-programmed decision-tree chatbot (not AI-based, for safety and fidelity) that delivers 10 sessions across five weeks. Content covers: emotion identification, emotion regulation (breathing, grounding), behavioural activation, problem-solving (STOP-THINK-GO strategy), supportive self-talk, and relapse prevention. Users interact with fictional characters who face relatable problems—school stress, unemployment, relationship conflict—and learn skills by guiding these characters through challenges. A "mood check-in" at each session personalises the experience.

Key design features: session length of 15-20 minutes; uses videos, audio, text, and quizzes for varied learning styles; password-protected for privacy; works on basic smartphones with low data requirements; optional human guidance via 15-minute weekly phone calls from trained non-specialist helpers. The chatbot is currently being tested in randomised controlled trials in Jordan and Lithuania [@keyan2025].

The human-centred design process involved iterative testing with 236 adolescents, 73 community members, and 9 experts across settings. End-users consistently preferred: a conversational format, relatable fictional characters, short sessions (5-10 min ideal), and videos over written information. Stigma reduction through non-diagnostic language was a central design principle.

## Key Points

- WHO-developed transdiagnostic chatbot for youth (15-21) in LMICs
- 10 sessions over 5 weeks: CBT-based skills for stress, mood, anger, problem-solving
- Co-designed with 236 adolescents across 6 countries using human-centred design
- Pre-programmed (non-AI) decision tree ensures safety and content fidelity
- Optional human guidance via 5 weekly 15-min calls from trained non-specialist helpers
- Stigma reduction through non-diagnostic language and relatable fictional character stories
- Currently in RCTs in Jordan and Lithuania
