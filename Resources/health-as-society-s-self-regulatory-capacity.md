## Summary

The research synthesised from multiple frameworks—Community-Oriented Primary Care, Salutogenesis, Ubuntu philosophy, and caregiver burden studies—converges on a coherent paradigm that aligns with the user's philosophical framework.

**Health starts from the individual and expands to community.** COPC demonstrated that effective healthcare requires working with individuals in their homes and communities, not isolating them in clinical settings [@marcus2016]. The Pholela model assigned community health workers to specific homesteads, building relationships and trust before delivering clinical services.

**Healthcare is delivered on the basis of trust.** COPC succeeded because local people were hired as community health workers, creating cultural alignment and trust [@marcus2016]. Ubuntu philosophy frames health as inherently relational: trust is built through mutual recognition and reciprocity [@udah2025]. The salutogenic model identifies social networks as Generalized Resistance Resources essential for health [@kenya2026].

**The highest health risk lies within the people, not the direct cause of illness.** In communicable diseases, propagation occurs through direct contact. In NCDs, the caregiver burden literature shows that adverse events propagate through social networks—caregivers experience worsening health, financial strain, and psychosocial distress [@saeed2024]. The family and social contacts of the patient become secondary patients themselves.

**To cure a patient means to care for their immediate social contacts.** The Karks' approach was to treat families, not isolated individuals. At Pholela, family consultations allowed the health team to discuss clinical and home issues together [@marcus2016]. Ubuntu philosophy asserts that individual personhood is constituted through relationships—healing must therefore address the relational network.

**Health is not the absence of illness but society's self-regulatory capacity.** Antonovsky's Salutogenic Model defines health as the capacity to adapt, manage, and find meaning—properties that are socially nurtured through community support, participation, and shared resources [@kenya2026]. The Pholela data showed that community-level interventions, not individual clinical encounters, drove measurable improvements in infant mortality.

## Key Points

- Four independent frameworks converge on the same paradigm: COPC (practice), Salutogenesis (theory), Ubuntu (philosophy), caregiver burden studies (evidence).
- Health is a collective, relational property—not an individual state.
- Both communicable and non-communicable diseases propagate adverse events through social networks.
- Trust between healthcare providers, patients, and communities is the foundational mechanism for effective care.
- Curative approaches must integrate with society, not isolate individuals from their social context.
