# Official Weekly Economic Data Sources for Indonesia

## Summary

Several Indonesian government and regulatory bodies publish weekly or daily economic data freely accessible to the public. Bank Indonesia (BI) publishes the JISDOR USD/IDR reference rate daily at https://www.bi.go.id/en/statistik/informasi-kurs/jisdor/, weekly news releases covering official reserve assets and monetary indicators at https://www.bi.go.id/en/publikasi/ruang-media/news-release/, and weekly International Reserves and Foreign Currency Liquidity (IRFCL) data at https://www.bi.go.id/en/statistik/sdds/. BI's Economic and Market Data portal is at https://www.bi.go.id/en/iru/economic-market-data/.

BPS Statistics Indonesia publishes Weekly Retail Price Movement of Selected Staple Goods covering rice, cooking oil, beef, chicken, eggs, chili, and onion across provincial capitals, accessible at https://www.bps.go.id/. The Ministry of Trade runs SP2KP (Sistem Pemantauan Pasar dan Kebutuhan Pokok), a real-time daily price tracking system for 10+ essential food commodities at https://sp2kp.kemendag.go.id/. The Ministry of Finance's DJPPR publishes weekly Government Securities (SUN) auction results every Tuesday at https://djppr.kemenkeu.go.id/. The Indonesia Stock Exchange (IDX) publishes weekly and daily statistics including JCI, sectoral indices, trading volume, market capitalization, and foreign net flows at https://www.idx.co.id/en/market-data/statistical-reports/statistics/.

## Key Points

- All official weekly data sources are free and publicly accessible without registration
- BI and BPS are the most authoritative sources for monetary, reserve, and price data
- SP2KP provides real-time food price tracking essential for high-frequency inflation monitoring
- Weekly SUN auction results signal government borrowing costs and investor demand for Indonesian debt
- IDX weekly data tracks foreign capital flows and overall market sentiment
- Most data is downloadable in Excel or PDF format
