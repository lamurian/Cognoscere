## Summary

Lannea coromandelica is the most popular living fence tree in Eastern Indonesia, with exceptionally fast establishment from large cuttings that root within a few months [@reville2017]. Growth is rapid in the first year, reaching 2-3 meters within a single wet season. The tree is dry deciduous, losing leaves in the dry season, but remains evergreen with groundwater access. Best uses include boundary markers, goat fodder during the August-November dry season, firewood from periodic pruning, and nurse plant function that gradually increases hedgerow biodiversity by catching bird-dispersed seeds. Plant at the start of the wet season from cuttings 1-2m long and 5-10cm diameter. Thrives in any soil type, tolerates salt spray, needs no fertilizer or irrigation after establishment. Avoid planting where evergreen windbreaks are needed since it drops leaves in dry season. Requires pruning every 1-2 years. Symbiotic benefits include attracting seed-dispersing birds, adding organic matter to poor soils through leaf litter, reducing soil evaporation under its canopy, and improving soil structure through root penetration.
