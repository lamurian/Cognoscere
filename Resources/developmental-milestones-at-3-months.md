## Summary

At three months, the infant becomes an increasingly active social partner. Weight reaches approximately 6.1 kg (13.4 lb) and length 61 cm (24.0 in). The infant now sustains longer periods of alertness and engages in proto-conversations — back-and-forth vocal exchanges with caregivers [@worldhealthorganization2006].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~6.1 kg (range 5.1–7.2 kg); median length ~61 cm. Growth velocity remains high at 150–200 g/week. Head circumference gain slows slightly from the peak newborn rate.

**Emotional Maturity:** Smiles spontaneously at people; not just in response. Enjoys playing with people and may cry when interaction stops. Begins to imitate some facial expressions. Shows early signs of recognising familiar vs. unfamiliar people — though true stranger anxiety is still months away.

**Psychological Development:** Piaget's sensorimotor substage 2 (primary circular reactions, 1–4 months). The infant repeats actions involving their own body that produce interesting results. Recognises familiar objects and people at a distance. Tracks moving objects smoothly through 180° arc. Shows interest in own hands and feet.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: raises head and chest when on tummy, supporting weight on forearms; head control improving — head lag decreasing when pulled to sit. Fine motor: opens and shuts hands; brings hands to mouth; bats at dangling objects; begins to hold a rattle briefly when placed in hand.

**Speech and Language Development:** Coos and makes pleasurable sounds; laughs and squeals. Responds to changes in tone of voice. Begins babbling-like sounds resembling speech. Turns head deliberately toward sound source [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: not smiling at people; not following moving objects with eyes; not bringing hands to mouth; marked head lag when pulled to sit; stiff or very floppy limbs; no cooing or pleasure sounds; crossing of weight percentiles downward on growth chart; not responding to loud sounds.
