## Summary

This paper reports the first Meta-Analytic Confirmatory Factor Analysis (MACFA) of the Perceived Stress Scale (PSS-10 and PSS-14), pooling 76 independent samples from 57 unique studies. The total sample comprised 28,632 participants for the PSS-14 and 46,053 for the PSS-10 [@ylmazkoar2023].

The MACFA, performed on a pooled correlation matrix generated via random-effects meta-analysis, confirmed that the correlated two-factor model best explains the factor structure of the PSS. The two factors correspond to perceived helplessness (negatively worded items) and perceived self-efficacy (positively worded items). The results demonstrated strict measurement invariance across age groups and clinical status, meaning the scale measures the same construct in comparable ways across these populations. Internal consistency was high across all models as assessed by omega coefficients [@ylmazkoar2023].

## Key Points

- Correlated two-factor model (helplessness + self-efficacy) confirmed as optimal for both PSS-10 and PSS-14.
- Strict measurement invariance achieved across age and clinical status, supporting valid cross-group comparisons.
- High internal consistency (omega) for both versions.
- First MACFA of the PSS, providing the strongest psychometric evidence to date for its two-factor structure.

## Relevance

This is the most comprehensive psychometric synthesis of the PSS. The confirmed two-factor structure and evidence of measurement invariance strengthen confidence in using the PSS-10 and PSS-14 across diverse populations, which is especially valuable for cross-cultural stress research in global mental health [@ylmazkoar2023].
