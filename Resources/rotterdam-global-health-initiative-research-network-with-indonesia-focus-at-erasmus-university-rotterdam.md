## Summary

The Rotterdam Global Health Initiative (RGHI) at Erasmus University Rotterdam (EUR) is an interdisciplinary academic network with partners across 30+ LMICs, including Indonesia [@erasmusuniversityrotterdam2026]. RGHI focuses on health equity, universal health coverage, health systems financing, and disease control. It has published research on Indonesia's JKN national health insurance expansion. RGHI coordinates global health research projects and serves as an entry point for connecting with EUR-based researchers working on Indonesia-related health systems and digital health.

While RGHI itself does not directly employ postdocs, its member institutes — including Erasmus School of Health Policy & Management (ESHPM) and Erasmus MC — host postdoctoral researchers and can serve as MSCA host institutions.

## Key Points

- **URL**: https://www.eur.nl/en/research/research-groups-initiatives/rotterdam-global-health-initiative
- **Salary**: Standard Dutch postdoc scale EUR 3,897-5,331/month (scale 10) at EUR; Erasmus MC postdocs EUR 3,724-5,867/month
- **Requirements**: PhD in public health, health sciences, health economics, or related field; LMIC research experience
- **Description**: Research network coordinating interdisciplinary global health research including Indonesia-based projects on UHC and health systems
- **Travel to Indonesia**: Very likely — active partnerships with Indonesian institutions
- **Remote Work**: Partial depending on specific position/project
- **Approach**: Contact RGHI coordinator or member researchers to discuss MSCA hosting or existing funded positions
