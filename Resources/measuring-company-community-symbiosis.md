Measuring company-community symbiosis requires a multidimensional approach. Social capital measurement provides a validated framework through four dimensions: municipality place attachment, local community impact (LCI), intrapersonal social capital, and collective social capital. LCI is the strongest predictor of pro-social consumer behaviors [@garczarekbk2026]. The Social License to Operate framework from extractive industries measures community acceptance on a spectrum from withdrawal to psychological identification. At the economic level, local multiplier effects, employment localization ratios, and supply chain localization provide quantitative indicators. Relational embeddedness can be assessed across three perspectives: economic (contract formality, transaction frequency), organizational (network density, tie strength), and interactionist (shared practices, institutional alignment) [@cyr2022]. Assessment must include both positive indicators (job creation, local sourcing) and negative indicators (pollution levels, displacement rates, health outcomes) to capture the ambivalence of symbiosis [@cuevasvalenzuela2022].
