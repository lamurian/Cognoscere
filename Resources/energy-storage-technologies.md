Grid energy storage is essential for managing the variability of renewable energy sources. Storage technologies span from seconds-duration frequency regulation to seasonal storage. As of 2023, pumped hydro dominates (181 GW installed), followed by utility-scale batteries (88 GW combined including behind-the-meter).

## Battery Storage

**Lithium-Ion**: Dominant for short-duration storage (under 8 hours). Costs have fallen 90% since 2010. Lithium iron phosphate (LFP) chemistry is preferred for grid applications due to lower cost, longer life, and better safety than nickel-based variants.

**Flow Batteries**: Vanadium redox flow batteries store energy in liquid electrolytes in separate tanks. Suitable for 4-12 hour storage. Lower energy efficiency (60-75%) but longer life and independent scaling of power and energy capacity.

**Emerging Chemistries**: Sodium-ion batteries may be 20-30% cheaper than lithium-ion at scale, using abundant materials. Iron-air batteries promise multi-day storage but are not yet commercial.

## Pumped Hydro Storage

The most mature large-scale storage technology (75-85% efficiency). Excess electricity pumps water to an upper reservoir; release generates power on demand. Requires specific geography with elevation difference. Global capacity of 181 GW as of 2023. Floating solar on reservoirs can improve efficiency.

## Compressed Air Energy Storage (CAES)

Compresses air into underground caverns; expanding air drives turbines. Advanced CAES achieves 60-80% efficiency by capturing compression heat. Three commercial plants operating in China as of 2023.

## Green Hydrogen

Produced via electrolysis of water using renewable electricity. Can be stored for weeks or seasons in salt caverns or depleted gas fields. Round-trip efficiency is approximately 41% when reconverted to electricity via fuel cells. Best suited for long-duration/seasonal storage where other options are uneconomical.

## Thermal Storage

Carnot batteries store electricity as heat for later reconversion via thermodynamic cycles. Concentrated solar power (CSP) plants use molten salt storage to provide dispatchable solar energy. Building thermal mass and hot water tanks provide low-cost load-shifting.

## Storage Economics

Lithium-ion battery pack prices fell 19% per doubling of cumulative capacity. At current trajectories, storage will be critical for grids with over 40% variable renewables. Short-duration storage (under 4 hours) is already cost-effective for frequency regulation and arbitrage. Longer-duration storage requires further cost reductions or alternative technologies.
