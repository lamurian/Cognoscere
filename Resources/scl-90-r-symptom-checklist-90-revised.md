## Summary

The Symptom Checklist-90-Revised (SCL-90-R) is a 90-item self-report instrument designed to measure psychological symptoms across nine primary symptom dimensions: somatization, obsessive-compulsive, interpersonal sensitivity, depression, anxiety, hostility, phobic anxiety, paranoid ideation, and psychoticism. Each item is rated on a 5-point Likert scale from 0 (not at all) to 4 (extremely), reflecting distress over the past week. The instrument yields three global indices: the Global Severity Index (GSI), the Positive Symptom Distress Index (PSDI), and the Positive Symptom Total (PST).

Developed by Derogatis (1994), the SCL-90-R has been validated extensively across clinical and non-clinical populations [@derogatis1994]. Internal consistency ranges from 0.70 to 0.90 across subscales, with the GSI demonstrating the highest reliability. Test-retest reliability ranges from 0.80 to 0.90 over one week. In clinical application, a GSI T-score >=63 (based on adult non-patient norms) is typically used as the cutoff for clinically significant distress.

Comparative psychometric analyses in patients with affective disorders found sensitivity approximately 0.80-0.88 for the GSI in detecting psychiatric caseness, with specificity of 0.70-0.85 depending on the population and comparison standard [@prinz2013]. The SCL-90-R is useful as a broadband screening instrument for general psychopathology rather than as a diagnostic tool for specific disorders. The SCL-K-9 (9-item short version) shows comparable discrimination for specific conditions such as binge eating pathology.

## Key Points

- 90 items covering 9 symptom dimensions, 0-4 scale, past week recall
- Three global indices: GSI, PSDI, PST
- Internal consistency 0.70-0.90, test-retest 0.80-0.90 [@derogatis1994]
- GSI cutoff T>=63: sensitivity ~0.80-0.88, specificity ~0.70-0.85
- Broadband screening instrument, not designed for specific disorder diagnosis
- Psychometric weaknesses have been noted despite widespread use
- Short versions (SCL-K-9, SCL-27) available with comparable properties
