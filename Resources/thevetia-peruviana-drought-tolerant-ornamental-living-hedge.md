## Summary

Thevetia peruviana (yellow oleander, Cascabela thevetia) is a medium-growth-rate, evergreen tropical shrub or small tree (8-20 ft) used for hedges and living fences in dry regions [@northcarolinastateuniversityextension2024a]. It is highly drought-tolerant, handles full sun to partial shade, and produces showy yellow flowers from summer to fall. In West Timor and other drier parts of NTT, it persists where many ornamentals cannot. Best uses: ornamental hedge and privacy screen, drought-tolerant landscaping, and as a visual barrier due to its dense, willow-like foliage. Its low water requirements make it excellent for water-scarce environments. Plant from stem cuttings in well-drained soil. Avoid planting where children or pets access the area -- all parts of the plant are highly toxic if ingested, and sap causes skin reactions [@northcarolinastateuniversityextension2024a]. Smoke from burning plant material is also toxic. Not suitable for animal fodder. Cannot tolerate frost. Flowers are fragrant and attract some bird species that feed on the fruits despite toxicity. Symbiotic benefits: provides dense evergreen cover throughout the year, flowers support local pollinators. Low-maintenance barrier with high ornamental value. Needs regular pruning to maintain hedge shape. Its deep root system helps bind soil on slopes.
