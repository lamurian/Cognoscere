## Summary

The correlation between positive affect and perceived social support is consistently positive but small-to-moderate in magnitude. Reported Pearson r values for positive affect with social support range from r = 0.15 to r = 0.30.

Acoba @acoba2024 found: Family Support with PANAS positive affect (r = 0.249, p < 0.01), Significant Other Support with positive affect (r = 0.273, p < 0.01), and Friend Support with positive affect (r = 0.201, p < 0.01). Yang et al. @yang2020 reported r = 0.151 (p < 0.01) between MSPSS total and PANAS positive affect.

These small-to-moderate positive correlations suggest that perceived social support and positive affect are related but distinct constructs, sharing 2-8% variance. The differential pattern by support source (significant other > family > friend) is consistent across studies.

## Key Points
- Positive affect and social support: r ≈ 0.15 to 0.30 (small to moderate positive)
- The asserted bound |r| < 0.70 in test #5 covers the empirical range, but is unnecessarily wide
- The ad-hoc bound |r| < 0.5 is more reasonable but still generous
- The weakest association is with friend support (r ≈ 0.15-0.20)
