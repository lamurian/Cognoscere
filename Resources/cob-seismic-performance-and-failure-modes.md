Cob can fail in two distinct modes: brittle fracture along shear planes (like concrete) or ductile bulging (like compacted clay), depending on soil composition and moisture content [@pullen2009]. Its modulus of elasticity is highly variable (14-207 MPa, COV 127%), making stiffness prediction challenging. For rammed earth, cracking inter-storey drift is approximately 0.08%, with field damping ratios of 2.5-4% [@peri2021]. The primary seismic vulnerability is out-of-plane wall collapse. However, cob's monolithic construction (no mortar joints) provides better integrity than adobe brick. Reinforcement strategies include internal timber posts, textile-reinforced mortars (TRM), surface meshes, and connection to timber roof diaphragms that tie walls together [@peri2021].
