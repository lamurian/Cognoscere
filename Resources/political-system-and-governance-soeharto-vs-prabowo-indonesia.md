## Political System and Governance: Soeharto vs Prabowo Indonesia

### Soeharto Era (1966-1998)

Authoritarian military regime with controlled elections. Golkar was the electoral vehicle, consistently winning ~60-70% of the vote. Only two opposition parties were allowed (PPP and PDI), both tightly controlled. The military (ABRI) had 100 appointed seats in parliament through Dwifungsi. Presidents were elected unanimously by the MPR every five years. Press was completely controlled through SIUPP licensing. Civil society was corporatized through state-controlled organizations (KORPRI for civil servants, SPSI for workers, MUI for clerics). Term limits did not exist in practice -- Soeharto served seven consecutive terms. Opposition was criminalized through the Anti-Subversion Law. Total control of the judicial system.

### Prabowo Era (2024-present)

Formal democracy with regular, competitive elections since 1998. Prabowo won the 2024 election with ~58% of the vote in a three-candidate race. The Red and White Cabinet is the largest since the 1960s (48 ministers). His coalition (KIM Plus) commands a supermajority in parliament. Concerns exist about democratic backsliding: the Constitutional Court changed age requirements for Gibran's candidacy while chaired by Jokowi's brother-in-law. The 2025 Armed Forces Act revision re-expanded military roles in civilian positions. Press freedom, while better than Soeharto, has seen deterioration since the Jokowi era. Critics point to Prabowo's authoritarian tendencies, past human rights record, and consolidation of power.

### Key Differences

- **Regime type**: Soeharto was an outright authoritarian regime. Prabowo operates within a democratic framework with checks and balances.
- **Elections**: Soeharto's elections were shams with predetermined outcomes. Prabowo won a genuinely competitive (though imperfect) election.
- **Press freedom**: Soeharto banned critical media. Under Prabowo, media is independent but faces economic and political pressure.
- **Civil society**: Soeharto required all organizations to adopt Pancasila as sole principle. Today's civil society is diverse and vocal.
- **Military role**: Soeharto institutionalized Dwifungsi. Prabowo's reform partially restores military civilian roles, though far from New Order levels.
- **Opposition**: Soeharto eliminated opposition. Under Prabowo, opposition parties function in parliament.

### Similarities

- Both maintain/ed strong executive power with weak checks from parliament.
- Both use/d military backgrounds to legitimize political authority.
- Both built/are building large coalition governments to minimize opposition.
- Both have/had personal control over their political parties (Golkar under Soeharto; Gerindra under Prabowo).
- Both prioritize/zed stability and development over democratic liberalization.
- Both faced/face allegations of using legal instruments to target political opponents.
