## Summary

At four months, the infant enters a period of rapid motor and social expansion. Weight averages 6.7 kg (14.8 lb) and length 63 cm (24.8 in). Rolling over typically emerges, and hand-eye coordination improves markedly as the infant reaches for and grasps objects purposefully [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~6.7 kg (range 5.6–7.9 kg); median length ~63 cm. Birth weight typically doubled by this age. Head circumference ~41 cm. Growth velocity begins to decelerate slightly from peak newborn rates.

**Emotional Maturity:** Smiles spontaneously, especially at people. Likes to play with people and may cry when playing stops. Copies some facial expressions and movements. Shows pleasure by laughing aloud. Begins to display distinct emotional expressions — joy, anger, surprise distinguishable by caregivers.

**Psychological Development:** Middle of Piaget's substage 2: primary circular reactions become more coordinated. Discovers hands and feet as parts of self. Recognises familiar people at a distance. Responds to affection. Shows interest in novel stimuli. Beginning of object permanence precursors — looks for partially hidden objects.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: rolls from front to back; holds head steady without support when held; pushes down on legs when feet are on a hard surface; may roll from back to side. Fine motor: reaches for and grasps objects (palmar grasp); brings hands to midline; shakes a rattle; brings objects to mouth.

**Speech and Language Development:** Babbles with expression; copies sounds. Responds to tone of voice — smiles to happy tone, may cry to angry tone. Notices toys that make sounds. Pays attention to music. Laughs and makes gurgling sounds when alone or playing [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: not rolling in either direction; persistent head lag; not reaching for or grasping objects; not bringing objects to mouth; no laughter or pleasurable sounds; no social smile; crossed eyes most of the time (persistent strabismus); not responding to sounds or voices; not pushing down with legs when feet placed on firm surface.
