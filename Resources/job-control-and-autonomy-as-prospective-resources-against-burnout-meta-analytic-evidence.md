## Summary

A 2024 meta-analysis of 47 prospective studies (N=39,467 European healthcare employees) provides robust longitudinal evidence that job control and autonomy function as key job resources protecting against strain and promoting well-being over time [@marzocchi2024]. The study applied the IGLO framework (Individual, Group, Leadership, Organisation) to categorize job demands and resources, testing their prospective associations with well-being outcomes.

**Key quantitative findings:**

- **Job control/autonomy** was the most frequently studied group-level resource (12 independent samples)
- **Group-level resources** (including team autonomy/decision authority) showed stronger negative associations with strain (ρ = -0.25 to -0.40) than leadership-level or organisation-level resources
- **Hindrance demands** (role ambiguity, role conflict, bureaucratic constraints) had stronger detrimental effects on well-being (ρ = 0.30–0.40) than challenge demands (workload, cognitive demands)
- **Threat demands** (violence, aggression) showed the strongest associations with strain (ρ = 0.35–0.50)
- Baseline resources predicted follow-up well-being, confirming their **protective/prospective role**
- **Time lag did not significantly moderate** the associations, indicating stability of effects

The meta-analysis used a three-wave prospective design framework, providing stronger causal inference than cross-sectional studies. The finding that group-level autonomy (shared decision-making authority, team control over work methods) has stronger protective effects than individual autonomy suggests that **organisational interventions increasing collective decision-making authority** may yield greater burnout prevention benefits than individual job redesign alone.

This aligns with the job crafting evidence from Gordon et al. (2018) where team-level job crafting (improving teamwork and quality of care) significantly improved work engagement and reduced emotional exhaustion [@cohen2023]. It also connects to the bullying intervention (Li et al., 2023) where "managing tasks and workload" and team coordination were key intervention targets [@li2023].

## Key Points

- 47 prospective studies, N=39,467, European healthcare sector
- Job control/autonomy is the most studied group-level resource (12 samples)
- Group-level resources > leadership/organisation-level resources for strain prevention
- Hindrance demands more damaging than challenge demands
- Resources protect prospectively — baseline predicts follow-up well-being
- Time lag does not moderate effects — stable protective role
- Supports team/collective autonomy interventions over individual-only approaches
