The architecture of Indonesia reflects the remarkable diversity of cultural, historical, and geographic influences across the archipelago. Traditional houses of several hundred ethnic groups each have their own history and social significance [@wikipediacontributors2025]. Most share Austronesian ancestry: timber longhouses on stilts with steep roofs, seen in Batak and Torajan houses.

Major foreign influences include Indian (Hindu-Buddhist), Chinese, Arab (Islamic), and European (colonial) traditions, all reinterpreted locally [@anggakara2020]. The houses demonstrate local ingenuity in spatial organisation and environmental relations.
