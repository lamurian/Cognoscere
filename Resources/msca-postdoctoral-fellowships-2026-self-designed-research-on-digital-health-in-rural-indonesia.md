## Summary

The Marie Sklodowska-Curie Actions (MSCA) Postdoctoral Fellowships 2026 call, launched on 9 April 2026 with a budget of EUR 399.05 million, is the single most relevant opportunity for a PhD graduate wanting to design their own postdoc on digital health implementation in rural Indonesia while based at a Dutch university [@europeancommission2026]. The programme funds nearly 1,600 postdoctoral researchers for 1-3 years, covering salary, research costs, and mobility allowance. Applications are made jointly with a host institution — any Dutch university or research institute.

This is the best route because it allows proposing your own research question rather than fitting into an existing project. Dutch universities strong in global health (Erasmus MC, Amsterdam UMC/AIGHD, Radboudumc, Utrecht University, Wageningen University) could serve as host. The fellowship includes European (staying in Europe) and Global (spending part of the fellowship in Indonesia) options.

## Key Points

- **Deadline**: 9 September 2026
- **URL**: https://marie-sklodowska-curie-actions.ec.europa.eu/funding/msca-postdoctoral-fellowships-2026
- **Salary**: ~EUR 5,400-6,500/month living allowance + ~EUR 600/month mobility + research costs, tax-free; 30% ruling may apply in Netherlands
- **Requirements**: PhD at deadline; max 8 years post-PhD experience; must not have worked in host country >12 of past 36 months
- **Description**: Propose and conduct own research project under host PI supervision; includes training, publication, fieldwork opportunity
- **Travel to Indonesia**: Yes — Global Fellowship option allows up to 2 years in Indonesia + 1-year return phase at Dutch host
- **Remote Work**: Partial depending on host policy; significant in-person presence expected during return phase
