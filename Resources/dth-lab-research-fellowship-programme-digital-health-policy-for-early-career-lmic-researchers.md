## Summary

The Digital Transformations for Health Lab (DTH-Lab) Research Fellowship Programme offers six-month research fellowships for students and early career professionals (aged 30 or under) from low- and middle-income countries [@digitaltransformationsforhealthlab2026]. In 2026, eight research fellowships were offered on topics including national policies for young people's digital wellbeing, mapping national digital health services for youth, digital citizenship for health, AI competencies for the health workforce, and emerging roles in an AI-enabled health workforce.

The DTH-Lab was established to advance the recommendations of the Lancet and Financial Times Commission on Governing Health Futures 2030. The fellowship duration is 6 months (1 May to 31 October 2026). The deadline for the 2026 round has passed (12 April 2026), but the programme may run again in future years.

## Key Points

- **URL**: https://www.dthlab.org/articles/dth-lab-fellowship-programme-applications-open-now-2026
- **Salary**: Not publicly specified; fellowship covers research costs and stipend
- **Requirements**: Aged 30 or under; based in LMIC; CV, two referees, research proposal
- **Description**: Six-month research fellowship on digital health transformations; policy-oriented; online collaboration
- **Travel to Indonesia**: Remote fellowship — no travel required
- **Remote Work**: Fully remote fellowship, designed for LMIC-based researchers
- **Note**: 2026 round deadline passed (12 April 2026); monitor for future cohorts on DTH-Lab website
