## Summary

Several Android RPGs use step count from your phone's pedometer instead of GPS to power progression. They have zero location-based battery drain and work indoors, on treadmills, or anywhere. WalkScape is the most anticipated — an idle RPG inspired by RuneScape where walking trains 15+ skills like Woodcutting, Mining, Fishing, and Combat. Currently in closed beta with instant access via Patreon support. Pedometer-only, zero battery drain, deep skill system, marketplace, and online leaderboards. The closest match to Orna's progression depth without the GPS requirement.

Monster Walk is a creature-collecting RPG from Talofa Games (creators of Run Legends). Steps generate energy to explore a fantasy world, battle and befriend monsters, and build a base. No GPS, available in early access on Android and iOS. MistyWay is a hand-drawn fantasy RPG with 45 illustrated biomes, quests, treasures, and character progression — all powered by step count. Available now with no waitlist, no ads, and no GPS. Synced with Apple Health and Google Fit. The Walk is a finite audio thriller (65 episodes) unlocked by step count from Six to Start. First 5 episodes free, then one-time purchase. Light on RPG mechanics but strong narrative drive.

Compared to Orna, these pedometer-based games sacrifice location-based discovery and combat systems for universal accessibility and zero battery impact. WalkScape comes closest in progression depth (skill-based leveling instead of class trees) but is still in beta. MistyWay is the best immediately-available option with genuine RPG mechanics, though less deep than Orna. No pedometer game matches Orna's class-based combat system — the GPS requirement is inherent to that design.
