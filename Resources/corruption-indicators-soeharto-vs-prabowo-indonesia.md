## Corruption Indicators: Soeharto vs Prabowo Indonesia

### Soeharto Era (1966-1998)

Corruption under Soeharto (KKN -- Korupsi, Kolusi, Nepotisme) was systemic and reached the highest levels of government. Transparency International ranked Soeharto among the most corrupt leaders globally. Suharto family members controlled an estimated 36,000 km2 of real estate, free shares in 1,251 companies, and monopolies in cloves, cars, toll roads, and film distribution. A network of yayasans (foundations) extracted 'donations' from businesses. The Pertamina default of 1975 (US$15 billion) exemplified state-linked corruption. There was no effective anti-corruption institution. The legal system was weak with no effective way to enforce contracts or sue for bankruptcy.

### Prabowo Era (2024-present)

Indonesia's Corruption Perceptions Index (CPI) score is 37 out of 100 (2024), ranking 99th globally. This reflects ongoing serious corruption issues despite the existence of the KPK (Corruption Eradication Commission) established in 2002. Prabowo's presidency has been marked by concerns about potential backsliding. In January 2025, Presidential Instruction No. 1/2025 on budget efficiency cut US$18.8 billion, affecting public works, education, and health sectors. The Danantara sovereign wealth fund, launched in 2025, raised concerns about lack of oversight. The Red and White Cabinet is the largest since the 1960s, which critics argue increases patronage opportunities.

### Key Differences

- **Institutional framework**: Soeharto had zero anti-corruption institutions. Prabowo operates with the KPK, though its independence has been eroded since 2019.
- **Systemic vs individual**: Soeharto's corruption was institutionalized through family and cronies. Prabowo-era corruption involves established but weakened watchdog institutions.
- **International perception**: Soeharto was globally identified as corrupt. Indonesia's CPI rank has fluctuated but still scores poorly.
- **Transparency**: Soeharto era had no press freedom to report corruption. Today, despite concerns, media and civil society can expose corruption.
- **Accountability**: Soeharto died without facing trial for corruption. Prabowo faces active media scrutiny and KPK investigations of officials.

### Similarities

- Both eras are/were characterized by deep corruption in the political-business nexus.
- In both periods, the military had/has significant involvement in the economy, creating conflict of interest.
- Crony capitalism -- favoring connected business groups -- persists in both eras.
- The legal system remains weak in enforcing anti-corruption effectively in both periods.
