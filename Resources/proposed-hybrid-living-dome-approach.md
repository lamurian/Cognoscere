## Summary

A 20m living dome requires synthesizing multiple proven techniques. The proposed hybrid approach combines: (1) a reciprocal roundwood timber frame as permanent scaffold — reciprocal roofs reach 20m diameter [@voiceofnature2022]; (2) inosculated hardwood panels or ribs at structural nodes, adapted from ArborStructures panel method; (3) Baubotanik-style progressive load transfer where living trees planted at the base and along ribs gradually assume structural function over 15-30 years.

Species selection is critical. Long-lived hardwoods (Platanus, Quercus, Ficus elastica) enable multi-century lifespans, while willows grow faster but are shorter-lived. Living wood in compression has comparable strength to concrete, suggesting feasibility if enough cross-sectional area is achieved in mature trunks.

No single technique achieves both 20m height and centennial lifespan. The hybrid approach has no full-scale precedent but each component is separately proven. Estimated timeline: 4 years to initial occupancy (using timber frame + immature trees), 15-30 years to self-supporting, 500+ years lifespan with Ficus elastica [@wikipediacontributors2025a].
