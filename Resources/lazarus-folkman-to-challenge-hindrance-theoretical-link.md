## Summary

Lazarus & Folkman's Transactional Model [@lazarus1984] distinguishes problem-focused coping (managing the stressor) and emotion-focused coping (regulating emotional response). The Challenge-Hindrance Stressor Framework (CHSF) by Cavanaugh et al. [@cavanaugh2000] extends this by classifying demands as challenges (growth-promoting, controllable) or hindrances (growth-impeding, overwhelming). Challenge appraisal increases coping probability; hindrance decreases it. The simulation operationalises this through: appraisal weights (omega_c, omega_o) → challenge/hindrance classification via sigmoid transform → threshold evaluation → probabilistic coping success. Coping success integrates base rate, challenge bonus, hindrance penalty, and social influence [@bakker2017].
