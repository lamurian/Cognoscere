Perceived stress (PSS-10) shows a moderate negative correlation with life satisfaction as measured by the Satisfaction with Life Scale (SWLS). Bodys-Cupak et al. [@bodyscupak2022] reported Spearman rho = -0.376 (p < 0.001, n = 307) in Polish nursing students. In their regression model, life satisfaction (β = -0.283, p < 0.001) was a significant predictor of PSS-10 alongside optimism, while self-efficacy and self-esteem were non-significant when these two were controlled.

This moderate correlation (rho ≈ -0.38, ~14% shared variance) aligns with COR theory: individuals with greater resource reservoirs (including life satisfaction) appraise stressors as less taxing. Life satisfaction reflects a cognitive-judgmental evaluation of one's life circumstances, which overlaps with but is distinct from affective resources like optimism. Source: @bodyscupak2022.
