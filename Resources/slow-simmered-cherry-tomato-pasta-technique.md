## Summary

The slow-simmered cherry tomato pasta sauce, popularized by Yotam Ottolenghi in his cookbook "Ottolenghi Simple" [@kimball2019], relies on simmering halved cherry tomatoes in a mixture of olive oil and water over low heat for nearly an hour. The water and oil together slow the cooking process, preventing browning while allowing the tomatoes' natural sweetness to intensify and concentrate. The result is a silky sauce that clings to pasta without being watery or greasy.
