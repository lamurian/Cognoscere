## Summary

Battery drain in walking games is determined by GPS usage and screen-on time. Games with continuous GPS (Pikmin Bloom, Orna, Monster Hunter Now) drain 12-15% extra battery daily. Pedometer-only games (Walkr, The Walk, Wokamon) have near-zero battery impact — they use only the phone's built-in step counter. Audio-based games (Zombies Run, The Walk) save power by allowing screen-off operation during activity.

**Heavy drain (GPS + screen-heavy):** Pikmin Bloom (~157 MB APK) is Niantic's casual AR flower-planting game — closest to Pokemon Go in polish but known for battery issues (1.5-2%/hr background drain, overheating). Orna (~151-172 MB APK) uses pixel-art 2D graphics with deep RPG mechanics (class trees, turn-based combat, raids) and users report lower drain than Pokemon Go due to simpler rendering. Best for dedicated walking sessions with planned charging.

**Moderate drain (GPS + audio):** Zombies Run / ZRX (~220 MB iOS) is an audio-narrative fitness app where the screen stays off during activity — significantly less battery impact than visual AR games. Subscription $7.99/month. Geocaching uses GPS for navigation between hidden caches with optional screen-off intervals; premium sub for full access.

**Near-zero drain (pedometer-only):** Walkr (~80-100 MB) is a space exploration game powered entirely by step count — freemium with some ads. The Walk is a finite audio thriller (65 episodes, one-time purchase) unlocked by walking steps. Wokamon is a simple virtual pet fed by steps, free with ads. MistyWay is a newer fantasy RPG with hand-drawn biomes driven by step count, free and ad-free. All have negligible battery impact.

**Top picks by use case:** For battery efficiency above all, Walkr or The Walk. For deep RPG with moderate drain, Orna offers the best balance of depth and efficiency. For the closest Pokemon Go feel despite high drain, Pikmin Bloom. For runners, Zombies Run with its screen-off audio mode. App sizes range from ~80 MB (Walkr) to ~220 MB (Zombies Run). No app matches Pokemon Go's AR polish at lower battery use — the visual-fidelity-versus-power-consumption tradeoff is inherent to the category.
