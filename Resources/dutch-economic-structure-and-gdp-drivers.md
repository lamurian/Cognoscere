## Summary

The Netherlands achieves a high GDP (18th nominal, 10th per capita globally) through a highly diversified, services-heavy economy with exceptional trade openness. Services account for 80.2% of GDP, industry 17.9%, and agriculture 1.6% — yet the country is the world's second-largest agricultural exporter. Trade totals over 160% of GDP, reflecting deep integration with the European single market and global supply chains.

Rotterdam, Europe's largest port, contributes €45.6 billion (6.2% of Dutch GDP) through direct and indirect effects including re-exports via logistics and distribution [@erasmus-rotterdam-effect]. Amsterdam hosts European headquarters for IBM, Microsoft, Google, Oracle, Cisco, Uber, and Netflix, along with domestic giants Shell, Philips, Unilever, ING, Heineken, and Ahold Delhaize. The Netherlands scores 8th globally on the Global Innovation Index and 4th on the WEF Global Competitiveness Report.

## Key Points

- GDP per capita: $77,881 (nominal, 2026f), 10th globally
- Trade-to-GDP ratio exceeds 160% — one of the most open economies worldwide
- Rotterdam port accounts for 6.2% of Dutch added value (EUR study)
- The Netherlands is the 5th largest economy in the EU by nominal GDP
- 91% of Dutch enterprises invested in new goods/services, beating EU average of 82%
