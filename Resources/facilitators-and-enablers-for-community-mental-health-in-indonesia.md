## Summary

Despite significant barriers, several facilitators enable access to mental health services in Indonesia. The most critical is BPJS Kesehatan (national health insurance) membership. Participants in the Munira study expressed gratitude that BPJS covers mental health treatment costs, with one noting 'I feel lucky I have BPJS Kesehatan which covers mental health services.' However, many Indonesians remain unaware of BPJS's tiered referral system, and awareness campaigns are needed [@munira2023].

Social support from spouses, family, and close friends constitutes a second facilitator. Positive support gave participants a sense of safety, comfort, and motivation to undergo treatment. The expert consensus similarly emphasised family involvement as critical, with the PIS-PK programme embedding family-based assessment of mental health into primary care visits [@munira2023; @basrowi2024].

The emerging Community Caucus Caring for Mental Health (Kaukus Masyarakat Peduli Kesehatan Jiwa), established in November 2023, represents a civil society mobilisation for mental health advocacy. The Caucus conducted an expert discussion among 45 specialists to identify priorities and recommendations, proposing a Mental Health Care Movement as a community-based initiative to raise awareness and reduce stigma [@basrowi2024].

Technology presents opportunities for screening and diagnosis. The expert consensus recommended developing telemedicine and app-based innovations for mental health screening. Indonesia's SATUSEHAT platform already aggregates mental health data from healthcare facilities, and Google Trends data shows correspondence between search volumes and survey-measured mental health prevalence, suggesting digital surveillance is feasible [@basrowi2024].

Self-help emerged as an internal facilitator. Some participants continued seeking treatment despite stigma because personal motivation outweighed external barriers. This aligns with evidence that self-help strategies are effective for mild to moderate depression [@munira2023].

## Key Points
- BPJS Kesehatan membership is the most critical financial facilitator
- Family and social support provides motivation and safety for treatment-seeking
- Community Caucus (2023) represents emerging civil society advocacy
- Telemedicine and digital tools offer screening and access opportunities
- Self-help motivation can overcome stigma barriers
- Mental health first aid training (adapted from Australia) recommended

## Confidence: Moderate
Evidence from qualitative study (Munira et al. 2023) and expert consensus (Basrowi et al. 2024); technology recommendations are aspirational rather than evidence-based.
