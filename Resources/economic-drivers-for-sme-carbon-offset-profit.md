## Summary

Carbon offsets let SMEs profit by generating verified emission reductions through land-based practices [@hoover2021]. The voluntary carbon market transacted ~100 MMTCO2e in 2018, with forestry and land-use projects at 37% of volume. Compliance markets like California's cap-and-trade and RGGI create additional demand for agricultural and forestry offsets.

Carbon pricing shows a leverage effect: a 1% price increase drives 1.78% marginal emission reductions in covered sectors [@song2025]. This amplification means SME-generated credits in liquid markets benefit from strong price signals. Agriculture contributes 10% of US GHG emissions while forestry sequesters 12% — creating a large offset supply opportunity [@hoover2021].

Key SME incentives: (1) diversified income combining carbon credits with primary products, (2) operational cost cuts from sustainable practices (less fertilizer, less energy), and (3) premium market access through climate-smart certification. SMEs face barriers: high MRV costs, technical knowledge gaps, limited access to carbon registries [@hoover2021].
