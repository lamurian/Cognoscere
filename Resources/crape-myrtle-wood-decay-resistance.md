Crape myrtle wood (Lagerstroemia spp.) is rated as moderately durable to durable regarding decay resistance, with intermediate resistance to insect attack [@wooddatabase2010]. This natural durability comes from extractives in the heartwood that inhibit fungal colonization. The wood is suitable for outdoor applications including furniture, decking, and fencing.

For fence posts and ground-contact structures, crape myrtle offers significantly better longevity than willow without treatment. However, landscape specimens of common crape myrtle (L. indica) rarely produce lumber larger than 1 ft in diameter, limiting their use for structural posts. The larger Lagerstroemia species from Southeast Asia provide dimensional lumber. While crape myrtle's moderate durability is adequate for above-ground outdoor use, preservative treatment is still recommended for ground-contact applications to maximize service life.
