## Summary

At four years, the preschooler is socially engaged, physically coordinated, and highly verbal. Weight averages about 16.5 kg (36.4 lb) and height 103 cm (40.6 in). The four-year-old hops on one foot, draws a person with 3–4 body parts, speaks in complex sentences, plays cooperatively with a group, and negotiates peer conflicts with growing skill — representing the integration of all developmental domains [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~16.5 kg (range 14.0–19.3 kg); median height ~103 cm. Weight gain ~2 kg/year; height ~6–7 cm/year. Preschool lean period — BMI at lifetime low. Head circumference ~50 cm (adult size nearly reached). All primary teeth erupted; first permanent molars may begin appearing around this age.

**Emotional Maturity:** Enjoys doing new things. Plays "mom" or "dad" in pretend play. Is more and more creative with make-believe play. Would rather play with other children than alone. Cooperates with other children. Often cannot tell what is real and make-believe. Talks about what they like and are interested in. May show increasing independence (wants to do things without help). Shows empathy more consistently. Beginning to understand others' perspectives (theory of mind emerging, though still limited) [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

**Psychological Development:** Preoperational stage (Piaget). The child uses symbols and language extensively. Magical thinking common — believes wishes cause events. Egocentrism persists — difficulty taking another's visual or cognitive perspective. Counts to 10 or higher by rote. Understands the concept of counting (one-to-one correspondence for 3–4 objects). Names 4–6 colours. Draws a person with 3–4 body parts. Tells stories that mix reality and fantasy. Understands the idea of "same" and "different." Knows some letters and numbers [@misirliyan2023].

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: hops on one foot 2–3 times; stands on one foot for 3–5 seconds; catches a bounced ball most of the time; jumps over low objects; walks up and down stairs alternating feet without support; climbs playground equipment with agility; may begin to skip with practice. Fine motor: copies a cross and square; draws a person with 3–4 body parts; cuts along a line with child-safe scissors; strings beads; pours from a small pitcher; dresses and undresses with minimal help; uses fork and spoon; brushes teeth with help.

**Speech and Language Development:** Speech is 100% intelligible to strangers; uses sentences of 4–6 words; tells stories that stick to topic; answers simple "who," "what," "where," and "why" questions; uses past tense correctly; can say first and last name; understands prepositions and comparatives; may still have difficulty with l, r, s, sh, ch, th sounds; sings songs and recites rhymes from memory; talks about what happened during the day [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: cannot jump in place or hop; cannot stand on one foot; difficulty scribbling; speech unintelligible to strangers; does not use "me" and "you" correctly; cannot retell a favourite story; does not understand "same" and "different"; does not play cooperatively with other children; no pretend play; ignores other children; does not respond to people outside family; extreme aggression or withdrawal; flat affect; not interested in interactive games; cannot dress self with help; loss of previously acquired skills.
