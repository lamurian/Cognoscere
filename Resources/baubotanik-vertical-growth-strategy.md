## Summary

Baubotanik, developed by Ferdinand Ludwig at University of Stuttgart and TU Munich (2007-present), is the only systematic engineering approach for multi-story living structures. Its core method: temporary steel scaffolding supports floor plates while trees grow and inosculate (naturally graft). Over 10-15 years, living wood assumes structural load and scaffolding is removed [@mylo2023].

Key projects: Baubotanik Footbridge (2005) — 22m span, 2.5m high, 80 willow bundles. Baubotanik Tower (2009) — 9m, 3 levels, 400 white willows, steel scaffold. Plane Tree Cube Nagold (2012) — 10m cube, London plane trees across 6 levels [@mylo2023]. The Bird-Watching Station Waldkirchen (2007) — first 2-story Baubotanik, supports to 10m.

For 20m domes, Baubotanik would need a geodesic steel scaffold with trees at structural nodes, inosculating at force-distribution points. Species selection matters: willows for speed (1m/year), plane trees for rigidity. Timeline to self-supporting: estimated 15-30 years.
