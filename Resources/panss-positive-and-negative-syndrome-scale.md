## Summary

The Positive and Negative Syndrome Scale (PANSS) is a 30-item clinician-rated instrument designed to assess symptom severity in schizophrenia. It provides balanced representation of positive symptoms (7 items: delusions, conceptual disorganization, hallucinatory behavior, excitement, grandiosity, suspiciousness/persecution, and hostility), negative symptoms (7 items: blunted affect, emotional withdrawal, poor rapport, passive/apathetic social withdrawal, difficulty in abstract thinking, lack of spontaneity and flow of conversation, and stereotyped thinking), and general psychopathology symptoms (16 items: somatic concern, anxiety, guilt feelings, tension, mannerisms and posturing, depression, motor retardation, uncooperativeness, unusual thought content, disorientation, poor attention, lack of judgment and insight, disturbance of volition, poor impulse control, preoccupation, and active social avoidance). Each item is rated on a 7-point severity scale (1=absent, 7=extreme), yielding subscale and total scores.

The PANSS was originally developed and standardized by Kay, Fiszbein, and Opler (1987) based on data from 101 schizophrenic inpatients [@kay1987]. The original publication established inter-rater reliability of 0.80-0.87 across subscales and internal consistency (alpha) of 0.70-0.87. While factor-analytic studies consistently support a five-factor structure (positive, negative, cognitive, excitement, and depression/anxiety), the original three-subscale structure (positive, negative, general) remains the most clinically used scoring method.

The PANSS is specifically a symptom severity instrument rather than a diagnostic screener, so sensitivity and specificity for schizophrenia diagnosis are not typically reported. Instead, its primary clinical applications include assessing baseline symptom severity, monitoring treatment response, and evaluating treatment resistance. A reduction of >=30% in PANSS total score from baseline is commonly used as a threshold for treatment response in clinical trials. The PANSS has been translated into over 40 languages and is the most widely used schizophrenia symptom rating scale globally.

## Key Points

- 30 items: 7 positive, 7 negative, 16 general psychopathology; 1-7 severity scale
- Original standardization (Kay 1987): inter-rater reliability 0.80-0.87, alpha 0.70-0.87 [@kay1987]
- Five-factor structure supported: positive, negative, cognitive, excitement, depression/anxiety
- Severity measure, not a diagnostic screener; sensitivity/specificity not typically reported for diagnosis
- >=30% total score reduction from baseline commonly used as treatment response threshold
- Most widely used schizophrenia symptom rating scale globally (>40 language versions)
- Requires trained clinician rater; administration time 30-45 minutes
