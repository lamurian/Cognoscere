Some willow rods inevitably fail to root or die back, especially in the first few years. During the dormant-season inspection, identify dead or failing wands by checking for brittle, non-flexible stems with no buds [@rachelthegardener2017]. Replace these using vigorous shoots pruned from the same willow structure — no need to buy new material. Take a pruned wand from the butt end of the strongest growth, slide it through the lattice alongside the dead one, and push it into the ground as deep as possible [@rachelthegardener2017; @deesidewillow2024].

For established structures where removing a dead rod might damage the lattice, insert a new whip alongside rather than attempting extraction [@deesidewillow2024]. Water the replacement well, and it will typically root within weeks. Expect to replace 10-12 stems per year in the first few years, dropping to 1-2 after the fence is fully established [@rachelthegardener2017]. Regular replacement maintains the structural integrity and density of the living fence over its 30-50 year lifespan [@greayer2024; @penn2024].
