Effective integrated farming combines traditional ecological knowledge with modern biomimetic design. Indonesia's pekarangan (home gardens) and Subak (water temple) systems have sustained biodiversity and resource cycling for centuries through democratic councils and spiritual rituals [@GlobalEcovillageNetwork]. These models inform modern approaches like the 1m² urban homestead, which applies biomimicry principles to limited urban spaces, and the West Timor living fence systems that integrate cattle feeding with soil conservation [@ngongo2022]. The convergence of traditional and modern practices demonstrates that closed-loop, biodiverse farming is scalable from a single square meter to community landscapes, provided that design respects local ecology and cultural context.
