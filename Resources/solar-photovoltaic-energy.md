Solar photovoltaic (PV) energy converts sunlight directly into electricity via semiconducting materials exhibiting the photovoltaic effect. It is the fastest-growing renewable energy source, with global installed capacity reaching approximately 1,185 GW by end of 2022, supplying over 6% of global electricity. Costs have fallen 85% since 2010.

## PV Cell Technologies

**Crystalline Silicon (c-Si)**: Dominates 95% of the market. Monocrystalline silicon offers higher efficiency (up to 24.5% for commercial modules) while polycrystalline is cheaper but slightly less efficient. Modern cells use PERC (passivated emitter rear contact), TOPCon (tunnel oxide passivated contact), and IBC (interdigitated back contact) architectures to improve light capture.

**Thin-Film Technologies**: Cadmium telluride (CdTe) and CIGS (copper indium gallium selenide) are lighter and flexible, suitable for building-integrated PV. Single-junction thin-film GaAs cells have reached 29.1% efficiency, while multi-junction concentrator cells have hit 47.6% in lab conditions.

**Emerging Technologies**: Perovskite solar cells are a third-generation technology with rapidly improving lab efficiencies exceeding 26%. Tandem cells combining perovskite with silicon promise to break the single-junction efficiency limit.

## Efficiency and Performance

Commercial module efficiency ranges from 15% to 24.5%. Capacity factor averages 10-20% depending on geographic latitude and cloud cover. Temperature negatively affects performance -- most panels lose 0.3-0.5% efficiency per degree Celsius above 25C.

## Optimal Deployment

**Tracking Systems**: Single-axis and dual-axis solar trackers can increase energy yield by 25-35% compared to fixed-tilt systems, though with added mechanical complexity and maintenance costs.

**Bifacial Panels**: Capture light on both sides, boosting output by 5-30% depending on ground albedo. As of 2024, bifacial panels are the leading choice for utility-scale installations.

**Orientation**: In the northern hemisphere, south-facing at latitude tilt maximises annual output. East-west facing arrays capture more morning/evening power, better matching demand curves.

**Soiling and Degradation**: Dust accumulation can reduce output by up to 30% in arid regions. Panel degradation averages 0.8-1.0% per year for crystalline silicon. Cleaning mechanisms, including robotic and electrostatic systems, are being developed.

## LCOE and Grid Parity

As of 2025, utility-scale solar PV has reached grid parity in most sunny regions. Levelized cost of energy (LCOE) has fallen to $20-40/MWh in optimal locations, making it one of the cheapest electricity sources available. Solar PV costs are projected to continue declining following Swanson's Law (20% reduction per doubling of installed capacity).
