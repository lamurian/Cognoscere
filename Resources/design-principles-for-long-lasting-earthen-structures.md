Long-lasting earthen structures require five design principles working together. First, moisture management: raised stem walls (300-500 mm), wide eaves (600-900 mm), breathable lime renders, and proper site drainage protect against water damage [@hamard2016]. Second, conservative sizing: cob walls 450-600 mm thick provide adequate compressive capacity for two-storey buildings. Third, structural integration: timber ring beams at roof level distribute loads and tie walls, while internal timber posts or surface meshes provide seismic reinforcement where needed [@peri2021]. Fourth, passive design: south-facing orientation and appropriate glazing reduce thermal stress and moisture cycling within walls [@giada2019]. Fifth, regular maintenance: re-application of lime renders every 15-25 years and annual foundation inspections ensure longevity. Cob is not maintenance-free — it requires ongoing care and understanding of its behavior, just like any other building system.
