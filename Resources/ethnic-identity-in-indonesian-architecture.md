Each Indonesian sub-culture has its own distinct Rumah Adat based on local beliefs and values, yet all share common Austronesian ancestry [@anggakara2020]. Examples include boat-shaped Batak Toba houses with carved gables, Minangkabau Rumah Gadang with upsweeping gables reflecting matrilineal social structure, Torajan Tongkonan with massive saddle roofs, and Papuan Dani honai circular dome huts [@wikipediacontributors2025].

Banjarese Bubungan Tinggi represent royalty. Dayak longhouses can exceed 300m. Balinese homes are open-structure compounds within high walls reflecting Hindu cosmology. Javanese houses uniquely sit on the ground rather than stilts [@wikipediacontributors2025].
