## Human Rights Record: Soeharto vs Prabowo Indonesia

### Soeharto Era (1966-1998)

Soeharto's New Order was brutally authoritarian. Mass killings of 500,000 to 1 million suspected communists in 1965-66. Petrus (mysterious shootings) killed up to 10,000 suspected criminals in 1983-85. Tanjung Priok massacre (1984) killed up to 100 protesters. East Timor invasion (1975) and occupation caused 90,000-214,000 deaths. Santa Cruz massacre (1991) killed hundreds. Operation Jaring Merah in Aceh (1990-92) killed up to 12,000 civilians, mostly civilians. Political opponents were kidnapped and killed (1997-98 activist kidnappings involving Tim Mawar led by Prabowo). Press freedom was eliminated -- magazines banned (Tempo, 1994). Political parties were controlled through a state corporatist system. Dwifungsi gave military pervasive roles in civilian life. The hijab was banned in schools (1982-1991).

### Prabowo Era (2024-present)

Prabowo himself is an alleged human rights violator from his military career -- involvement in East Timor operations, activist kidnappings as commander of Kopassus/Kostrad, and dismissal from the military for 'misinterpreting orders'. As president, Prabowo has acknowledged state responsibility for past gross human rights violations (following Jokowi's precedent). The 2024 US State Department report notes: arbitrary killings, torture, and limited justice for past abuses. In August 2025, protests erupted over the killing of Affan Kurniawan, and Prabowo addressed the nation. Concerns exist about backsliding on democratic freedoms. The revision of the Armed Forces Act in 2025 expanded military roles in civilian positions, raising concerns about a return to Dwifungsi-style military involvement.

### Key Differences

- **Scale and severity**: Soeharto's human rights violations were massive in scale -- hundreds of thousands killed. Prabowo's era has not seen mass state killings of remotely similar magnitude.
- **Institutional context**: Soeharto operated under authoritarian rule with no democratic checks. Prabowo operates in a democratic (though flawed) system with civil society and some press freedom.
- **Press freedom**: Soeharto eliminated independent media entirely. Under Prabowo, media remains relatively free but concerns exist about pressure on critical outlets.
- **Accountability**: Soeharto never faced accountability. Prabowo faces ongoing scrutiny from media, NGOs, and international bodies.
- **Direct involvement**: Soeharto ordered massacres from the top. Prabowo is alleged to have personally led operations with human rights abuses.

### Similarities

- Both presidents have a military background and maintain close military ties.
- Both have been accused of using state apparatus to suppress political opponents.
- Both eras saw/see the military playing roles beyond conventional defense (economic involvement in Soeharto era; expanded roles under Prabowo's Armed Forces Act revision).
- Impunity for past human rights abuses remains a feature of both eras.
- Papua conflict -- where human rights abuses continue -- spans both eras.
