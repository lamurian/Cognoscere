Swiss architect Marcel Kalberer developed a living willow rod construction technique in 1988 that enables large architectural structures built by community volunteers with no special skills. Bundled living willow rods are planted directly into the ground, then bent, woven, and tied to form domes, towers, and pavilions. The Auerworldpalast (Germany, 1998) is a circular dome 20m in diameter and 5.5m tall, built by 300 volunteers [@kalberer2025]. Planting occurs in spring (Feb-Apr) or fall (Oct-Dec). The first two summers require watering, with periodic shaping. Structures are alive and strengthen over time as roots deepen and stems thicken.
