## Summary

Dietary Reference Intakes are established through a rigorous process involving systematic review of scientific evidence by expert committees of the Food and Nutrition Board at the National Academies. For macronutrients, methods include nitrogen balance studies (protein), glucose utilisation data (carbohydrate), and meta-analyses of chronic disease endpoint trials [@nationalacademiesofsciences2024]. The EAR is determined first, based on a functional criterion—for example, nitrogen equilibrium for protein, or maintenance of plasma nutrient levels for vitamins.

For micronutrients, multiple biomarkers are used. Vitamin C requirements are based on near-maximal neutrophil saturation and minimal urinary loss. Selenium requirements use plasma selenoprotein P saturation as the criterion [@gombart2020]. When biomarker data are insufficient, factorial methods estimate requirements by summing obligatory losses (e.g., through urine, faeces, sweat) and adding an absorption correction factor. For many nutrients, the RDA is calculated as the EAR plus two standard deviations of the requirement distribution, covering 97–98% of the healthy population. The European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) uses a similar but independently derived set of Dietary Reference Values (DRVs) for European populations.
