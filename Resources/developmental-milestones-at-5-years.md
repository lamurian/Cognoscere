## Summary

At five years, the child reaches a critical school-readiness milestone. Weight averages about 18.7 kg (41.2 lb) and height 110 cm (43.3 in). The five-year-old skips, draws a person with 6+ body parts, uses complex sentences, counts to 10 meaningfully, and plays cooperatively with rules — demonstrating the cognitive, social, and motor readiness expected for kindergarten entry [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~18.7 kg (range 15.8–22.2 kg); median height ~110 cm. Birth length approximately doubled. Growth velocity stable. First permanent molars may erupt ("6-year molars").

**Emotional Maturity:** Wants to please friends and be like them. More likely to agree with rules. Likes to sing, dance, and act. Shows awareness of gender. Can tell what is real and what make-believe. Shows more independence — may visit next-door neighbour alone. Understands and follows rules in games (though may still cheat to win). Aware of others' feelings and can respond appropriately. Self-regulation significantly improved — can wait, share, and take turns. Developing sense of right and wrong (conscience) [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

**Psychological Development:** Preoperational to concrete operational transition beginning (Piaget). The child can classify objects by multiple attributes (colour AND shape). Counts to 10 with one-to-one correspondence. Names 10+ colours. Draws a person with 6+ body parts. Prints some letters and numbers. Knows about things used every day (money, food, appliances). Understands simple time concepts (morning, afternoon, night, days of week). Attention span 15–20 minutes for favoured activities. Recognises own name in print; may write it [@misirliyan2023].

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: skips with alternating feet; stands on one foot for 10+ seconds; somersaults; hops 5–6 metres on one foot; catches a small ball with hands only (no chest); walks up and down stairs alternating feet confidently; may learn to ride bicycle with training wheels, swim, or skate. Fine motor: copies triangle and diamond; draws a person with 6+ body parts (head, body, arms, legs, facial features); colours within lines; prints some letters and numbers (especially own name); uses scissors to cut out simple shapes; dresses and undresses independently except for difficult fasteners; uses fork, spoon, and table knife [@misirliyan2023].

**Speech and Language Development:** Speech fully intelligible with minor articulation errors (l, r, s, v, z, ch, sh, th may still be developing); sentences of 5–8 words; uses adult-like grammar including future tense, comparatives, and conditionals; tells stories with plot and characters; defines simple words; understands opposites; uses rhyming words; knows address and phone number (some children); vocabulary exceeds 2000 words; follows three-step commands; understands humour and tells simple jokes [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: cannot skip or hop; cannot stand on one foot for 5 seconds; cannot catch a ball; speech unintelligible to unfamiliar listeners; does not speak in full sentences; cannot tell a simple story; cannot distinguish real from make-believe; does not play cooperatively or take turns; cannot follow rules in simple games; cannot draw a recognisable person; cannot name colours or count to 5; does not respond to people; extreme behaviour problems (aggression, withdrawal); cannot dress self; loss of previously acquired skills. This is the key pre-school surveillance visit recommended by AAP.
