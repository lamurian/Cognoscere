## Summary

The Netherlands functions as the world's largest conduit offshore financial center (OFC). A 2017 Nature Research study identified it as the top global conduit for corporate tax avoidance, routing almost half of global flows to 24 "sink" OFCs. The primary mechanism is the "Dutch Sandwich" BEPS (Base Erosion and Profit Shifting) tool, often combined with Ireland's "Double Irish" arrangement. This structure exploits Dutch tax law allowing royalty payments to non-EU tax havens (like Bermuda) without incurring withholding taxes.

As of 2016, multinationals moved approximately €22 billion in royalties and interest through the Netherlands annually for tax avoidance. The tool was created after lobbying from US tax lawyers in 2003-2006, attributed to then-State Secretary Joop Wijn. Google used it to shield €16 billion from tax in 2016 alone; Apple similarly avoided US taxes on roughly $110 billion in overseas profits. The Netherlands is ranked 7th largest tax haven globally by the Tax Justice Network. This modern role can be understood as a continuation of the country's historical intermediary function: just as the VOC served as a trading intermediary between Europe and Asia, the Netherlands today serves as a fiscal intermediary between high-tax countries and tax havens.

## Key Points

- World's largest conduit OFC (Nature Research, 2017)
- €22 billion/yr routed through Netherlands for tax avoidance
- 7th largest tax haven globally (Tax Justice Network)
- "Dutch Sandwich" often combined with "Double Irish" — most used by US tech & pharma
- Under pressure: withholding tax reforms introduced from 2021; Double Irish closed to new users in 2015
