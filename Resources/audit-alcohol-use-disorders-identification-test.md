## Summary

The Alcohol Use Disorders Identification Test (AUDIT) is a 10-item screening instrument developed by the World Health Organization (WHO) for identifying hazardous and harmful alcohol consumption as well as possible alcohol dependence. The AUDIT covers three domains: hazardous alcohol use (items 1-3: frequency, quantity, and frequency of heavy drinking), dependence symptoms (items 4-6: impaired control, increased salience, morning drinking), and harmful alcohol use (items 7-10: guilt after drinking, blackouts, alcohol-related injuries, and others' concern). Each item is rated 0-4, producing a total score range of 0-40.

The AUDIT was developed through a WHO multi-national collaborative project across six countries (Australia, Bulgaria, Kenya, Mexico, Norway, and the USA) conducted by Saunders, Aasland, Babor, de la Fuente, and Grant (1993). The original validation study demonstrated strong psychometric properties, with a cutoff score of >=8 providing the optimal balance between sensitivity and specificity for detecting hazardous and harmful alcohol use [@saunders1993].

At the standard cutoff >=8, the AUDIT demonstrates sensitivity ranging from 0.82 to 0.95 and specificity from 0.78 to 0.95 across diverse populations and settings. The AUDIT-C (first 3 items only) is a shorter alternative focused on consumption with comparable sensitivity but typically lower specificity. The AUDIT has been validated in primary care, emergency departments, general populations, and across numerous cultural contexts. Internal consistency is high (alpha 0.80-0.94), and test-retest reliability typically exceeds 0.80.

## Key Points

- 10 items, 0-4 scale, total 0-40, three domains: hazardous use, dependence, harmful use
- WHO multi-national development across 6 countries [@saunders1993]
- Cutoff >=8: Se 0.82-0.95, Sp 0.78-0.95 for hazardous/harmful alcohol use
- AUDIT-C (3-item consumption version) available for brief screening
- High internal consistency (alpha 0.80-0.94) and retest reliability (>0.80)
- Validated across numerous cultures and clinical settings
- Identifies the full spectrum of alcohol problems from hazardous use to dependence
