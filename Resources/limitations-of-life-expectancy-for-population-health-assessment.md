## Summary

Life expectancy at birth is the most commonly used single metric for comparing population health across countries. However, it captures only the mortality dimension and ignores morbidity, disability, functional limitations, and mental health. A population with high life expectancy may still have high levels of chronic disease and disability, creating a misleading picture of overall health.

The WHO defines health as a state of complete physical, mental, and social wellbeing, not merely the absence of disease [@tolonen2021]. Life expectancy operationalises only the mortality aspect of this definition. The England Health Index showed that some areas with high life expectancy scored poorly on mental health indicators, demonstrating how single metrics can conceal important health deficits [@frenisterrantino2022].
