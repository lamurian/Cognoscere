Built examples prove ecological integration in tall buildings works. Bosco Verticale in Milan (2014) hosts 800 trees, 5,000 shrubs, and 15,000 plants across two towers, creating a 30,000 m2 woodland on a 3,000 m2 footprint. After completion, it established habitat for over 1,600 bird and butterfly species [@alkodmany2023]. Oasia Hotel Downtown in Singapore achieves a 1,100% green plot ratio using a red aluminum mesh exoskeleton covered in 21 species of creepers, providing shade, absorbing heat, and purifying air [@alkodmany2023]. The ACROS Fukuoka Prefectural International Hall in Japan integrates 15 stepped vegetated terraces, finding a 15°C difference between exposed and plant-covered surface temperatures [@alkodmany2023]. These case studies demonstrate that ecological integration is not theoretical — it has been proven at scale across diverse climates.
