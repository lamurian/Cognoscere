## Summary

The average PSS-10 score in the general population without disorder or acute stressful situation ranges from approximately **12 to 14 points** (out of 40), depending on the country, year, and demographic composition of the sample. Scores vary meaningfully by age, sex, and socioeconomic status. The scale's scoring bands define 0-13 as low stress, 14-26 as moderate stress, and 27-40 as high perceived stress.

## Major Normative Studies

**US — Cohen & Williamson (1988), original norms from 2,387 respondents**
Overall mean approximately **13.1** (computed from sex-stratified data). By sex: males 12.1 (SD 5.9), females 13.7 (SD 6.6). By age: 18-29 years 14.2 (6.2), 30-44 years 13.0 (6.2), 45-54 years 12.6 (6.1), 55-64 years 11.9 (6.9), 65+ years 12.0 (6.3) [@cohen1988].

**US — Cohen & Janicki-Deverts (2012), probability samples from 2006 and 2009**
2006 sample (n=1,669): mean **12.73** (SD 7.34). 2009 sample (n=1,380): mean **15.21** (SD 7.28) — a significant increase of 2.48 points over three years, attributed to the Great Recession [@cohen2012].

**Germany — Klein et al. (2016), representative sample of 2,463 participants**
Overall mean **12.57** (SD 6.42). By age: 14-19 years 14.05 (6.54), 20-39 years 12.74 (6.67), 40-59 years 12.82 (6.42), 60+ years 11.94 (6.14). Women scored higher than men across all age groups [@klein2016].

**Sweden — Nordin & Nordin (2013)**
Overall mean approximately **13.0** (SD 6.6), based on a representative Swedish sample [@nordin2013].

## Demographic Patterns

The normative data consistently show three patterns. First, women report higher perceived stress than men by about 1.0-1.6 points. Second, perceived stress decreases with age: younger adults (18-34) score around 14-17, while older adults (60+) score around 11-12. Third, lower education, unemployment, and lower income are associated with higher PSS-10 scores.

## Clinical Interpretation

A PSS-10 score of 27 or higher is considered clinically elevated stress. The normative mean of 12-14 falls well within the low-stress range (0-13) or lower end of the moderate range (14-26), consistent with a general population sample not selected for any disorder or acute stressor.
