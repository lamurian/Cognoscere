The UK Office for National Statistics created an annual England Health Index (2015-2018) as a composite indicator to monitor population health across 149 Upper Tier Local Authorities. It is structured around three domains: Healthy People (mortality, physical health, mental health, wellbeing), Healthy Lives (risk factors, unemployment, education), and Healthy Places (environment, housing, services, crime). These contain 17 subdomains and 58 indicators [@frenisterrantino2022].

The index uses time-series factor analysis for indicator weights, z-score normalization anchored to the 2015 baseline year (mean=100, SD=10), and arithmetic aggregation. Winsorization replaced heavy mathematical transformations in later versions to preserve interpretation. The index revealed that London boroughs score high on Healthy People but low on Healthy Places, a trade-off invisible in single metrics. The index now covers 307 Lower Tier Local Authorities and has been assessed for statistical coherence through sensitivity analysis [@frenisterrantino2022].
