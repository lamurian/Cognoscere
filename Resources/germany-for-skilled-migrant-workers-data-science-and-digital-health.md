## Summary

Germany recruits skilled non-EU workers through the EU Blue Card and the Opportunity Card (Chancenkarte). Data scientist and healthcare IT roles are eligible. Key hubs are Berlin (health tech startups), Munich (SAP, Siemens Healthineers, BMW), and Frankfurt. The healthcare IT sector is expanding rapidly due to the electronic patient record (ePA) rollout and Telematics Infrastructure (TI) requirements [@pandey2026]. Regular openings: data scientist, healthcare IT consultant, FHIR implementation specialist, clinical data analyst, and medical informatics researcher.

## Key Details
Free public education from primary through university (minimal semester fees). Mandatory statutory health insurance (GKV) with employer sharing ~50% of the ~14.6%+ premium. Family insurance covers spouse and children at no extra cost. PhD data scientist salary: EUR 65,000 - EUR 110,000 (median ~EUR 75,000, senior roles EUR 90,000-110,000) [@yosifova2025; @codelabsacademy2025]. Living cost: EUR 1,800 - 2,600/month including rent. Numbeo estimates EUR 1,001/month excluding rent; city-centre one-bedroom rent averages EUR 806. Munich is most expensive; Berlin moderate; Leipzig very affordable [@numbeo2026a; @numbeo2026e].
