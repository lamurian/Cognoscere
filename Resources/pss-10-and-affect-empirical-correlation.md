## Summary

The correlation between PSS-10 (perceived stress) and positive affect is consistently negative but small in magnitude, while the correlation with negative affect is moderate and positive. The reported Pearson r values for PSS-10 with positive affect range from -0.18 to -0.30.

Acoba @acoba2024 reported r = -0.176 (p < 0.01) between PSS-10 and PANAS positive affect in a Filipino adult sample (n = 426). Yang et al. @yang2020 found r = -0.204 (p < 0.05) between PSS-14 and PANAS positive affect in a Chinese substance use disorder sample (n = 415).

These consistent small-to-moderate negative correlations (r ≈ -0.15 to -0.30) indicate that perceived stress shares limited variance with positive affect (2-9%), confirming they are clearly distinct constructs.

## Key Points
- PSS-10 and positive affect: r ≈ -0.18 to -0.30 (small to moderate negative)
- The asserted bound |r| < 0.6 covers the empirical range comfortably, but is unnecessarily wide
- The ad-hoc bound |r| < 0.5 is more empirically defensible
- Negative affect typically shows stronger correlations with PSS-10 (r ≈ 0.30-0.50)
