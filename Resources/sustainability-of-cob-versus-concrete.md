Cob consumes approximately 1% of the energy required for concrete production and is perfectly aligned with circular economy principles: it is infinitely recyclable, contains zero VOCs, and harmlessly disintegrates at end-of-life [@morel2021]. Cement production alone accounts for 8% of global CO2 emissions, while cob's emissions are near-zero beyond transport and labor. Cob walls provide excellent passive hygrothermal regulation with thermal conductivity of 0.25-0.95 W/mK (depending on density and fiber content) and high thermal effusivity that buffers temperature swings [@giada2019]. Despite exponential growth in academic research on earthen materials, adoption remains niche due to non-technical barriers — lack of building codes, insurance difficulties, shortage of skilled labor, and negative perception — which practitioners rate as equally important as technical challenges [@morel2021].
