## Summary

At thirteen months, the toddler walks with increasing confidence — or is on the verge of walking independently. Weight averages about 9.8 kg (21.6 lb) and length 76.5 cm (30.1 in). The toddler combines gestures with vocalisations to make their intentions unmistakably clear [@centersfordiseasecontrolandprevention2026].

## Key Points

**Body Weight and Height:** Median weight ~9.8 kg (range 8.5–11.5 kg); median length ~76.5 cm. Weight gain ~40–60 g/week. Head circumference ~46.5 cm. Anterior fontanelle may still be palpable but closing.

**Emotional Maturity:** Shows a range of emotions — affection, frustration, jealousy, joy. May have tantrum-like reactions when frustrated. Seeks comfort from caregiver when upset. Shows preferences for certain people and toys. Beginning of assertive independence — wants to do things "myself."

**Psychological Development:** Piaget substage 4 continues. The toddler experiments with objects systematically — fills and empties containers, stacks rings. Understands object permanence consistently. Imitates actions after observing them. Points to body parts when named. Follows simple one-step commands with gesture cues.

**Motoric Development:** Gross motor: walks independently (may still be unsteady); stoops to pick up objects from floor; crawls up stairs; may walk backwards a few steps. Fine motor: precise pincer grasp; puts small objects into narrow-necked containers; scribbles spontaneously with crayon (if given); tries to use spoon independently; drinks from cup with some spilling.

**Speech and Language Development:** Uses 2–4 words meaningfully; understands many more words than can say; follows a one-step command with gesture ("give me the ball" while hand out); points to one body part when named; jabbers with conversational intonation; uses gestures and vocalising together to communicate [@nationalinstituteondeafnessandothercommunicationdisorders2022].

**Signs of Growth Retardation:** Red flags: not walking independently or cruising by 13 months; not using pincer grasp; not pointing at objects; does not say any words (including mama/dada); does not respond to name; does not look where you point; does not imitate actions or sounds; not seeking caregiver for comfort; does not show interest in other children.
